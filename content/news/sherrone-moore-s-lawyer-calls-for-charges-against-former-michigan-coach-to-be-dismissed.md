<p>Fired <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/ncaa/michigan-wolverines" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Michigan Wolverines</a> head football coach Sherrone Moore was in court Thursday, where his lawyer said he is innocent and seeking to have home invasion and other charges dismissed.</p><p>Moore’s attorney, Ellen Michaels, filed a motion to dismiss the arrest warrant against her client.</p><p>"This warrant was issued based on false and misleading statements presented as facts," Michaels said in an <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/midwest/michigan" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Ann Arbor</a> courtroom. "We’re confident the truth will come out in court under oath where it belongs."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE FOR MORE SPORTS COVERAGE ON </strong></a><a href="http://FOXNEWS.COM"><strong>FOXNEWS.COM</strong></a></p><p>This was Moore’s second court appearance since his arrest after authorities said he entered a woman’s apartment Dec. 10, blaming her for his firing at the University of Michigan, and proceeded to grab butter knives and kitchen scissors as he threatened to take his own life.</p><p>Moore is facing three charges, including felony home invasion and stalking. He also faces two misdemeanor charges of stalking and breaking and entering without the owner’s permission.</p><p>Moore’s wife, Kelli, arrived at the courthouse alongside her husband Thursday morning for the status hearing.
