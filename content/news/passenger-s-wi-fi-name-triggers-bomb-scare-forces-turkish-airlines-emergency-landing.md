<p>A Turkish Airlines flight was forced to make an emergency landing in Barcelona Thursday morning after a passenger created a hoax threat by setting up a Wi-Fi hotspot suggesting there was a "bomb threat" on board, according to airline officials.  </p><p>The emergency protocol was alerted when Flight 1853 was already approaching its intended designation at Barcelona-El Prat Airport from Istanbul. <a href="https://www.euronews.com/2026/01/15/turkish-airlines-flight-makes-emergency-landing-in-barcelona-after-in-flight-alert"><u>Euronews</u></a> said the Airbus A321, which was carrying 148 passengers and seven crew members, was directed to a designated area for inspection upon landing. </p><p>"​​It was detected that a passenger had created an in-flight internet access point and named the network in a way that included a bomb threat," Yahya Üstün, senior vice president of communications at <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/world-regions/turkey" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Turkish Airlines</a>, said in a post on X.</p><p><a href="https://simpleflying.com/wifi-hotspot-bomb-threat-turkish-airlines-emergency-landing/"><u>Simpleflying</u></a> reported that the flight was cruising over the Mediterranean when a flight crew member noticed an alarming Wi-Fi name that reportedly said, "I have a bomb, everyone will die."  </p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/all-8-tires-burst-harrowing-atlanta-landing-failure-involving-passenger-jet" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>PASSENGERS BAFFLED AND CONFUSED AFTER SCREAMS BURST FROM BENEATH TAXIING AIR CANADA PLANE</u></strong></a></p><p>Necessary safety procedures were immediately initiated following the alert, Üstün said. </p><p>The aircraft was then escorted by two fighter jets, <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/world-regions/spain" target="_blank" rel="noopener">one Spanish</a> and one French, during the emergency protocol, Euronews reported.  </p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/cause-failed-alaska-airlines-landing-gear-sent-passengers-screaming-revealed" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>CAUSE OF FAILED ALASKA AIRLINES LANDING GEAR THAT SENT PASSENGERS SCREAMING REVEALED</u></strong></a></p><p>Following the aircraft’s safe landing, response crews inspected the plane and worked to identify the passenger who created the threatening Wi-Fi hotspot name, <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/travel/general/airlines" target="_blank" rel="noopener">airline officials</a> said. The operation involved Spanish authorities such as personnel from the Civil Guard, the National Police and Catalonia’s regional police and fire services, according to Euronews.</p><p>According to footage from the scene, a dog was deployed to examine the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/travel" target="_blank" rel="noopener">passenger's luggage</a> on the tarmac. </p><p>However, "no irregularities were found" following a thorough inspection, according to officials.  </p><p>"Our aircraft's return flight will be carried out after the completion of passenger boarding," Üstün said. </p><p>Operations at Barcelona-El Prat Airport resumed normally following the explosive scare, Reuters reported.</p><p><i>Reuters contributed to this report.</i></p>
