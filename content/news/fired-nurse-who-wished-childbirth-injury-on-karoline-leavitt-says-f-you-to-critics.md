F--- you," said Lexie Lawler, a former labor and delivery nurse at Baptist Health Boca Raton Regional Hospital. </p><p>Lawler was fired from Baptist Health Boca Raton Regional Hospital after posting a video where she wished Leavitt permanent harm during childbirth, saying, "As a labor and delivery nurse, it gives me great joy to wish Karoline Leavitt a fourth degree tear."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media/leavitt-accuses-democrats-lying-from-very-beginning-about-minneapolis-ice-shooting" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>LEAVITT ACCUSES DEMOCRATS OF 'LYING FROM THE VERY BEGINNING' ABOUT MINNEAPOLIS ICE SHOOTING</strong></a></p><p>She added, "I hope you f------ rip from bow to stern and never s--- normally again, you c---," she said.  </p><p>The hospital told Fox News Digital that her comments "do not reflect our values or the standards we expect of healthcare professionals."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/white-house-baby-boom-full-bloom-usha-vance-top-trump-aides-announce-pregnancies" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>WHITE HOUSE BABY BOOM IN FULL BLOOM AS USHA VANCE, TOP TRUMP AIDES ANNOUNCE PREGNANCIES</u></strong></a></p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/southeast/florida" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Florida</a> Attorney General James Uthmeier is calling for the Florida Board of Nursing to revoke Lawler’s license. </p><p>Uthmeier told Fox News Digital in a statement that, "Women shouldn’t have to worry about a politically-driven nurse who wishes them pain and suffering being in the delivery room during childbirth." </p><p>He added, "It’s evil.
