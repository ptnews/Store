<p>A <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/southwest/texas" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Texas woman</a> was charged in connection with a fire that tore through an apartment complex after she allegedly burned her boyfriend’s clothing in a barbecue pit on Monday morning.</p><p>The suspect in the fire at Southlake Villas apartments in Harris County was identified as Crystal D. Randall, the Harris County Fire Marshal confirmed to Fox News Digital on Tuesday.</p><p>Investigators said the fire was sparked following a domestic disturbance that led a woman to burn her boyfriend’s clothing in a barbecue pit on an apartment patio, <a href="https://www.fox26houston.com/news/harris-county-fire-woman-charged-after-burning-boyfriends-clothes-bbq-pit-causes-large-apartment-fire" target="_blank" rel="noopener">FOX26 Houston</a> reported.</p><p>Fire marshals said the suspect tried to extinguish the fire, though it later rekindled and spread to the building.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/chicago-woman-set-fire-during-argument-cta-train-police-say" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CHICAGO WOMAN SET ON FIRE DURING ARGUMENT ON CTA TRAIN, POLICE SAY</strong></a></p><p>The <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/fires" target="_blank" rel="noopener">flames</a> "heavily and irreparably damaged" 13 apartment units, the Sheldon Fire Department said.</p><p>"It spread way over here," witness Mario Thompson told <a href="https://abc13.com/post/woman-charged-starting-13-unit-apartment-fire-ne-harris-county-burning-boyfriends-clothes-officials-say/18358610/" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">KTRK-TV</a>. "The roof had caved in. It was like stuff falling off the back. I'm like, 'Dang.'"</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/swiss-nightclub-promoted-flaming-bottle-stunts-deadly-inferno-packed-new-years-eve-venue" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>SWISS NIGHTCLUB PROMOTED FLAMING BOTTLE STUNTS BEFORE DEADLY INFERNO IN PACKED NEW YEAR'S EVE VENUE</strong></a></p><p>Officials said all residents got out of the building safely and <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/health" target="_blank" rel="noopener">no injuries</a> were reported.</p><p>An ember rekindled a hotspot that forced firefighters to return to the scene.
