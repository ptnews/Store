Thursday reportedly asked Justice Department lawyers to point to what authority allows the president to engage in a construction project at the White House.</p><p>"Where do you see the authority for the president to tear down the East Wing and build something in its place?" the judge asked, according to <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2026/01/22/trump-ballroom-approval-lawsuit/" the judge promised that he would issue a decision in February.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/white-house-responds-reports-trump-named-new-ballroom-after-himself" target="_blank"
