<p>Pop star Chappell Roan walked back a brief tribute to <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/entertainment/events/departed" target="_blank" rel="noopener">French screen icon Brigitte Bardot</a>, who died Sunday, after fans criticized the late actress’ history of controversial views.</p><p>"Holy s--- I did not know all that insane s--- Ms. Bardot stood for [obviously] I do not condone this. Very disappointing to learn," the "Pink Pony Club" singer, 27, wrote in her Instagram Story on Monday.</p><p>Roan had previously paid a brief tribute to Bardot by pointing out that she had been the inspiration for her 2023 hit "Red Wine Supernova," and tacking on a message for her to "rest in peace."</p><p>Fan backlash preceded her retraction and she deleted the post, however.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/entertainment/brigitte-bardot-hollywood-icon-animal-rights-activist-dead-91" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>BRIGITTE BARDOT, HOLLYWOOD ICON AND ANIMAL RIGHTS ACTIVIST, DEAD AT 91</strong></a></p><p>"Chappell Roan honors the openly racist and homophobic, Brigitte Bardot, on her Instagram story," <a href="https://x.com/MrPopOfficial/status/2005627760207855882" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">one post read</a>.</p><p>Another urged Roan to "open Brigitte Bardot's Wikipedia page" so she could read about the actress' views herself.</p><p>Others defended Roan, arguing that critics are holding her to an unattainable standard and that she couldn't possibly know everything about Bardot.</p><p>"And yet you all streamed red wine supernova and didn’t gaf when her name was mentioned in the first line.
