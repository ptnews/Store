<p>The Small Business Administration (SBA) announced Thursday that it suspended 6,900 <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/midwest/minnesota" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Minnesota borrowers</a> after uncovering what it says is widespread suspected fraud.</p><p>SBA Administrator Kelly Loeffler said the agency reviewed thousands of Paycheck Protection Program (PPP) and Economic Injury Disaster Loan (EIDL) loans approved in Minnesota, and identified nearly $400 million in potentially fraudulent loans tied to borrowers in Minnesota.</p><p>"These individuals will be banned from all SBA loan programs, including disaster loans, going forward," Loeffler wrote on X.</p><p>Loeffler said the agency will refer appropriate cases to <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/police-and-law-enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">federal law enforcement</a> for prosecution and repayment.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-targets-minnesota-fraud-allegations-says-were-going-get-bottom-it" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TRUMP TARGETS MINNESOTA FRAUD ALLEGATIONS, SAYS ‘WE’RE GOING TO GET TO THE BOTTOM OF IT’</strong></a></p><p>"After years, the American people will finally begin to see the criminals who stole from law-abiding taxpayers held accountable — and this is just the first state," she stated.</p><p>The suspected fraudulent activity included 7,900 PPP and EIDL loans approved during the COVID-19 pandemic, Loeffler said.</p><p>The announcement comes as Minnesota Gov. <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/tim-walz" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Tim Walz</a> and his administration face scrutiny over billions of dollars in social services fraud.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media/minn-fraud-committee-chair-says-walz-turned-blind-eye-fraud-warnings-years" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>MINNESOTA FRAUD COMMITTEE CHAIR CLAIMS WALZ 'TURNED A BLIND EYE' TO FRAUD WARNINGS FOR YEARS</strong></a></p><p>Loeffler sent a letter Tuesday to Walz on Dec. 23, telling him that her agency will "halt" more than $5.5 million in annual support to resource partners in the state "until further notice."</p><p>"I am notifying you that effective immediately and until further notice, the SBA is halting the disbursement of federal funds to SBA resource partners operating in the state of Minnesota, totaling over $5.5 million in annual support," Loeffler wrote.</p><p>The SBA said that at least $2.5 million in PPP and EIDL funds issued during the pandemic era were connected to a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/opinion/loeffler-vast-network-somali-nonprofits-ripped-off-minnesotas-welfare-state" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Somali fraud scheme based</a> in Minneapolis.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/hhs-cuts-off-minnesota-child-care-payments-over-alleged-daycare-fraud-scheme" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>HHS CUTS OFF MINNESOTA CHILD CARE PAYMENTS OVER ALLEGED DAYCARE FRAUD SCHEME</strong></a></p><p>Loeffler told Walz that $430 million in PPP funds tied to roughly 13,000 loans were flagged as potentially fraudulent but were still funded anyway, including some that were forgiven during the Biden administration.</p><p>"The volume and concentration of <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/corruption" target="_blank" rel="noopener">potential fraud</a> is staggering, matched in its egregiousness only by your response to those who attempted to stop it," she wrote.</p><p>Fox News Digital has reached out to Walz’ office for comment.</p><p><i>Fox News Digital's Charles Creitz contributed to this report.</i></p>
