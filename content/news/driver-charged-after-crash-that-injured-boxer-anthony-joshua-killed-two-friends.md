<p>The driver in a crash that injured boxer <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/boxing" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Anthony Joshua</a> and killed two of his friends was charged with dangerous and reckless driving, police spokesperson Oluseyi Babaseyi said in a statement Friday.</p><p>The vehicle carrying <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports/boxing-star-anthony-joshuas-friends-identified-victims-deadly-car-crash" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Joshua, Sina Ghami and Latif "Latz" Ayodele</a> hit a stationary truck on the Lagos-Ibadan Expressway, which links Ogun State to Lagos.</p><p>The defendant was charged with four counts, including dangerous driving causing death, reckless and negligent driving, driving without due care and driving without a valid national driver’s license.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE FOR MORE SPORTS COVERAGE ON FOXNEWS.COM</strong></a><i><strong> </strong></i></p><p>Joshua, a three-time heavyweight champion, was released from a hospital Wednesday after sustaining minor <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports/boxing-star-anthony-joshua-injured-fatal-car-crash-nigeria" target="_blank" rel="noopener">injuries in the crash,</a> which raised questions about road safety in Nigeria. The boxer was discharged after being deemed clinically fit to recover at home, authorities said.</p><p>Nigerian President Bola Tinubu wrote on X that he had sent his condolences to Joshua for the loss of his friends.</p><p>"I have spoken with AJ to personally convey my condolences over the passing of his two close associates, Kevin Latif Ayodele and Sina Ghami in the recent accident," he wrote in a post.
