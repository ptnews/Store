<p>The New York State Department of Health reported a record surge in influenza activity, with 71,123 positive <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/health/respiratory-health/cold-and-flu" target="_blank" rel="noopener">flu cases</a> recorded statewide during the week ending December 20.</p><p>Health officials said the figure represents the highest number of flu cases ever reported in a single week since influenza became a reportable disease in New York in 2004.</p><p>State health data show the weekly total reflects a 38% increase from the previous reporting period, signaling a rapidly <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/health/doctors-reveal-troubling-signs-early-flu-trends-warn-unusually-bad-season" target="_blank" rel="noopener">intensifying flu season.</a></p><p>There have been 189,312 reported positive flu cases so far this season, while influenza-related hospitalizations rose 63% in the most recent week.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/health/flu-state-where-seasons-highly-contagious-variant-spreading-most" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>FLU BY STATE: WHERE THIS SEASON’S HIGHLY CONTAGIOUS VARIANT IS SPREADING THE MOST</strong></a></p><p>"We are seeing the highest number of flu cases ever recorded in a single week in New York state," Health Commissioner Dr. James McDonald said in a press release.</p><p>Earlier this month, the department declared influenza prevalent statewide, a designation that requires unvaccinated health care workers to wear masks in patient care settings.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/health/surge-whooping-cough-cases-southern-state-prompts-health-alerts" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>SURGE IN WHOOPING COUGH CASES IN SOUTHERN STATE PROMPTS HEALTH ALERTS</strong></a></p><p>Health officials continue to emphasize that <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/health/aggressive-new-flu-variant-sweeps-globe-doctors-warn-severe-symptoms" target="_blank" rel="noopener">vaccination remains the most effective</a> way to prevent severe illness and hospitalization from influenza.</p><p><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/health" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>CLICK HERE FOR MORE HEALTH STORIES</u></strong></a></p><p>New Yorkers who have not yet received a seasonal flu shot are still encouraged to do so, with experts saying vaccination can offer protection even later in the season.</p><p><a spread, the department advises individuals experiencing flu-like symptoms — including fever, cough, sore throat, or body aches — to stay home.
