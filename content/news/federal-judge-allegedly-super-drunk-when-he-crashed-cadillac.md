<p>A <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/federal-judges" target="_blank" rel="noopener">federal judge in Michigan</a> was "super drunk" when he allegedly crashed his car near his vacation home last year, according to court documents obtained by The Detroit News.</p><p>Judge Thomas Ludington, 72, crashed the Cadillac into two traffic signs near Petoskey in <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/midwest/michigan" target="_blank" rel="noopener">northern Michigan</a> on October 3. The hits wrecked the vehicle and deployed at least one of the airbags, according to the report.</p><p>Michigan State Police arrested Ludington at the scene, and was later released upon posting a $500 bond.</p><p>The judge faces charges of operating a motor vehicle while intoxicated and operating with a high blood-alcohol content. </p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/illegal-immigrant-accused-deaths-college-soccer-player-girlfriend-had-prior-dwi-dismissed-records" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>ILLEGAL IMMIGRANT ACCUSED IN DEATHS OF COLLEGE SOCCER PLAYER, GIRLFRIEND HAD PRIOR DWI DISMISSED: RECORDS</strong></a></p><p>Under state law, the former charge applies when a driver has a blood alcohol content of 0.08, while the latter charge applies when the blood alcohol content is over 0.17 under what is known as Michigan's "super drunk" laws.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/illegal-immigrant-allegedly-kills-county-board-member-wife-fatal-dui-crash-dhs" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>ILLEGAL IMMIGRANT ALLEGEDLY KILLS COUNTY BOARD MEMBER AND WIFE IN FATAL DUI CRASH: DHS</u></strong></a></p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/environment/regulation" target="_blank" rel="noopener">The first charge</a> is punishable by up to 93 days in jail, a $500 fine, 360 hours of community service, and vehicle immobilization. The second charge carries up to 180 days in jail, a $700 fine, 360 hours of community service, and vehicle immobilization.</p><p>Ludington, who serves on the bench for the Northern Division of Michigan’s Eastern District, is currently scheduled to have his case go before a jury beginning February 27. </p><p>News of the case comes after a separate drunk driving incident last week had far worse results.</p><p>A college athlete and his girlfriend were killed in a crash in North Carolina, in an incident where illegal immigrant Juan Alvarado Aguilar was charged with felonies.</p><p>According to the North Carolina State Highway Patrol, the crash happened late Friday night when <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/immigration/enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><u>Aguilar crossed the center line</u></a> and collided head-on with another vehicle.</p><p>Investigators say Aguilar refused medical treatment at the scene and was transported for a blood draw before being <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/police-and-law-enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">formally charged</a>.</p><p>The victims were identified as 20-year-old Fletcher Mark Daniel Harris and 19-year-old Skylar Nichole Provenza. Harris was the driver of the vehicle and a student-athlete at Catawba College, where he played on the men’s soccer team. Both victims were pronounced dead at the scene.</p>
