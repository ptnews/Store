<p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/nfl/new-orleans-saints" target="_blank" rel="noopener">New Orleans Saints</a> star wide receiver Chris Olave will not play in Week 18 after a blood clot was reportedly found in his lung. </p><p>The blood clot was caught early, and Olave was expected to be discharged from a hospital Thursday, according to Saints reporter <a href="https://x.com/nick_underhill/status/2006746100296982542?s=20" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">Nick Underhill.</a> Olave has no prior history of blood clots and is expected to be sidelined for about a month, according to ESPN. </p><p>Olave, 25, a former Ohio State star, caught 100 passes for 1,163 yards and nine touchdowns in 16 games. </p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE FOR MORE SPORTS COVERAGE ON FOXNEWS.COM </strong></a></p><p>Olave’s 100 receptions and 1,163 yards are the sixth most and seventh most in the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/nfl" target="_blank" rel="noopener">NFL</a>, respectively. </p><p>The Saints enter their Week 18 matchup against the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/nfl/atlanta-falcons" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Atlanta Falcons</a> on a four-game winning streak, and Olave’s connection with rookie quarterback Tyler Shough is a big reason for it. During the win streak, Olave had 27 catches for 382 yards and four touchdowns. </p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports/3-historic-nfl-franchises-find-themselves-same-boat-since-turn-century" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>3 HISTORIC NFL FRANCHISES FIND THEMSELVES IN SAME BOAT SINCE TURN OF THE CENTURY</strong></a></p><p>Olave’s absence also has playoff implications for the NFC South. The <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/nfl/carolina-panthers" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Carolina Panthers,</a> who play the Tampa Bay Buccaneers Saturday, can win the NFC South with a win over the Buccaneers or if the Saints lose to the Atlanta Falcons.</p><p>If the Panthers win Saturday, the Saints-Falcons matchup has no playoff implications. </p><p>If <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/nfl/tampa-bay-buccaneers" target="_blank" rel="noopener">the Buccaneers</a> win Saturday, then the Saints-Falcons matchup will decide who wins the NFC South. If the Saints lose, then the Buccaneers advance. But if the Saints beat the Falcons, the Panthers will win the division.</p><p>The Buccaneers and Panthers play Saturday at 4:30 p.m. ET, while the Saints play the Falcons at 1 p.m. ET Sunday. </p><p><i><strong>Follow Fox News Digital’s </strong></i><a href="https://twitter.com/FoxNewsSports_" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><i><strong>sports coverage on X</strong></i></a><i><strong>, and subscribe to </strong></i><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/newsletters" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><i><strong>the Fox News Sports Huddle newsletter</strong></i></a><i><strong>.</strong></i></p>
