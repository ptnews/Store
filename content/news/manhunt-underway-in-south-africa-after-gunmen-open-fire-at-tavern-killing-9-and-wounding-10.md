<p>Nine people were killed and at least 10 others wounded when gunmen opened fire inside a South African pub early Sunday, sparking a manhunt for the attackers in the country’s second <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/mass-shootings" target="_blank" rel="noopener">mass shooting</a> in less than a month.</p><p>The shooting happened just before 1 a.m. at KwaNoxolo tavern in Bekkersdal, a township located 28 miles <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/south-africa" target="_blank" rel="noopener">west of Johannesburg.</a> Police said about 12 unknown suspects in a white mini-bus and a silver sedan opened fire at pub patrons and continued to shoot randomly as they sped away from the scene.</p><p>"Some victims were randomly shot in the streets by unknown gunmen," police said.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/shooting-crowded-south-carolina-bar-leaves-least-dead-others-injured" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>SHOOTING AT CROWDED SOUTH CAROLINA BAR LEAVES AT LEAST 4 DEAD, 20 OTHERS INJURED</strong></a></p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/police-and-law-enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Maj. Gen. Fred Kekana</a>, Gauteng’s acting provincial commissioner, told The Associated Press that the gunmen, some of whom covered their faces with balaclavas, were armed with an AK-47 rifle and several 9mm pistols.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-blocks-south-africa-from-2026-g20-summit-alleged-horrific-human-rights-abuses" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TRUMP BLOCKS SOUTH AFRICA FROM 2026 G20 SUMMIT FOR ALLEGED 'HORRIFIC HUMAN RIGHTS ABUSES'</strong></a></p><p>Authorities have launched a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/manhunt" target="_blank" rel="noopener">manhunt</a> for the suspects, led by Gauteng’s Serious and Violent Crime Investigations unit in coordination with the Crime Detection Tracing Unit.</p><p>The attack follows a string of mass shootings at bars — often called shebeens or taverns in South Africa. Earlier this month, multiple gunmen opened fire at an unlicensed bar near the capital, killing at least 12 people and injuring 13 others.</p><p><i>The Associated Press contributed to this report.</i></p>
