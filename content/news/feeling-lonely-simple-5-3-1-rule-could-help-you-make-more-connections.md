<p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/health/wellness" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Social connection</a> is known to be a key factor in overall health and well-being.</p><p>About one in six people worldwide experience loneliness, which is linked to around 871,000 deaths annually due to <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/health" target="_blank" rel="noopener">impacts on health</a>, according to the World Health Organization.</p><p>One approach gaining attention is the 5-3-1 rule, a simple framework designed to help people build and maintain social relationships in everyday life.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/health/one-toxic-behavior-kills-relationships-leading-happiness-expert-warns" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>ONE TOXIC BEHAVIOR KILLS RELATIONSHIPS, LEADING HAPPINESS EXPERT WARNS</strong></a></p><p>The rule was reportedly developed by Canadian sociologist Kasley Killam, who argues that social health should be treated with the same consistency as physical or <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/health/mental-health" target="_blank" rel="noopener">mental health</a>.</p><p>"We need to be intentional about connection, just like we are with exercise and eating healthy foods," Killam recently told <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/5-3-1-rule-loneliness-socializing-less-stressful-2024-6" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">Business Insider</a>.</p><p>The 5-3-1 rule encourages social connection with three clear targets, as listed below.</p><p><strong>5:</strong> Each week, spend time with five different people or <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/topic/family-and-friends" target="_blank" rel="noopener">social groups</a>, such as friends, family members, coworkers, neighbors or acquaintances.</p><p><strong>3:</strong> Each month, have three deeper conversations with people you trust, where interactions go beyond small talk.</p><p><strong>1:</strong> Each day, aim for about one hour of social interaction, even if that time is spread out across shorter moments.</p><p><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/health" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>CLICK HERE FOR MORE HEALTH STORIES</u></strong></a></p><p>The goal is to encourage regular, intentional connection.</p><p>Jess Diller Kovler, a New York-based psychologist with Well By Messer and the Cognitive Therapy Center of Manhattan, said frameworks like the 5-3-1 rule are especially relevant right now.</p><p>"We need this now, more than ever," Kovler told Fox News Digital.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/newsletters?cmpid=fnfirstnl" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>CLICK HERE TO SIGN UP FOR OUR HEALTH NEWSLETTER</u></strong></a></p><p>She pointed out that many people underestimate how isolated they are, as <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/tech" target="_blank" rel="noopener">modern communications</a>, such as texting or social media, cannot fully substitute for face-to-face interaction.</p><p>The framework is meant to serve as a guideline, not something that needs to be followed perfectly, Kovler noted.</p><p>"Whether it’s 5-3-1 or 1-2-3 or 1-3-5 doesn’t matter. Anything beats zero-zero-zero," she said.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/lifestyle/quizzes" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>TEST YOURSELF WITH OUR LATEST LIFESTYLE QUIZ</u></strong></a></p><p>Jonathan Alpert, a New York-based psychotherapist and author of "Therapy Nation," told Fox News Digital that <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/lifestyle/relationships" target="_blank" rel="noopener">building stronger social connections</a> often starts with small, consistent steps rather than dramatic changes.</p><p>Alpert suggested choosing activities that create familiarity over time. </p><p>"<a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/lifestyle" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Join a class</a>, volunteer or show up regularly at the same gym or café. Familiarity builds comfort, and comfort builds relationships," he said.</p><p>The expert also encouraged people to <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/lifestyle/social-etiquette" target="_blank" rel="noopener">be more proactive</a>. </p><p>"Instead of waiting for others to reach out, send the first text. Suggest the coffee. Most people want more connection but don’t know how to start."</p>
