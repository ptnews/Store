<p>Mexican President Claudia Sheinbaum on Monday condemned what she described as <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/world-regions/latin-america" target="_blank" rel="noopener">U.S. intervention</a> in Venezuela and rejected the idea of American troops entering Mexico, reaffirming her government’s commitment to national sovereignty.</p><p>"We categorically reject intervention in the internal affairs of other countries," Sheinbaum said at a press conference in <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/world-regions/location-mexico" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Mexico City</a>, according to an official transcript <a href="https://www.gob.mx/presidencia/es/prensa/posicionamiento-presidenta-claudia-sheinbaum-pardo?idiom=es" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow"><u>of the speech</u></a> released by her office.</p><p>"The history of Latin America is clear and forceful, the intervention has never brought democracy, it has never generated well-being or lasting stability. Only people can build their own future, decide their path, exercise sovereignty over their natural resources and freely define their form of government," she said.</p><p>The U.S. military on Saturday carried out an <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/captured-venezuelan-dictator-maduro-faces-new-york-federal-judge-after-dramatic-palace-raid" target="_blank" rel="noopener">operation in Caracas, extracting former</a> Venezuelan leader Nicolás Maduro and his wife, Cilia Flores, from their compound.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/maduro-capture-echoes-noriega-takedown-used-rock-music-psychological-warfare-dictator" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>MADURO CAPTURE ECHOES NORIEGA TAKEDOWN THAT USED ROCK MUSIC AS PSYCHOLOGICAL WARFARE AGAINST DICTATOR</strong></a></p><p>Maduro and Flores were boarded onto USS Iwo Jima and <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/venezuelan-leader-maduro-lands-new-york-after-being-captured-us-forces-drug-conspiracy-charges" target="_blank" rel="noopener">flown to New York to face</a> federal charges, with their arraignment taking place on Monday in Manhattan.</p><p>Maduro is charged with four counts: narco-terrorism conspiracy, cocaine importation conspiracy, possession of machine-guns and destructive devices, and conspiracy to possess machine-guns and destructive devices.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/venezuelan-president-nicolas-maduro-narco-case-echoes-us-history-targeting-alleged-foreign-drug-kingpins" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>VENEZUELAN PRESIDENT NICOLAS MADURO’S NARCO CASE ECHOES US HISTORY OF TARGETING ALLEGED FOREIGN DRUG KINGPINS</strong></a></p><p>His wife is charged with three counts: cocaine importation conspiracy, possession of machine-guns and destructive devices, and conspiracy to possess machine-guns and destructive devices.</p><p>Sheinbaum said that following the capture of Venezuela’s leader and his wife, and <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-issues-direct-warning-venezuelas-new-leader-delcy-rodriguez-following-maduro-capture" target="_blank" rel="noopener">amid warnings from President Donald Trump</a> that Mexico must "get their act together," Mexican sovereignty and self-determination remain non-negotiable.</p><p>Trump told reporters aboard Air Force One that he thinks Sheinbaum is a "terrific person," but the cartels are "running Mexico."</p><p>"We’re going to have to do something. We’d love Mexico to do it, they’re capable of doing it, but unfortunately the cartels are very strong in Mexico," Trump said.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media/greene-hits-trump-over-venezuela-strikes-argues-action-doesnt-serve-american-people" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>GREENE HITS TRUMP OVER VENEZUELA STRIKES, ARGUES ACTION 'DOESN'T SERVE THE AMERICAN PEOPLE'</strong></a></p><p>Sheinbaum said her country is cooperating with the United States to help <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/drugs" target="_blank" rel="noopener">fight against drug trafficking</a>, organized crime and the flow of fentanyl.</p><p>"I don’t believe in an invasion. I don’t even think it’s something they’re taking very seriously," Sheinbaum told reporters in Spanish when asked about a potential U.S. intervention, according to <a href="https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/mexicos-sheinbaum-again-rejects-us-intervention-venezuela-2026-01-05/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow"><u>Reuters’ translation</u></a> of her remarks.</p><p>She said Trump has repeatedly insisted during their phone conversations that the U.S. Army be allowed to enter Mexico.</p><p>"We have said no very firmly — first because we defend our sovereignty, and second because it is not necessary," Sheinbaum told reporters.</p>
