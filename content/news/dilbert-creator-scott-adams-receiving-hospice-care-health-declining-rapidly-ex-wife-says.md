<p>"Dilbert" creator Scott Adams is in hospice care at his Northern California home as his health declines "rapidly" amid his battle with <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/health/cancer/prostate-cancer" target="_blank" rel="noopener">prostate cancer</a>, his ex-wife said.</p><p>Shelly Miles, who was married to the cartoonist from 2006 to 2014, told <a href="https://www.tmz.com/2026/01/12/dilbert-creator-scott-adams-hospice-prostate-cancer/" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">TMZ</a> that Adams had begun receiving "end-of-life care" at his home last week.</p><p>Miles said that she, her sister and Adams' stepdaughter Savannah, along with nurses, have been caring for him.</p><p>Earlier this month, Adams shared a health update on his podcast "Real Coffee with Scott Adams."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/health/celebrities-who-went-public-alarming-health-diagnoses-2025" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>5 CELEBRITIES WHO WENT PUBLIC WITH ALARMING HEALTH DIAGNOSES IN 2025</strong></a></p><p>"I talked to my radiologist yesterday, and it’s all bad news — the odds of me recovering are essentially zero," he said. "I’ll give you any updates if that changes, but it won’t."</p><p>"So there’s no chance that I’ll get my feeling back in my legs, and I’ve got some <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/health/wellness" target="_blank" rel="noopener">ongoing heart failure</a>, which is making it difficult to breathe sometimes during the day," the cartoonist continued. "However, you should prepare yourself that January will probably be a month of transition, one way or another."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/biden-rang-bell-completing-radiation-therapy-prostate-cancer" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>BIDEN 'RANG THE BELL' AFTER COMPLETING RADIATION THERAPY FOR PROSTATE CANCER</u></strong></a></p><p>Adams first announced his prostate cancer diagnosis on his show in May, saying that he was in pain every day and had been using a walker for months.</p><p>"If you're wondering if I'll get better, the answer is no, it will only get worse," he said at the time. "There's only one direction this goes."</p><p>In November, <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-admin-responds-dilbert-creators-plea-help-save-my-life-expediting-cancer-treatment" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Adams posted a plea</a> to President Donald Trump for help in scheduling a cancer drug treatment that he believed could prolong his life.</p><p>Health and Human Services Secretary <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/robert-f-kennedy-jr" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><u>Robert F. Kennedy Jr.</u></a> responded to Adams’ viral post, asking how to reach him.</p><p>"The President wants to help," RFK Jr. wrote.</p><p>Adams began writing and illustrating the Dilbert comic strip in 1989. Hundreds of newspapers pulled Dilbert in early 2023, after <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/laid-off-newspapers-drop-office-cartoon-dilbert-over-creators-racial-remarks" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow">he made racially charged comments</a> during his show. </p><p>He then launched a new, online subscription-based version called "Dilbert Reborn," which he describes on his website as "uncensored and spicier."</p>
