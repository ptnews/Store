<p>Television host <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/kelly-ripa" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Kelly Ripa</a> recently discovered she had misunderstood an internet travel trend.</p><p>During a recent episode of <a href="https://livewithkellyandmark.com/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">"Live with Kelly and Mark,"</a> Ripa and her husband, Mark Consuelos, chatted about the viral phrase "naked flying" — a term that's recently gained traction on social media.</p><p>Ripa initially believed it referred to a far more personal aspect of airplane attire.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/travel/viral-video-divides-passengers-about-handling-tsa-security-bins-not-after-screening" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>VIRAL VIDEO DIVIDES PASSENGERS ABOUT HANDLING TSA SECURITY BINS OR NOT AFTER SCREENING</strong></a></p><p>"I don't wear a bra on an airplane," she said on the show. "I just refuse. I feel like life is hard. What's the point?"</p><p>After realizing that "naked flying" referred instead to <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/travel/general/travel-tips" target="_blank" rel="noopener">traveling without luggage</a> — not clothing — Ripa said this may be the year she tries the trend herself.</p><p>She said the practice involves boarding a flight with no bags and relying only on items that fit in pockets, such as a phone and wallet.</p><p>Consuelos raised questions about how travelers manage once they arrive at their destination without any bags.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/travel/stash-simple-item-your-carry-on-avoid-travel-delays-stress-experts-say" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>STASH A SIMPLE ITEM IN YOUR CARRY-ON TO AVOID TRAVEL DELAYS AND STRESS, EXPERTS ADVISE</strong></a></p><p>Fox News Digital previously reported on the eyebrow-raising trend — citing <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/travel/flying-naked-latest-travel-trend-dividing-internet-doesnt-mean-what-you-think" target="_blank" rel="noopener">social media posts</a> that described how travelers handle clothing and other necessities on the other end of their trips. </p><p>Some say they rely on washing clothes in hotel sinks or showers during their trip.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/newsletters?cmpid=fnfirstnl"><strong><u>CLICK HERE TO SIGN UP FOR OUR LIFESTYLE NEWSLETTER</u></strong></a></p><p>Others say they shop or thrift-shop for clothing at their destination — then buy a suitcase or bag to bring their purchases home or ship the items back. </p><p>In some cases, the strategy can cost more than checking a bag.</p><p>While some travelers praise the trend for reducing stress and avoiding baggage fees, others say <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/travel" target="_blank" rel="noopener">flying without bags</a> is unrealistic for longer trips or work travel.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/lifestyle"><strong><u>CLICK HERE FOR MORE LIFESTYLE STORIES</u></strong></a></p><p>Brandon Blewett, a weekly traveler based in Texas and the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/lifestyle/lifestyle-books" target="_blank" rel="noopener">author of the book</a> "How to Avoid Strangers on Airplanes," told Fox News Digital that most work travelers still need at least a small bag for essentials like a laptop or charger.</p><p>Blewett said traveling with a small bag that fits under the seat can offer many of the same benefits, including faster exits, no baggage fees and <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/travel/general/travel-safety" target="_blank" rel="noopener">less stress</a> while deplaning.</p><p>"The sweet spot isn’t flying naked," he declared. "It’s flying light."</p><p><i>Sydney Borchers contributed reporting. </i></p>
