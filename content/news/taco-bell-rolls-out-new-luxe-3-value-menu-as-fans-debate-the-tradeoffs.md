<p>A major <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/food-drink/food/fast-food" target="_blank" rel="noopener">fast-food chain</a> is revamping its value menu as diners grow more price-conscious — and the early reaction is split.</p><p>Taco Bell is expanding its value offerings with the launch of its new <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/food-drink" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Luxe Value Menu,</a> featuring 10 items priced at $3 or less. The menu, which debuts nationwide Jan. 22 and combines five new items with five returning fan favorites, is being billed as part of the "new era of value."</p><p>"This isn't just a menu refresh. It's a new standard for value at Taco Bell and across the industry," Luis Restrepo, Taco Bell's chief marketing officer for North America, said in a statement. </p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/food-drink/fast-food-restaurants-use-new-technology-change-how-customers-order" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>FAST-FOOD RESTAURANTS USING NEW TECHNOLOGY TO RESHAPE HOW CUSTOMERS PLACE ORDERS</strong></a></p><p>The move comes as restaurant prices continue to outpace grocery inflation, putting pressure on chains to prioritize affordability. <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/food-drink/food/restaurants" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Restaurant prices rose</a> more than 4% over the past year, according to data from the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics.</p><p>New items include the Mini Taco Salad, Beefy Potato Loaded Griller, Chips &amp; Nacho Supreme Dip, Avocado Ranch Chicken Stacker and Salted Caramel Churros. </p><p>Prices range from $1.99 to $2.99. <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/dessert" target="_blank" rel="noopener">The churros</a> are the lowest-priced new addition and the only limited-time item, according to a news release.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/food-drink/fast-food-goes-glp-1-shake-shack-latest-roll-out-ozempic-friendly-menu" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>FAST FOOD GOES GLP-1: SHAKE SHACK JOINS PROTEIN CRAZE WITH NEW OZEMPIC-FRIENDLY MENU</strong></a></p><p>Five popular items from Taco Bell's previous Cravings Value Menu are also sticking around, including the Cheesy Roll Up, Spicy Potato Soft Taco and Cheesy Double Beef Burrito.</p><p>Early feedback was generally positive.</p><p><a href="https://www.allrecipes.com/new-taco-bell-luxe-value-menu-11882672" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">Allrecipes praised</a> the Mini Taco Salad, calling it "worth the wait" but noting that it is "not very drive-thru-friendly" and is best eaten inside the restaurant.</p><p>Also highlighted were the crisp exterior and creamy filling of the limited-time churros — while <a href="https://www.tastingtable.com/2073664/taco-bell-luxe-value-menu-review/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">Tasting Table</a> named them the best item on the menu and one of the easiest to eat on the go.</p><p><a however. </p><p>Some reviewers said the Avocado Ranch Chicken Stacker is lacking <a menu items can be messy to eat in the car.</p><p>Some longtime fans also said the value comes with a cost, meaning the removal of beloved menu staples to make room for the new. One Redditor summarized it this way: "Good if you like those items. Bad if you liked the old ones."</p><p>"RIP Loaded Beef Nachos," another user wrote in the r/fastfood subreddit.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/lifestyle/quizzes" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TEST YOURSELF WITH OUR LATEST LIFESTYLE QUIZ</strong></a></p><p>"Not happy they got rid of the double stack and loaded beef nachos," another Redditor said.</p><p>"They dropped the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/food-drink/recipes/cuisines/mexican" target="_blank" rel="noopener">chicken enchilada burrito,"</a> someone else lamented in r/tacobell.</p><p>But there may be hope for those mourning certain menu items.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/lifestyle" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE FOR MORE LIFESTYLE STORIES</strong></a></p><p>"While menus evolve to make room for new innovations, we're always listening to feedback and never say never on a possible return," the company told Fox News Digital. "In the meantime, our team members are ready to help guests find a new favorite on the Luxe Value Menu."</p><p>Taco Bell's expanded value menu reflects a broader shift in fast food as consumers continue to look for discounts and <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/food-drink/recipes/meals" target="_blank" rel="noopener">lower-priced meals</a> after years of rising costs. </p><p>Value menus have become a key way for chains to draw customers back to the drive-thru, <a href="https://qz.com/taco-bell-value-menu-additions-2026" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">Quartz reported.</a> </p><p>Taco Bell's value options have helped fuel strong sales growth, even as some competitors see traffic slow, according to <a href="https://www.restaurantdive.com/news/taco-bell-expands-new-value-menu-nationwide/809265/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">Restaurant Dive.</a></p><p>The strategy stands out as rival chains lean into premium upgrades and protein-focused limited-time items, with Taco Bell positioning its Luxe Value Menu as an ongoing offering rather than a short-term promotion.</p><p>The company said it has "long been committed to providing <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/food-drink/food" target="_blank" rel="noopener">high-quality, craveable food</a> at accessible price points."</p>
