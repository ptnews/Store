<p>A longtime television weatherman has died in a small plane crash <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/west/idaho" target="_blank" rel="noopener">in Idaho</a> earlier this week.</p><p>On Tuesday, Jan. 27, Roland Steadham, the chief meteorologist for CBS2, was named as one of the people who died in the two-person crash near Emmett, Idaho earlier that day, CBS affiliate <a href="https://idahonews.com/news/local/cbs2-meteorologist-roland-steadham-dies-in-plane-crash" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">KBOI</a> announced.</p><p>"For the past decade, Roland Steadham guided people through storm after storm in Idaho," the station wrote. "He loved to study <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/weather" target="_blank" rel="noopener">the weather</a> and shared it with people across Idaho. Roland’s excitement for the weather was contagious."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/well-known-texas-attorneys-wife-among-victims-deadly-private-jet-crash-maine" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>WELL-KNOWN TEXAS ATTORNEY'S WIFE AMONG VICTIMS OF DEADLY PRIVATE JET CRASH IN MAINE</u></strong></a></p><p>According to the Gem County Sheriff’s Office, officials responded to a report of a plane crash near the Payette River, just west of Montour, Idaho, on Tuesday at 10:58 a.m. local time.</p><p>Preliminary information, authorities said, indicated that the small plane clipped a power line and crashed upon the ice in the river. Both male occupants were "fatally injured" in the crash, authorities said.</p><p>Officials did not reveal who was flying the plane at the time of the incident.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/business-jet-crashes-maine-airport-leaving-all-passengers-presumed-dead-winter-storm-pummels-us" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>BUSINESS JET CRASHES AT MAINE AIRPORT LEAVING ALL PASSENGERS PRESUMED DEAD AS WINTER STORM PUMMELS US</u></strong></a></p><p>The chief meteorologist, 67, was a devoted husband, father of six and grandfather, the station said.</p><p>"Roland often talked about his wife, Erin, and <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/faith-values/family" target="_blank" rel="noopener">his six children and grandchildren</a>," KBOI said. "In recent years, he and his family have enjoyed their time kayaking throughout Idaho and spending time together grilling food in their backyard."</p><p>"Rarely did he have a conversation without sharing about his love for his family," the outlet said.</p><p>Along with his love of his family, Steadham owned a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/environment/air" target="_blank" rel="noopener">small aircraft</a> and enjoyed recreational flying.</p><p>"It was a hobby he enjoyed and often would share stories about local pilots and their accomplishments," the outlet said.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/tips" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>SEND US A TIP HERE</strong></a></p><p>Steadham attended the University of Utah and Brigham Young University before beginning his career as a meteorologist in 1995, according to his <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/roland-steadham-12121317/" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">LinkedIn page</a>.</p><p>In a statement to Fox News Digital, the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) confirmed that a small aircraft carrying two people crashed into a frozen river near Emmett, Idaho.</p><p>"<a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/finance/investigations" target="_blank" rel="noopener">The FAA and NTSB</a> will investigate. The NTSB will be in charge of the investigation and will provide further updates," the agency said.</p>
