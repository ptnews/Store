<p>The <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/mlb/toronto-blue-jays" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Toronto Blue Jays</a>, who were two outs away from winning the World Series, continue to make big moves in free agency as they look to bolster their squad.</p><p>The Blue Jays agreed to a contract with Japanese star infielder Kazuma Okamoto, according to multiple reports. The contract is four-year, $60 million, according to the <a href="https://x.com/JonHeyman/status/2007539628732199066?s=20" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">New York Post.</a></p><p>Okamoto starred in the Nippon Professional Baseball’s most storied franchise, the Yomiuri Giants, which is Japan’s equivalent of the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/mlb/new-york-yankees" target="_blank" rel="noopener">New York Yankees.</a></p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE FOR MORE SPORTS COVERAGE ON FOXNEWS.COM</strong></a></p><p>The 29-year-old was a six-time All-Star in Japan, and starred both at the plate and in the field. He led the NPB Central League in home runs in 2020, 2021 and 2023.</p><p>Starting in 2018, Okamoto had a run of six consecutive seasons with 30 or more home runs. His career high in home runs came in 2023, when he hit 41.</p><p>Okamoto can play first and third base, capably winning two Gold Gloves at third and one at first base.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports/ex-mlb-all-star-lenny-dykstra-faces-drug-charges-following-new-years-day-traffic-stop-pennsylvania" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>EX-MLB ALL-STAR LENNY DYKSTRA FACES DRUG CHARGES FOLLOWING NEW YEAR'S DAY TRAFFIC STOP IN PENNSYLVANIA</strong></a></p><p>In 2025, Okamoto was limited to just 69 games after sustaining a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/injuries" target="_blank" rel="noopener">left-elbow injury</a> in a collision with a batter running down the line while playing first. However, in limited action, Okamoto was outstanding, hitting .327 with 15 home runs and 49 RBI, good for a 1.014 OPS.</p><p>In 11 seasons in the NPB, Okamoto had a .277 batting average with 248 home runs and 717 RBI.</p><p>Okamoto has already had success in the United States, as he hit a home run off Kyle Freeland to help Japan beat the United States in the 2023 World Baseball Classic final.</p><p>The Blue Jays had already signed starting pitcher Dylan Cease to a seven-year, $210 million contract, KBO MVP Cody Ponce to a three-year, $30 million contract, and reliever Tyler Rogers to a three-year, $37 million contract.
