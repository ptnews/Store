<p>A single 10-minute workout may trigger blood changes that help fight <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/health/cancer/colon-cancer" target="_blank" rel="noopener">colon cancer</a>.</p><p>That's according to new research from scientists at Newcastle University, who found that exercise quickly changes the blood in ways that affect colon cancer cells in the lab.</p><p>In the study, the U.K. researchers exposed colon cancer cells to human blood serum collected immediately after exercise, finding that the cells repaired DNA damage faster and showed gene activity patterns linked to slower growth.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/health/spike-deadly-cancer-before-50-linked-common-convenience-foods" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>SPIKE IN DEADLY CANCER BEFORE 50 LINKED TO COMMON CONVENIENCE FOODS</strong></a></p><p>The blood samples came from 30 adults who had just completed a short, high-intensity <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/health/nutrition-and-fitness/fitness" target="_blank" rel="noopener">cycling workout</a> that lasted about 10 to 12 minutes, according to a press release.</p><p>Samuel T. Orange, an associate professor at Newcastle University and one of the study's authors, spoke with Fox News Digital about the findings.</p><p>"Our findings show that exercise rapidly triggers molecular changes in the bloodstream that can act directly on colon cancer cells, reshaping gene activity and supporting <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/genetics" target="_blank" rel="noopener">DNA damage repair</a>," he said.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/health/common-over-the-counter-medication-slashes-colorectal-cancer-recurrence-half" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>COMMON OVER-THE-COUNTER MEDICATION SLASHES COLORECTAL CANCER RECURRENCE IN HALF</strong></a></p><p>The results suggest that even brief activity can make a difference.
