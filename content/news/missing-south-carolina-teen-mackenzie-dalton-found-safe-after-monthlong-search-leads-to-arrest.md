<p>A South Carolina teenager <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/topic/missing-persons" target="_blank" rel="noopener">reported missing</a> late last year has been found safe, and a man has been arrested in the case, authorities said.</p><p>The Lexington County Sheriff’s Office said Mackenzie Dalton, 16, was found in Lexington County following an investigation into her disappearance.</p><p>Deputies said there is no indication Dalton was ever held against her will or with someone she did not want to be with.</p><p>Dalton was <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/teen-girl-vanishes-from-south-carolina-home-police-fear-shes-serious-danger-after-monthlong-search" target="_blank" rel="noopener">reported missing</a> after she was last seen leaving her home in late November, according to a Jan. 2 statement from the sheriff’s office. At the time, authorities said she was considered an endangered juvenile due to her age and the length of the time she had been gone.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/teen-girl-vanishes-from-south-carolina-home-police-fear-shes-serious-danger-after-monthlong-search" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TEEN GIRL VANISHES FROM SOUTH CAROLINA HOME; POLICE FEAR SHE'S IN SERIOUS DANGER AFTER MONTHLONG SEARCH</strong></a></p><p>Investigators previously said they believed Dalton may have been with a man she knew, although authorities did not release the man's identity.</p><p>The sheriff’s office said the vehicle Dalton drove away in and the suitcase she took from her home were recovered during the investigation. The teen was described as 5 feet, 4 inches tall and weighing 118 pounds.</p><p>Deputies arrested Mackenzie Keith English Jr., 18, charging him with obstruction of justice, during their investigation.</p><p>Authorities accuse English of lying to investigators about being with Dalton and about his knowledge of her whereabouts.</p><p>English is being held at the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/police-and-law-enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Lexington County Detention Center</a>, the sheriff’s office said.</p>
