<p>Israeli archaeologists recently unearthed <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/science/archaeology" target="_blank" rel="noopener">ancient gold</a> at a former Christian monastery in the Judean Desert.</p><p>The announcement was made by Israel's Coordinator of Government Activities in the Territories (COGAT) this month. The ancient treasure was found at the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/travel/general/historic-sites" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Hyrcania archaeological site</a> in the northern Judean Desert, located around ten miles east of Jerusalem in the West Bank.</p><p>The treasure consisted of two gold coins and a delicate gold ring. Despite the site being vulnerable to antiquities looting in the past, the artifacts miraculously survived 1,400 years.</p><p>The site was founded in the late Hellenistic period, between the second and first centuries BC, when it was used as a fortress.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/newsletters?cmpid=fnfirstnl" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>CLICK HERE TO SIGN UP FOR OUR
