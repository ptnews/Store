<p>FOX Weather will be providing extensive live coverage of the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/weather/winter-storm" target="_blank" rel="noopener">major winter storm</a> that is expected to impact huge swaths of the country.</p><p>Meteorologists and correspondents from FOX Weather, FOX News Media's free ad-supported streaming television channel, will be dispatched to cities from Texas to the northeast to offer on-the-ground reporting of the storm, which is projected to cross over 40 states throughout the weekend. </p><p>FOX Weather will have live programming from 6 a.m. ET through midnight Saturday and Sunday.</p><p>Beginning Thursday, storm specialist Mike Seidel will report from <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/southwest/oklahoma" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Tulsa, Oklahoma,</a> and meteorologist Bayne Froney will report from Dallas. On Friday, meteorologist Bob Van Dillen will lead reporting from Atlanta with correspondent Brandy Campbell contributing to coverage from Oxford, Mississippi.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxweather.com/watch/fmc-p42hpeb59aweygtq" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>HISTORIC WINTER STORM COULD IMPACT OVER 235 MILLION AMERICANS</strong></a></p><p>On Saturday, correspondent Katie Byrne will report live from Washington, D.C.
