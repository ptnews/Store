<p>Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy said Russian attack drones struck a civilian passenger train in the Kharkiv region on Tuesday, killing at least four people in what he called an "<a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/terrorism" target="_blank" rel="noopener">act of terrorism</a>."</p><p>"In any country, a drone strike on a civilian train would be regarded in the same way – purely as an act of terrorism. There would be no doubt about the classification, neither in Europe, nor in America, nor in the Arab world, nor in China, nor anywhere else," he wrote in a post on X. "There is, and can be, no military justification for killing civilians in a train carriage."</p><p>The <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/conflicts/ukraine" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Kharkiv Regional Prosecutor's Office</a> said Wednesday on Telegram that the number of confirmed fatalities had risen to five, with two people injured and one man reported missing.</p><p>Oleksiy Kuleba, Ukraine’s deputy prime minister, said three Russian drones were used in the attack on the train, which was carrying more than 200 people.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/zelenskyy-says-fresh-russian-attack-ukraine-shows-putins-true-attitude-ahead-trump-meeting" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>ZELENSKYY SAYS FRESH RUSSIAN ATTACK ON UKRAINE SHOWS PUTIN'S 'TRUE ATTITUDE' AHEAD OF TRUMP MEETING</strong></a></p><p>Eighteen passengers were inside the carriage hit by one of the drones, he added.</p><p>The attack followed <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/zelenskyy-touts-constructive-trilateral-talks-between-us-russia-ukraine-abu-dhabi" target="_blank" rel="noopener">trilateral talks between Moscow, Kyiv</a> and Washington in Abu Dhabi over the weekend that were aimed at ending the nearly four-year war.</p><p>U.S. special envoy Steve Witkoff said the meetings were "very constructive" and plans were being made for the sides to meet again this week.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/russia-ukraine-discuss-territory-trump-says-both-sides-want-make-deal" target="_blank" rel="noopener">strike Kyiv’s energy infrastructure</a> during negotiations, leaving thousands of residents without power and heat as winter conditions persist.</p><p><a Crews from almost across the entire country have been deployed to assist," he noted.</p>
