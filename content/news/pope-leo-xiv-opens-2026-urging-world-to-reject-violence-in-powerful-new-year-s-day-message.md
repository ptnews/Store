<p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/religion/roman-catholic" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Pope Leo</a> XIV opened the new year Thursday with an appeal to the world for peace and urging people everywhere to begin 2026 by rejecting violence and committing to reconciliation.</p><p>Speaking during his <a href="https://www.vaticannews.va/en/pope/news/2026-01/pope-in-angelus-start-today-to-build-a-year-of-peace.html" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">first Angelus address</a> of 2026, the pope invited the faithful to "build a year of peace by disarming our hearts and refraining from every form of violence."</p><p>Around 40,000 gathered in <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/world-regions/italy" target="_blank" rel="noopener">St. Peter’s Square in Rome</a> to hear the pope’s New Year's Day message on the Solemnity of Mary, the Mother of God, which also coincides with World Day of Peace.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/pope-leo-xiv-calls-out-christian-persecution-amid-latest-massacre-civilians-african-nation" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>POPE LEO XIV CALLS OUT CHRISTIAN PERSECUTION AMID LATEST MASSACRE OF CIVILIANS IN AFRICAN NATION</strong></a></p><p>At the start of his address, Pope Leo extended his wishes for peace and good in the new year and offered a special greeting to the president of the Italian Republic, Sergio Mattarella.</p><p>After the Angelus prayer, the pope said Jan. 1 has been marked as the World Day of Peace since 1968 at the initiative of Pope St. Paul VI.</p><p>Reflecting on the meaning of the day, Pope Leo referred to the words that came to him after his election as bishop of Rome: "Peace be with you all."</p><p>"This peace," the pope said, "is unarmed and disarming. It comes from God; it is a gift of his unconditional love, and it is entrusted to our responsibility."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/opinion/advent-peace-demands-action-defend-those-who-risk-lives-worship-freely" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>ADVENT PEACE DEMANDS ACTION: DEFEND THOSE WHO RISK THEIR LIVES TO WORSHIP FREELY</strong></a></p><p>He also emphasized that <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/pope-leo-xiv-vows-work-unity-peace-during-inaugural-mass" target="_blank" rel="noopener">peace is not something</a> imposed by force, but a gift that needs personal conversion and choices in daily life.</p><p>Calling on Christians and all people of goodwill, Pope Leo urged them to begin immediately.</p><p>"With <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/christianity" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Christ’s grace</a>," he said, "let us start today to build a year of peace, disarming our hearts and refraining from all violence."</p><p>The pope acknowledged that efforts to build peace are already underway across the world and expressed appreciation for individuals and organizations dedicated to ending conflict and promoting dialogue.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/pope-leo-xiv-delivers-first-christmas-message-calling-end-violence-middle-east-russia-ukraine-war" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>POPE LEO XIV DELIVERS FIRST CHRISTMAS MESSAGE CALLING FOR END TO VIOLENCE IN MIDDLE EAST, RUSSIA-UKRAINE WAR</strong></a></p><p>In line with the peacekeeping theme, he highlighted a national <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/world-protests" target="_blank" rel="noopener">peace march</a> held Dec. 31 in Catania, Italy, and mentioned another organized by members of the Community of Sant’Egidio, known for its international peace initiatives.</p><p>Pope Leo also welcomed students and teachers from <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/northeast/new-jersey" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Richland, New Jersey</a>, who were in the square watching.</p><p>At the end of the address, the pope spoke about the 800th anniversary of the death of St. Francis of Assisi, a figure also closely associated with peace.</p><p>He closed with the saint’s biblical blessing: "The Lord bless you and keep you; the Lord make his face shine upon you and be gracious to you; the Lord lift his countenance upon you and <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/pope-leo-xiv-makes-first-social-media-post-calling-peace" target="_blank" rel="noopener">give you peace</a>."</p><p>Pope Leo ended by entrusting the new year to Mary, the Mother of God, asking that she guide humanity on its journey in the new year.</p>
