<p>Every golfer's dream is to play at <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/golf/the-masters" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Augusta National</a>, and Michael La Sasso had a chance to live it — but he turned it down.</p><p>La Sasso, who won the NCAA individual title last year at Omni La Costa Resort &amp; Spa in California, forfeited his Masters' eligibility after joining <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/golf/liv-golf" target="_blank" rel="noopener">LIV Golf</a> on Tuesday.</p><p>Reigning NCAA individual champions are eligible to play in the following year's Masters if they remain amateurs. La Sasso’s deal with the Saudi-backed league makes him a professional golfer, however, no longer making him eligible to play at Augusta.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE FOR MORE SPORTS COVERAGE ON FOXNEWS.COM</strong></a><i><strong> </strong></i></p><p>Last year's Masters field <a href="https://www.masters.com/en_US/news/articles/2025-04-07/2025-04-07_how_to_qualify_for_the_masters.html" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">had 21 criteria</a>, including the reigning NCAA champion, but La Sasso does not fit into any of the others.</p><p>"It's a rare opportunity to learn from one of the greatest players in the history of the game, and I don't take that lightly," La Sasso said in a statement. "LIV Golf allows me to compete at the highest level on a global stage, and I thrive in a team environment, especially one with the camaraderie and support that defines HyFlyers GC. My focus is on learning, continuing to improve, and doing everything I can to help our team succeed."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports/life-size-statue-golf-legend-seve-ballesteros-stolen-from-his-spain-hometown" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>LIFE-SIZE STATUE OF GOLF LEGEND SEVE BALLESTEROS STOLEN FROM HIS SPAIN HOMETOWN</strong></a></p><p>HyFlyers GC is captained by Phil Mickelson, who was one of the first golf stars to join LIV in 2022.</p><p>La Sasso attended <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/ncaa/ole-miss-rebels" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Ole Miss</a> and helped the Rebels to an eighth-place finish in the team competition. He finished two strokes ahead of Phichaksn Maichon of Texas A&amp;M to take home the title.</p><p>He defeated Jose Luis Ballester of Arizona State by six strokes. Ballester joined LIV shortly after last year's Masters, which he earned entry to as the 2024 U.S. Amateur champion.</p><p>La Sasso competed in last year's <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/golf/us-open-golf" target="_blank" rel="noopener">U.S. Open</a> at Oakmont but missed the cut.</p><p><i><strong>Follow Fox News Digital’s </strong></i><a href="https://twitter.com/FoxNewsSports_" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><i><strong>sports coverage on X</strong></i></a><i><strong>, and subscribe to </strong></i><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/newsletters" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><i><strong>the Fox News Sports Huddle newsletter</strong></i></a><i><strong>.</strong></i></p>
