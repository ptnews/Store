<p>A group of Republican attorneys general and female sports advocates gathered Monday in Washington, D.C., to call on the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/judiciary/supreme-court" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Supreme Court</a> to give states the ability to set sex-based rules for school athletics.</p><p>The group, which included former college swimmer turned activist Riley Gaines, said a decision by the high court in a pair of cases examining transgender athletes in school sports would serve to preserve fairness in women’s sports.</p><p>The Supreme Court will hear oral arguments in the two cases, brought by Idaho and West Virginia, on Tuesday.</p><p>Those gathered at the event said the cases could be landmarks in the legal fight over policies surrounding transgender people.</p><p>The high court could decide to rule narrowly on the rights of transgender athletes or offer a more sweeping precedent over discrimination claims across industries. The decision could have far-reaching impacts on transgender bathroom policies and sex designation on documents, such as passports and driver's licenses.</p><p><i>This is a developing story.
