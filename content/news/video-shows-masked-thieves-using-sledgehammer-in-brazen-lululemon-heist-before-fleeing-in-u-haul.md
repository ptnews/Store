<p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/northeast/pennsylvania" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Pennsylvania authorities</a> revealed Tuesday that surveillance video captured a brazen Lululemon smash-and-grab, with two masked individuals using a sledgehammer to carry out the overnight break-in.</p><p>The suspects, seen grabbing armfuls of merchandise during multiple rounds of theft, reportedly fled in a U-Haul truck, Lower Merion Police told Fox 29 Philadelphia.</p><p>The incident in the Ardmore shopping center reportedly occurred just before 2 a.m. Tuesday and resulted in an estimated $5,000 in stolen merchandise.</p><p>In the video released by police, two masked suspects dressed in all black swung what appeared to be a sledgehammer into the store’s windowed entrance. They then quickly rushed through the opening without hesitation, immediately targeting winter coats and shirts in the men’s section.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/holiday-crime-fears-grow-jugging-thieves-target-shoppers-carrying-cash-gifts-only-matter-time" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>HOLIDAY CRIME FEARS GROW AS ‘JUGGING’ THIEVES TARGET SHOPPERS CARRYING CASH AND GIFTS: ‘ONLY A MATTER OF TIME’</strong></a></p><p>The suspects appeared to grab entire racks of puffer jackets and sweatshirts before returning outside. The video then shows the men returning to the store a second time after dropping off the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/crime" target="_blank" rel="noopener">stolen items</a> to take even more merchandise.</p><p>The footage shows the heist lasted roughly five minutes.</p><p>The thieves fled after loading the stolen merchandise into the bed of a U-Haul pickup truck parked outside, Fox 29 reported.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/suspected-thieves-caught-camera-smashing-washington-state-storefront-truck-atm-heist-attempt" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>SUSPECTED THIEVES CAUGHT ON CAMERA SMASHING WASHINGTON STATE STOREFRONT WITH TRUCK IN ATM HEIST ATTEMPT</strong></a></p><p>By the afternoon, the store had reopened, but the front door's glass remained shattered, with a large banner placed in front of the entrance, the local station reported. </p><p>According to an employee from another business nearby, Tuesday’s incident was not the first time the Lululemon store was targeted. </p><p>"I walked out here with my bartender, and we looked, and it’s scary that it’s happening right across the way from us," Gina Picciano, general manager of Lola’s Garden, told Fox 29.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/masked-looters-caught-camera-trashing-florida-store-stealing-clothes" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>MASKED LOOTERS CAUGHT ON CAMERA TRASHING FLORIDA STORE, STEALING CLOTHES</strong></a></p><p>Last year, Picciano said, a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/robbery-theft" target="_blank" rel="noopener">theft occurred</a> during store hours. </p><p>"All of the sudden, a bunch of people were just running out with armfuls of stuff. So, it happens in broad daylight," Picciano added. "It doesn’t matter whether it’s night or day, and that’s crazy."</p><p>The Lower Merion Police told Fox 29 that delayed alarm alerts can delay response times, emphasizing crime prevention and partnerships with merchants.</p><p>"Sometimes there’s a delay in receiving these calls with Lululemon because their operations center, they’re the ones that receive the video and the alarm," <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/police-and-law-enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Superintendent of Police</a> Andy Block said. "Prevention here is the key for this, and we’re always concerned about the criminal element coming into our communities. And our officers work all the time to try to prevent this. We want to work with our merchants to try to make our community safer."</p><p>Fox News Digital reached out to the Lower Merion Police and Lululemon for more information.</p>
