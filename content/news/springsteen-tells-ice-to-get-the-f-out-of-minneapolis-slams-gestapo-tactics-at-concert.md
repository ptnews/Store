<p>Bruce Springsteen called out U.S. <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/immigration" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE)</a> during a show in New Jersey on Saturday and told the agency to "get the f--- out" of Minneapolis, according to reports, dedicating a song to Renee Good.</p><p>"If you believe in democracy, in liberty, if you believe that truth still matters, that it’s worth speaking out, that it is worth fighting for, if you believe in the power of the law and no one stands above it, if you stand against heavily armed masked federal troops invading American cities and using Gestapo tactics against our fellow citizens, if you believe you don’t deserve to be murdered for exercising your American right to protest, then send a message to this president," Springsteen told the crowd, <a href="https://www.njarts.net/springsteen-criticizes-ice-dedicates-the-promised-land-to-renee-good-at-light-of-day-show/" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">according to</a> NJArts.net.</p><p>Springsteen performed at the Light of Day festival in New Jersey on Saturday night.</p><p>Quoting <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media/minneapolis-mayor-frey-refuses-walk-back-get-f-out-message-ice" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Minneapolis Mayor Jacob Frey</a>, he added, "and as the mayor of that city has said, ‘ICE should get the f--- out of Minneapolis.'"</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media/ex-nbc-news-host-chuck-todd-laments-democratic-party-collection-people-dont-like-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>EX-NBC NEWS HOST CHUCK TODD LAMENTS DEMOCRATIC PARTY A 'COLLECTION OF PEOPLE THAT DON'T LIKE TRUMP'</u></strong></a></p><p>The rock legend went on to dedicate his song "The Promised Land" to Renee Good, who was fatally shot by an ICE agent in Minneapolis earlier this month.</p><p>"So this one is for you, and the memory of the mother of three and American citizen Renee Good," Springsteen said.</p><p>Springsteen, a longtime Democrat, campaigned for Vice President Kamala Harris in 2024, <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/joe-biden" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Joe Biden</a> in 2020, Hillary Clinton in 2016 and Barack Obama in 2012 and 2008.</p><p><a Americans.</p><p>"We’re desperately in need of an effective alternative party or for the Democratic Party to find someone who can speak to the majority of the nation.
