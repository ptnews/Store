<p>The French navy intercepted a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/world-regions/russia" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Russian oil</a> tanker in the Mediterranean suspected of operating as part of Moscow’s shadow fleet, a network of falsely-flagged vessels used to export oil and avoid Western sanctions, according to reports.</p><p>President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/emmanuel-macron" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Emmanuel Macron</a> said Thursday that French forces had boarded and searched the tanker, which is subject to international sanctions. </p><p>The ship was reportedly sailing from Murmansk, in northern Russia, under the Comoros flag.</p><p>Writing on X, Macron said the operation took place on the high seas in the Mediterranean with the support of several allied countries.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/ghost-ships-moving-illicit-oil-has-entered-trumps-crosshairs-escalating-quiet-war-sea" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>‘GHOST SHIPS’ FERRYING ILLICIT OIL HAVE SAILED INTO TRUMP’S CROSSHAIRS</strong></a></p><p>The French president added that the vessel had been diverted for further checks.</p><p>The tanker, identified as the Grinch, was intercepted between the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/world-regions/spain" target="_blank" rel="noopener">southern coast of Spain</a> and the northern coast of Morocco in the western Mediterranean, French maritime police said. </p><p><a href="https://apnews.com/article/france-navy-russia-shadow-fleet-tanker-32fe6c46d2ad32219c01f49ef7c9dc16" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">The Associated Press</a> reported the interception.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/world-regions/france" target="_blank" rel="noopener">France and the U.K.</a> gathered and shared intelligence on the vessel, according to French military officials who spoke to The Associated Press on condition of anonymity.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-administration-files-seizure-warrants-targeting-ships-tied-venezuelan-oil-trade-report" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TRUMP ADMINISTRATION FILES SEIZURE WARRANTS TARGETING SHIPS TIED TO VENEZUELAN OIL TRADE: REPORT</strong></a></p><p>The interception is the latest in a growing number of <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/inside-sea-war-contain-dark-fleet-vessels-what-us-seizure-signals-russia" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Russian-linked vessels stopped</a> by U.S. and European authorities in recent months as Western powers intensify efforts to disrupt Russia’s oil exports.</p><p>As previously reported by Fox News Digital, in early January, <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/military/navy" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><u>U.S. forces</u></a> seized another sanctioned tanker in the Atlantic Ocean.</p><p>U.S. European Command (EUCOM) announced the seizure of the Russian-flagged Marinera oil tanker in the North Atlantic Sea.</p><p>France’s Mediterranean Maritime Prefecture said the team that boarded the Grinch Thursday had inspected the vessel and decided documents raised doubts about the regularity of the vessel’s flag.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/us-military-seizes-another-fugitive-oil-tanker-linked-venezuela" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>US MILITARY SEIZES ANOTHER FUGITIVE OIL TANKER LINKED TO VENEZUELA</strong></a></p><p>The tanker is being escorted by the French navy to an anchorage for additional verification.</p><p>The European Union has imposed 19 rounds of sanctions on Russia since its full-scale invasion of Ukraine in 2022. </p><p>Despite those measures, Russia has continued to export millions of barrels of oil, mainly to China and India and often at discounted prices.</p><p>What has become known as a "shadow fleet" consists of hundreds of old and poorly regulated tankers that change names, ownership structures and flags to avoid detection and sanctions.</p><p>Maritime data firm Lloyd’s List Intelligence estimates the global shadow fleet at about 1,400 tankers, many of which are subject to U.S., British or European sanctions, <a href="https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/over-30-sanctioned-ships-venezuela-risk-after-us-tanker-seizure-2025-12-11/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">Reuters</a> reported.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/volodymyr-zelenskyy" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy</a> criticized European countries for not doing more to stop the transport of Russian oil using sanctioned vessels and which he says helps fund the war in Ukraine.</p><p>"Why can [U.S. President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Donald Trump</a>] stop tankers of the ‘shadow fleet’ and seize their oil, while Europe can’t?" Zelensky asked at the World Economic Forum in Davos, Switzerland, Thursday.</p><p>"Russian oil is transported right along the European coast. This oil funds the war against Ukraine. This oil helps destabilize Europe."</p>
