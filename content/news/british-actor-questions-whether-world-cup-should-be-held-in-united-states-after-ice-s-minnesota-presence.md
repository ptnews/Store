<p>Actor and Monty Python alum <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/entertainment" target="_blank" rel="noopener">John Cleese</a> questioned whether the World Cup should be held in the U.S. in the wake of an ICE agent's fatal shooting of a woman in Minnesota.</p><p>Renee Good was killed while operating a vehicle that agents ordered her to exit, according to Department of Homeland Security Secretary <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/kristi-noem" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><u>Kristi Noem.</u></a> Good, according to Noem, refused and "attempted to run them over and ram them with her vehicle."</p><p>Cleese, however, disagreed with Noem's version of events.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE FOR MORE SPORTS COVERAGE ON FOXNEWS.COM</strong></a><i><strong> </strong></i></p><p>Cleese shared a post that showed ICE arresting a woman in Minneapolis in the aftermath of Good's death. The actor suggested that FIFA may want to reconsider holding games in the United States.</p><p>"Is it a good idea to hold a major event like the FIFA World Cup in a country where the Rule of Law no longer exists," Cleese <a href="https://x.com/JohnCleese/status/2009832032847741324?s=20" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">asked in an X post</a>.</p><p>President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Donald Trump</a> has threatened to keep games out of American cities he deems unsafe.</p><p>Cleese's post was shared by tennis legend Martina Navratilova amid dozens of other anti-ICE posts.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports/martina-navratilova-calls-ice-evil-thugs-amid-dozens-social-media-posts-following-minnesota-incident"><strong>MARTINA NAVRATILOVA CALLS ICE 'EVIL THUGS' AMID DOZENS OF SOCIAL MEDIA POSTS FOLLOWING MINNESOTA INCIDENT</strong></a></p><p>Noem said Good's actions against ICE officers leading up to the shooting amounted to an <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/noem-condemns-alleged-attack-ice-agents-stuck-snow-minneapolis-act-domestic-terrorism" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><u>"act of domestic terrorism."</u></a></p><p>"An officer of ours acted quickly and defensively, shot to protect himself and the people around him," she said.</p><p>Democrats have rushed to portray it as an example of unjust violence by the Trump administration.</p><p>Hillary Clinton posted on X Thursday that "last night, at the corner where an ICE agent murdered Renee Good, thousands of Minnesotans gathered in the frigid dark to protest her killing."</p><p>Minneapolis Mayor Jacob Frey told ICE to "get the f--- out of Minneapolis" during a Wednesday press conference, a sentiment that was echoed by Rep. Ilhan Omar, D-Minn., who also posted to the Department of Homeland Security, "Get out of our city."</p><p>The Minnesota Timberwolves <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports/timberwolves-hold-moment-silence-minnesota-woman-killed-ice-shooting" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><u>held a moment of silence</u></a> for Good before their game on Thursday night. During the moment of silence, one fan in attendance yelled, "Go home, ICE." Another yelled, "F--- ICE," and cheers erupted.</p><p><i>Fox News' Peter Pinedo contributed to this report.</i></p><p><i>Follow Fox News Digital’s </i><a href="https://twitter.com/FoxNewsSports_" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><i><u>sports coverage on X</u></i></a><i>, and subscribe to </i><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/newsletters" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><i><u>the Fox News Sports Huddle newsletter</u></i></a><i>.</i></p>
