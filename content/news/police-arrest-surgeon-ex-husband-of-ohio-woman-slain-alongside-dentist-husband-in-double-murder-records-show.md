<p><strong>FIRST ON FOX:</strong> The ex-husband of an <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/midwest/ohio" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Ohio dentist's</a> wife killed in a double murder was arrested on Saturday in the Chicago area, according to court records.</p><p>Michael David McKee, 39, was arrested, police records show.</p><p>Columbus, Ohio, officials said police responded to a Weinland Park home around 10 a.m. on Dec. 30, and found Spencer Tepe, 37 and <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/ohio-police-hunt-killer-after-dentist-wife-found-shot-death-children-safe-inside-home-timeline" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><u>Monique Tepe</u></a>, 39, both dead with gunshot wounds. McKee is Monique's ex-husband. </p><p>McKee was booked at the Winnebago County Sheriff's Office in Illinois just before noon on Saturday, records show. He’s being charged with two counts of murder in Ohio.</p><p>Detectives in Columbus believe the murders took place between 2 a.m. and 5 a.m. on Dec. 30. Both of the couple's children were found safe inside the home when officers arrived. Police didn't find signs of forced entry and a weapon wasn't recovered at the scene, officials said.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/911-call-near-slain-ohio-dentists-home-reported-door-banging-days-before-he-wife-were-found-shot-dead" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>911 CALL NEAR SLAIN OHIO DENTIST’S HOME REPORTED DOOR 'BANGING' DAYS BEFORE HE AND WIFE WERE FOUND SHOT DEAD</strong></a></p><p>According to charging documents, police were able to identify McKee by linking him to a car that arrived in the Tepes' neighborhood shortly before the murders, and left just after the homicide. The car was then located in Rockford, Illinois, and police found evidence it belonged to McKee.</p><p>The couple was killed just one month short of their five-year wedding anniversary, Rob Misleh, Spencer's brother-in-law, told <a href="https://abc6onyourside.com/news/local/no-suspect-as-police-investigate-murder-of-dentist-wife-in-their-weinland-park-home-north-fourth-street-columbus-crime-murder-homicide-cpd" target="_blank" rel="noopener">WSYX.</a></p><p>Court records show McKee’s divorce with Monique was finalized in 2017. He has lived in various states, including Virginia, Nevada and Illinois since 2020, according to public records. McKee and Monique got married in 2015. </p><p>McKee is a vascular surgeon in the Rockford, Illinois, area. </p><p>READ THE CHARGING DOCUMENTS:</p><p>Police on Monday <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/chilling-surveillance-video-shows-person-interest-unsolved-ohio-dentist-murder-case" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><u>released surveillance video showing</u></a> a "person of interest" walking in the alley near the Tepes' residence in the early morning hours of Dec. 30.</p><p>The video, released by the Columbus Division of Police, shows the man walking slowly in an alley near the Weinland Park home where Spencer and Monique lived. He's seen in what appears to be a dark coat and light-colored pants.</p><p>At 10:03 a.m. on Dec. 30, one of the Tepe's friends went to the couple's house and <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/frantic-911-calls-detail-moments-before-columbus-dentist-wife-found-dead-theres-body" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><u>told a 911 operator</u></a> he could see a body inside.</p><p>"There's a body," the person can be heard saying. "Our friend wasn't answering his phone. We just did a wellness check. We just came here and he appears dead. He's laying next to his bed, off of his bed in this blood. I can't get closer to see more than that."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/frantic-911-calls-detail-moments-before-columbus-dentist-wife-found-dead-theres-body" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>FRANTIC 911 CALLS DETAIL MOMENTS BEFORE COLUMBUS DENTIST AND WIFE FOUND DEAD: 'THERE'S A BODY'</u></strong></a></p><p>The couple's family said in a statement they are "heartbroken beyond words," and called the deaths "tragic and senseless."</p><p>"They were extraordinary people whose lives were filled with love, joy, and deep connection to others," read the statement.
