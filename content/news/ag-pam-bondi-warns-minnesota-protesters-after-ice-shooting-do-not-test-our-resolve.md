<p>U.S. Attorney General <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/pam-bondi" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Pam Bondi</a> warned protesters in Minnesota Thursday that while peaceful demonstrations are protected under the First Amendment, obstructing or attacking federal law enforcement will result in arrests and prosecution. </p><p>"Obstructing, impeding, or attacking federal law enforcement is a federal crime. So is damaging federal property," Bondi wrote on X.</p><p>"If you cross that red line, you will be arrested and prosecuted. Do not test our resolve."</p><p>The threat comes after dozens of people gathered in Minneapolis to <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/minneapolis-protesters-confront-federal-agents-day-after-deadly-shooting" target="_blank" rel="noopener">protest the death of</a> 37-year-old Renee Nicole Good, who was shot by an Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) officer.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/photos-released-renee-nicole-good-us-citizen-killed-ice-minnesota" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>PHOTOS RELEASED OF RENEE NICOLE GOOD, THE US CITIZEN KILLED BY ICE IN MINNESOTA</strong></a></p><p>Video from KMSP showed a large group of people standing together in a circle around what appeared to be a makeshift memorial in the snow.</p><p>Video from earlier Thursday morning painted a different picture as some people faced off with federal law enforcement agents outside the Bishop Henry Whipple Federal Building.</p><p>Fox News captured footage of officers pushing people back and appearing to take several individuals into custody.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/noem-condemns-alleged-attack-ice-agents-stuck-snow-minneapolis-act-domestic-terrorism" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>NOEM CONDEMNS ALLEGED ATTACK ON ICE AGENTS STUCK IN SNOW IN MINNEAPOLIS AS 'ACT OF DOMESTIC TERRORISM'</strong></a></p><p>The Department of Homeland Security (DHS) did not immediately respond to Fox News Digital’s request for an update on the number of arrests in Minnesota.</p><p>One man was seen in the crowd carrying an American flag upside down while others stood with signs.</p><p>"Rest in Power Renee," one handwritten sign placed among flowers and candles said. Other signs said "ICE Out!" and "You Can’t Shoot Us All."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/walz-prepares-national-guard-woman-killed-ice-operation-never-been-war-federal-government" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>WALZ PREPARES NATIONAL GUARD AFTER WOMAN KILLED IN ICE OPERATION: 'NEVER BEEN AT WAR' WITH FEDERAL GOVERNMENT</strong></a></p><p>The shooting of Good, a U.S. citizen, has <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/democrats-look-defunding-ice-impeaching-sec-noem-after-minneapolis-shooting" target="_blank" rel="noopener">intensified divisions on Capitol</a> Hill, as lawmakers clash over the Trump administration’s immigration policy and ICE enforcement operations.</p><p>DHS Secretary Kristi Noem said Good was trying to "weaponize her vehicle" and "attempted to run a law enforcement officer over" before she was shot. </p><p>Minnesota officials, including Minneapolis Mayor Jacob Frey and Gov. Tim Walz, <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/mayor-jacob-frey-tells-ice-get-f-out-minneapolis-rejects-dhs-self-defense-statement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">condemned the shooting</a> and publicly disputed the government’s version of events.</p><p>"The state will ensure there is a full, fair and expeditious investigation to ensure accountability and justice," Walz said.</p><p>Minneapolis Public Schools said on its website that it was canceling classes on Jan. 8–9 out of an abundance of caution due to safety concerns.</p>
