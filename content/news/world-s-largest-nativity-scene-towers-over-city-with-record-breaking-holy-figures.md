<p>The world’s largest nativity scene is welcoming visitors back this year <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/travel/regions/europe" target="_blank" rel="noopener">in Alicante, Spain</a>, where the towering display has once again taken over Town Hall Square in the city center.</p><p>First built in 2020, the oversized figures earned a Guinness World Records (GWR) for being the tallest nativity set in the world.</p><p>The Giant Nativity Scene, as it is known, has become a must-see holiday attraction for <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/travel" target="_blank" rel="noopener">locals and tourists alike</a>.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/travel/birthplace-jesus-resume-christmas-celebrations-after-war-forced-two-year-pause" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>BIRTHPLACE OF JESUS TO RESUME CHRISTMAS CELEBRATIONS AFTER WAR FORCED A TWO-YEAR PAUSE</u></strong></a></p><p>The massive scene features the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/religion" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Holy Family</a> — Jesus, Mary and Joseph — along with the three wise men. </p><p>Baby Jesus, shown lying in his manger, measures 10 feet, 10.7 inches high and 10 feet, 10.3 inches wide, while stretching more than 13 feet in length, according to GWR.</p><p>Joseph towers over <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/lifestyle/occasions/christmas" target="_blank" rel="noopener">the display</a> at 59 feet, 2.6 inches tall and about 15 feet wide.</p><p>Mary, the mother of God, measures 34 feet, 8.9 inches high and 11 feet, 5.4 inches wide.</p><p>"It's beautiful," wrote a Facebook user about the scene. "It's time we reconnect with the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/religion" target="_blank" rel="noopener">true meaning</a> of Christmas."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/newsletters?cmpid=fnfirstnl" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE TO SIGN UP FOR OUR LIFESTYLE NEWSLETTER</strong></a></p><p>The Giant Nativity Scene officially opened for the season on Nov. 23, inviting families to stop by for photos and holiday strolls.</p><p>Fox News Digital reached out to the tourism board in Alicante for comment and more details.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/lifestyle" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE FOR MORE LIFESTYLE STORIES</strong></a></p><p>Meanwhile, <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/lifestyle/occasions/christmas" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow">Christmas cheer</a> has returned to the historic city of Bethlehem in the West Bank — after a two-year pause due to the war in Gaza.</p><p>Located in the center of Bethlehem, Manger Square is named for the manger in which Jesus was laid after his birth.</p><p>Cardinal Pierbattista Pizzaballa, who has served as the Latin patriarch of Jerusalem since 2020, kicked off the celebration during a traditional procession from Jerusalem to Bethlehem, calling for <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/faith-values" target="_blank" rel="noopener">"a Christmas full of light,"</a> as The Associated Press reported.</p><p>"We, all together — we decide to be the light, and the light of Bethlehem is the light of the world," he told the assembled crowd. </p>
