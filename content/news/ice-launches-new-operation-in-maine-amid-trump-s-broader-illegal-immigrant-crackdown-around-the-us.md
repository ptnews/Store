<p>U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) made dozens of arrests in <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/northeast/maine" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Maine</a> on Tuesday amid an effort known as Operation Catch of the Day.</p><p>Fox News' Griff Jenkins reported on Wednesday that <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/immigration/enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">authorities had arrested</a> more than 50 people on Tuesday, and had informed him more arrests were taking place on Wednesday.</p><p>"We have approximately 1,400 targets here in Maine," ICE Deputy Assistant Director Patricia Hyde said on Tuesday, going on to mention crimes such as "rape of a child, drug trafficking" as well as "sexual assaults, simple assaults" and "driving while under the influence."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/minnesota-police-chiefs-allege-some-ice-agents-racially-profiled-us-citizens-including-off-duty-officers" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>MINNESOTA POLICE CHIEFS ALLEGE SOME ICE AGENTS RACIALLY PROFILED US CITIZENS, INCLUDING OFF-DUTY OFFICERS</strong></a></p><p>"You name it," she said. "They're on the target list."</p><p>The effort in Maine comes amid President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Donald Trump's</a> administration's broader crackdown against illegal immigrants around the U.S., a key campaign pledge.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-admin-argues-judge-limiting-ice-minnesota-would-unprecedented-overreach" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TRUMP ADMIN ARGUES JUDGE LIMITING ICE IN MINNESOTA WOULD BE ‘UNPRECEDENTED’ OVERREACH</strong></a></p><p>Portland Mayor Mark Dion issued a statement last week expressing opposition to the prospect of ICE in the area.</p><p>"Our community is anxious and fearful regarding the understanding that ICE is planning to send agents to Portland and Lewiston next week. We are a welcoming city. There is no evidence of unchecked criminal activity in our community requiring a disproportionate presence of federal agents. In that view, Portland rejects the need for the deployment of ICE agents into our neighborhoods," Dion said in part of the statement last week.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/video/6388008719112" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>US CITIZENS ARE ONLY ARRESTED BY ICE IF THEY VIOLATE A STATUTE ICE ENFORCES: TOM HOMAN</strong></a></p><p>"As a reminder, Portland Police does not cooperate with ICE, and they do not participate in enforcing federal immigration law," he noted.</p>
