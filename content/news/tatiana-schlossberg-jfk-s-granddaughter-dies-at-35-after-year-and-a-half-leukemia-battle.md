<p>Tatiana Schlossberg, the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/climate-change" target="_blank" rel="noopener">environmental journalist</a> and author who was the granddaughter of former President John F. Kennedy, has died at the age of 35, her family announced.</p><p>"Our beautiful Tatiana passed away this morning. She will always be in our hearts," <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/entertainment/genres/family" target="_blank" rel="noopener">the family</a> said in a statement shared via the JFK Library Foundation’s Instagram account Tuesday.</p><p>Born and raised in <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/health/infectious-disease/foodborne-illness" target="_blank" rel="noopener">New York City</a>, Schlossberg was the daughter of Caroline Kennedy and the granddaughter of Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis and JFK.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/parkland-survivor-jumps-crowded-nyc-house-race-gen-z-progressives-challenge-party-elders" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>PARKLAND SURVIVOR JUMPS INTO CROWDED NYC HOUSE RACE AS GEN Z PROGRESSIVES CHALLENGE PARTY ELDERS</strong></a></p><p>She built a career as a voice on climate and environmental issues after earning a bachelor's in history from <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/science/archaeology/history" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Yale University</a> and a master’s degree in American history from the University of Oxford.</p><p>In 2024, Schlossberg was <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/health/aggressive-cancer-warning-signs-revealed-after-jfks-granddaughters-diagnosis" target="_blank" rel="noopener">diagnosed with acute myeloid leukemia</a> and shared her experience publicly in a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/jfks-granddaughter-tatiana-schlossberg-reveals-terminal-cancer-diagnosis-heartbreaking-essay" target="_blank" rel="noopener">personal essay for</a> The New Yorker in November 2025.</p><p>"My parents and my brother and sister, too, have been raising my children and sitting in my various hospital rooms almost every day for the last year and a half," she <a href="https://www.newyorker.com/culture/the-weekend-essay/a-battle-with-my-blood" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">wrote at the time</a>.</p><p>Schlossberg also explained how <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/health/health-care" target="_blank" rel="noopener">doctors discovered her disease</a> while she was hospitalized after giving birth to her second child, a daughter.</p><p>She explained in her essay how doctors spotted that her white-blood-cell count "looked strange."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/white-house-hits-back-after-jfks-grandson-calls-rfk-jr-rabid-dog" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>WHITE HOUSE HITS BACK AFTER JFK’S GRANDSON CALLS RFK JR A ‘RABID DOG’</strong></a></p><p>She and her husband, George Moran, who she had married in 2017, also had a son.</p><p>After hearing from a doctor that she had "<a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/jfks-granddaughter-tatiana-schlossberg-reveals-terminal-cancer-diagnosis-heartbreaking-essay" target="_blank" rel="noopener">a year, maybe</a>" to live, Schlossberg told how her first thought was that "my kids, whose faces live permanently on the inside of my eyelids, wouldn’t remember me."</p><p>In her essay she also predicted that her son "might have a few memories, but he’ll probably start confusing them with pictures he sees or stories he hears."</p><p>At the time, Schlossberg said she had experienced a postpartum hemorrhage that nearly claimed her Kennedy Jr., in a plane crash in 1999.</p><p>Before her diagnosis, Shlossberg had been planning a research project focused on <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/science/planet-earth/oceans" target="_blank" rel="noopener">ocean conservation</a>.</p><p><i>This is a developing story.
