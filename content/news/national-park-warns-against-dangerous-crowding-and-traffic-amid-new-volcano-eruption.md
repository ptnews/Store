<p>Hawaii's Kilauea volcano erupted again this week — drawing renewed attention to Hawaiʻi Volcanoes National Park and fresh safety warnings. </p><p>The Jan. 12th event marks the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/science/planet-earth/geology" target="_blank" rel="noopener">volcano's 40th eruptive episode</a> since 2024, according to the U.S. Geological Survey (USGS).</p><p>In a Facebook post announcing the eruption, park officials cautioned that increased interest in the activity could lead to heavy traffic and busy conditions <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/travel/general/national-parks" target="_blank" rel="noopener">near the park</a>.</p><p><a href="https://https://www.foxnews.com/travel/researchers-find-compelling-evidence-possible-noahs-ark-remains-country" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>RESEARCHERS FIND 'COMPELLING EVIDENCE' OF POSSIBLE NOAH'S ARK REMAINS IN ONE COUNTRY</strong></a></p><p>Officials urged visitors to plan ahead, slow down, follow posted signs — and park only in designated areas.</p><p>The current eruption inside the Halemaʻumaʻu crater can be viewed from several overlooks along Crater Rim Drive, though visibility can change quickly and weather such as fog or rain may obscure the lava, the park’s website noted.</p><p>"Consider factors such as viewing experience, long waits and crowds, hiking ability and available time when selecting where to view the eruption," the park said.</p><p><a
