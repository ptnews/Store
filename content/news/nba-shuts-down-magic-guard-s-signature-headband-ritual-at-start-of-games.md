<p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/nba/orlando-magic" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Orlando Magic</a> guard Jalen Suggs always began games with his headband around his neck and would move it to his head once he felt the flow of the game.</p><p>The NBA reached out to Suggs and told the 24-year-old he could no longer wear his headband in his unique way, and must wear the headband on his head at tip-off, according to the <a href="https://x.com/MagicFilmRoom/status/2007262840286261404?s=20" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">Orlando Magic’s broadcast.</a></p><p>The five-year <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/nba" target="_blank" rel="noopener">NBA</a> veteran said he began wearing his headband on his neck once and stuck with it.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE FOR MORE SPORTS COVERAGE ON FOXNEWS.COM</strong></a></p><p>"I wear it on my neck, and once I feel into the game, into the flow, I put it on my head, and we rock," Suggs said earlier this season, according to <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/nba/magic/2026/01/03/jalen-suggs-headband-orlando-magic/88007401007/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">USA Today.</a> "That’s just me being J-Suggs.</p><p>"Really, it originates as football drip — that’s where it stems from.
