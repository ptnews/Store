<p>A 12-year-old <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/world-regions/australia" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Australian boy</a> died after a brutal shark attack in Sydney Harbor, his family confirmed Saturday. </p><p>Nico Antic had been <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/australian-boy-fighting-life-after-shark-attack-rescued-friends-sydney-harbor-waters" target="_blank" rel="noopener">fighting for his life</a> since Jan. 18, when he was attacked while swimming at a popular cliff-jumping spot known as Jump Rock near Shark Beach in the eastern Sydney suburb of Vaucluse. </p><p>His family announced Saturday that he had died.</p><p>"We are heartbroken to share that our son, Nico, has passed away," Nico’s parents, Lorena and Juan, said in a statement. "Nico was a happy, friendly, and sporty young boy with the most kind and generous spirit. He was always full of life and that’s how we’ll remember him."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/travel/northern-california-surfer-says-shark-attack-felt-like-being-hit-car-board-snaps-half" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>SURFER SAYS SHARK ATTACK FELT 'LIKE BEING HIT BY A CAR' AS BOARD BITTEN IN HALF: REPORTS</strong></a></p><p>They also thanked first responders and medical staff at Sydney Children’s Hospital in Randwick for their efforts and members of the community for their support.</p><p>A GoFundMe campaign launched by a family friend had raised more than $266,000 as of Saturday evening.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/fourth-shark-attack-three-days-rocks-australia-authorities-close-beaches-deploy-drumlines" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>FOURTH SHARK ATTACK IN THREE DAYS ROCKS AUSTRALIA AS AUTHORITIES CLOSE BEACHES AND DEPLOY DRUMLINES</strong></a></p><p>According to police, the attack happened around 4:20 p.m. Jan. 18, after Antic jumped from a 20-foot ledge into the water. He sustained severe injuries to both legs, believed to have been caused by a large shark.</p><p>His three friends immediately jumped into the water, pulled him to shore and called for help. Antic was rushed to the hospital in critical condition. </p><p>Local media reported at the time that he had lost both legs.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/travel/northern-california-surfer-says-shark-attack-felt-like-being-hit-car-board-snaps-half" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>SURFER SAYS SHARK ATTACK FELT "LIKE BEING HIT BY A CAR" AS BOARD BITTEN IN HALF: REPORTS</strong></a></p><p>The tragedy is among a surge of shark incidents across Australia. Dozens of beaches were closed this week after four shark attacks in mere days.</p><p>Australia averages roughly 20 shark attacks a year, with fewer than three proving fatal, according to Reuters.</p><p><i>Fox News Digital's Stephen Sorace contributed to this report.</i></p>
