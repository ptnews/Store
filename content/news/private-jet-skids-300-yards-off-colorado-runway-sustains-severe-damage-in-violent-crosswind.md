<p>A private business jet carrying three people onboard was violently thrown off a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/west/colorado" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Colorado runway</a> Tuesday after a sudden, brutal gust of wind slammed the side of the plane as it landed, officials announced.</p><p>The San Miguel County Sheriff’s Office said the plane, which had two pilots and one passenger onboard, skidded roughly "300 yards off the runway" and sustained substantial damage in multiple areas of the aircraft.</p><p>The jet lost directional control around 12:15 p.m. at Telluride Regional Airport in western Colorado, the sheriff's office announced later in the afternoon.</p><p>"The pilot told deputies that just after the plane's wheels touched down that a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/travel/general/travel-safety" target="_blank" rel="noopener">strong, gusting-crosswind</a> hit the plane's profile, shoving it off the runway," the authorities said in a post on Facebook. "The plane skidded approximately 300 yards coming to rest off the runway on its collapsed landing gear."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/all-8-tires-burst-harrowing-atlanta-landing-failure-involving-passenger-jet" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>PASSENGERS BAFFLED AND CONFUSED AFTER SCREAMS BURST FROM BENEATH TAXIING AIR CANADA PLANE</strong></a></p><p>The Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) noted Wednesday that the midsize Cessna 750, which is primarily used for long-range business or corporate travel, sustained substantial damage, beginning with the collapse of its left landing gear upon touchdown. The sheriff’s office added that the nose wheel and one of the plane's wings were also damaged during the slide. </p><p>Photos provided by the sheriff's office show the Cessna off a paved runway, resting on snow-dusted grass at what appears to be a rural mountain airport. One wing appears to be touching the ground, suggesting possible landing gear damage or collapse.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/cause-failed-alaska-airlines-landing-gear-sent-passengers-screaming-revealed" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>CAUSE OF FAILED ALASKA AIRLINES LANDING GEAR THAT SENT PASSENGERS SCREAMING REVEALED</u></strong></a></p><p>No injuries were reported after the incident, and Telluride fire crews responded to examine the passenger and crew members, according to authorities. </p><p>"All occupants were able to self-extricate, and there are no reported injuries," the sheriff's office said.</p><p>Officials said there was no fire or smoke, but the airport was closed after the crash.</p><p>According to the FAA’s aircraft registry, the Cessna is owned by Mach .92 Leasing LLC out of <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/southeast/north-carolina" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Raleigh, North Carolina</a>.</p><p>Federal agents from the National Transportation Safety Board and the FAA will be investigating.</p>
