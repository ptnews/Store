<p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/ncaa/vanderbilt-commodores" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Vanderbilt Commodores</a> quarterback Diego Pavia came up short in his pursuit of a ReliaQuest Bowl win over the Iowa Hawkeyes on Wednesday afternoon.</p><p>Iowa won the game, 34-27.</p><p>Pavia was 25-of-38 for 347 yards with three total touchdowns – two passing and one rushing. The Heisman Trophy finalist had an 11-yard touchdown run to cut the deficit to seven points. Iowa and Vanderbilt then traded field goals, but the Commodores couldn’t get the ball back.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE FOR MORE SPORTS COVERAGE ON FOXNEWS.COM</strong></a></p><p>The loss to end Vanderbilt’s season comes as Pavia fights the NCAA over its eligibility rules. Although he may end up entering the NFL Draft, the lawsuit is continuing in hopes of helping other junior college players. The lawsuit challenges the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/ncaa" target="_blank" rel="noopener">NCAA rule</a> that counts seasons spent at junior colleges against players’ eligibility for Division I football.</p><p>Last week, a lawyer for Pavia and 26 other football players cited the NCAA’s decision to allow an NBA draft pick to return to college basketball as a reason that a federal judge should let his clients play in 2026 and 2027.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports/oregons-dan-lanning-calls-college-football-season-end-jan-1-every-single-year" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>OREGON'S DAN LANNING CALLS FOR COLLEGE FOOTBALL SEASON TO END BY JAN 1 EVERY YEAR</strong></a></p><p>Pavia initially sued the NCAA in November 2024 and won a preliminary injunction weeks later that allowed him to play this season. He was the SEC Player of the Year this season with 3,192 passing yards and 27 touchdown passes in the regular season. The Commodores finished 10-3 after the loss to Iowa.</p><p>The quarterback’s lawsuit has since added 26 other plaintiffs, including <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/ncaa/tennessee-volunteers" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Tennessee Volunteers</a> quarterback Joey Aguilar.</p><p>NCAA rules give athletes five years to play four seasons under an eligibility clock that begins at any "collegiate institution" regardless of whether the school is an NCAA member.</p><p>Pavia played at New Mexico Military Institute in 2020, which wasn’t counted toward eligibility because of the COVID-19 pandemic. The led the school to a junior college national championship before he played at <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/ncaa/new-mexico-state-aggies" target="_blank" rel="noopener">New Mexico State</a> in 2022 and 2023 and later Vanderbilt.</p><p><i>The Associated Press contributed to this report.</i></p><p><i><strong>Follow Fox News Digital’s</strong></i><a href="https://twitter.com/FoxNewsSports_" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><i><strong> <u>sports coverage on X</u></strong></i></a><i><strong> and subscribe to</strong></i><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/newsletters" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><i><strong><u> the Fox News Sports Huddle newsletter</u></strong></i></a><i><strong>.</strong></i></p>
