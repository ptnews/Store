<p>Newly released FBI records connected to the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/nashville" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Covenant School shooting in Nashville</a> include writings made by shooter Audrey Elizabeth Hale that shed further light on her motivations, planning and personal finances.</p><p>The FBI released more than 100 pages of Hale’s writings following litigation, which included journal entries believed to date back to late 2021, handwritten notes outlining preparations for a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/mass-shootings" target="_blank" rel="noopener">school shooting</a> and references to weapons Hale intended to acquire.</p><p>Some of the writings list "Christian school (hate religion)" as a reason for targeting the Covenant School.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/georgia-high-school-shooting-suspect-looks-dramatically-different-court" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>GEORGIA HIGH SCHOOL SHOOTING SUSPECT LOOKS DRAMATICALLY DIFFERENT IN COURT</strong></a></p><p>Hale, 28, carried out the March 27, 2023, attack at the Christian elementary school she once attended, killing six people before being shot dead by responding Metro Nashville Police Department officers.</p><p>The victims were identified as school staff members Katherine Koonce, 60; Cynthia Peak, 61; and Mike Hill, 61; along with students Hallie Scruggs, Evelyn Dieckhaus and William Kinney, all 9.</p><p>Surveillance footage released by police following the attack showed Hale moving through the school armed with multiple firearms.</p><p>Authorities have said Hale entered the building through a side entrance and moved through several areas of the school before being confronted by officers.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/indiana-trans-student-admits-plotting-valentines-day-school-shooting-kept-shrine-mass-killers" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>INDIANA TRANS STUDENT ADMITS TO PLOTTING VALENTINE’S DAY SCHOOL SHOOTING, KEPT ‘SHRINE’ TO MASS KILLERS</strong></a></p><p>Shortly before the shooting, Hale sent a text message to a friend describing the planned attack as a "suicide mission" and stating the friend would likely "hear about me on the news after I die," according to summaries released by officials.</p><p>Among the<a href="https://vault.fbi.gov/nashville-covenant-school-shooting/nashville-covenant-school-shooting-part-06/view" target="_blank" rel="noopener"> newly released FBI</a> materials is a handwritten page seen by Fox News Digital and labeled "Account Savings Record," and referenced <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/education" target="_blank" rel="noopener">federal student financial aid</a>.</p><p>In the entry, Hale wrote that "FAFSA [sic] grant checks started at $2,050.86," followed by ledger-style notes documenting payments from Nossi College of Art and Design in Nashville, where Hale was enrolled at the time.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/university-delaware-student-allegedly-mapped-out-attack-campus-police-illegal-weapons-recovered" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>UNIVERSITY OF DELAWARE STUDENT ALLEGEDLY MAPPED OUT ATTACK ON CAMPUS POLICE; ILLEGAL WEAPONS RECOVERED</strong></a></p><p>The financial entries appear alongside extensive notes about <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/personal-freedoms/second-amendment" target="_blank" rel="noopener">firearms Hale planned to purchase</a> and use in the attack.</p><p>The Tennessee Star also reported those records may lend support to statements Hale’s parents made to Metro Nashville Police Department detectives shortly after the shooting.</p><p>Hale’s parents reportedly told investigators in 2023 that their child used <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/education/dept-of-education" target="_blank" rel="noopener">federal Pell Grant money</a> to purchase the firearms used in the attack.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/online-gore-forums-gateway-extremism-mass-shootings-normalizing-horror-kids-experts" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>ONLINE ‘GORE’ FORUMS ARE ‘GATEWAY TO EXTREMISM’ IN MASS SHOOTINGS, NORMALIZING HORROR FOR KIDS: EXPERTS</strong></a></p><p>Hale’s mother also reportedly told police that because Hale was over 25 and enrolled as a student, parental income no longer factored into financial aid eligibility, allowing Hale to qualify for grant funding despite being unemployed.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/police-and-law-enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Metro Nashville Police Chief John Drake</a> previously confirmed that investigators recovered a manifesto and hand-drawn maps from Hale’s vehicle after the shooting.</p><p>While portions of Hale’s writings have since been released, both city police and <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/tech/topics/fbi" target="_blank" rel="noopener">the FBI</a> have continued to resist public records requests for the full manifesto.</p>
