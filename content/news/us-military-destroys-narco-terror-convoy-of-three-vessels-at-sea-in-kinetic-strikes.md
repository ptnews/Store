<p>The <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/military" target="_blank" rel="noopener">U.S. military</a> carried out overnight kinetic strikes against a convoy of three vessels operated by <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/terror/counter-terrorism" target="_blank" rel="noopener">designated terrorist organizations</a> involved in narcotics trafficking, U.S. Southern Command said Wednesday.</p><p>According to Southern Command, the operation was conducted by Joint Task Force Southern Spear at the direction of Secretary of War <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/pete-hegseth" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Pete Hegseth</a>, using the title cited by the command.</p><p>The vessels were transiting known <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/drugs" target="_blank" rel="noopener">narcotics trafficking routes</a> in international waters when U.S. forces took action after intelligence confirmed the transfer of drugs between the boats, Southern Command said.</p><p>Three individuals aboard the first vessel were killed during the initial engagement, the command said.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/pentagon-wont-release-top-secret-full-unedited-video-september-drug-boat-strike-hegseth-says" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>PENTAGON WON'T RELEASE 'TOP SECRET, FULL, UNEDITED' VIDEO OF SEPTEMBER DRUG BOAT STRIKE, HEGSETH SAYS</strong></a></p><p>The remaining individuals aboard the other two vessels abandoned ship before follow-on strikes were conducted, resulting in both boats being sunk.</p><p>Southern Command said the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/military/coast-guard" target="_blank" rel="noopener">U.S. Coast Guard</a> was immediately notified following the strikes so the search and rescue system could be activated for individuals who entered the water.</p><p>The vessels were believed to be part of a coordinated narcotics trafficking operation tied to designated terrorist organizations and were operating outside U.S. territorial waters, the command said.</p><p><a oversees military operations</a> in Central and South America and <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/travel/regions/caribbean" target="_blank" rel="noopener">the Caribbean</a>, including counter-narcotics missions aimed at disrupting drug trafficking networks that pose a threat to American interests.</p><p>Fox News Digital reached out to U.S. Southern Command for additional details
