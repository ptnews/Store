<p><strong>FIRST ON FOX:</strong> Federal authorities arrested a man accused of breaking a window of a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/judiciary/federal-courts" target="_blank" rel="noopener">federal courthouse</a> in Minneapolis on Wednesday.</p><p>The FBI identified the suspect as Georgio James-Jones, saying he had attempted to breach the courthouse during a protest on Jan. 7. </p><p>FBI Director <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/kash-patel" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Kash Patel</a> highlighted James-Jones' arrest in a statement to Fox News Digital.</p><p>"This morning the FBI and partners arrested Georgio James-Jones – the individual who allegedly smashed windows attempting to breach a federal courthouse in Minneapolis last week. As the FBI has said repeatedly – if you attack law enforcement, impede their operations, or engage in violent, destructive behavior, this FBI will find you and bring you to justice," Patel said.</p><p>James-Jones was one of dozens of anti-ICE rioters who were seen banging on the doors of the Diana E. Murphy in Minneapolis.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/minnesota-anti-ice-agitators-swarm-confront-federal-agents-during-enforcement-operations" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>MINNESOTA ANTI-ICE AGITATORS SWARM, CONFRONT FEDERAL AGENTS DURING ENFORCEMENT OPERATIONS</strong></a></p><p>The protest was part of a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-protests" target="_blank" rel="noopener">nationwide streak of unrest</a> in the wake of the death of Renee Nicole Good, who was shot and killed by an Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) officer in Minneapolis last week.</p><p>Another protest in California on Tuesday saw agitators burning an American flag and shouting expletives through a megaphone.</p><p>The Department of Homeland Security (DHS) said <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/police-and-law-enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">two officers were injured</a> in a clash with the protesters. One of the demonstrators was also struck in the eye with a crowd-control munition, leaving the eye blind, according to his family.</p><p>The <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/justice-department" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Justice Department</a> has surged federal officers and prosecutors to Minneapolis this week, focusing primarily on fraud and immigration cases.</p><p>City officials said residents have raised concerns about neighborhood access, with multiple 311 calls requesting barrier removal. While memorials created by community members will remain intact, the city said surrounding streets must stay clear to ensure emergency access.</p>
