<p>Venezuelan President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/topic/venezuelan-political-crisis" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Nicolás Maduro</a> said Thursday that his government is open to negotiating an agreement with the United States after months of American military pressure targeting drug trafficking networks tied to his government.</p><p>In a pre-taped interview with Spanish journalist Ignacio Ramonet that aired on state television, Maduro said Venezuela is "ready" to discuss a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/drugs" target="_blank" rel="noopener">drug-trafficking deal</a> with the U.S. He called on the countries to "start talking seriously, with data in hand."</p><p>"The U.S. government knows, because we’ve told many of their spokespeople, that if they want to seriously discuss an agreement to combat drug trafficking, we’re ready," he said. "If they want oil, Venezuela is ready for U.S. investment, like with Chevron, whenever they want it, wherever they want it and however they want it."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trumps-venezuela-oil-blockade-puts-chevron-middle-high-stakes-sanctions-crackdown" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Chevron Corp. is the only</a> major U.S. oil company currently exporting Venezuelan crude to the United States.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/maduro-trapped-few-retaliation-options-after-trump-admin-seizes-venezuelan-oil-tanker" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>MADURO TRAPPED WITH FEW RETALIATION OPTIONS AFTER TRUMP ADMIN SEIZES VENEZUELAN OIL TANKER</strong></a></p><p>Maduro said the U.S. wants a regime change in Venezuela and access to its oil reserves through a months-long pressure campaign that began with a major <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/defense" target="_blank" rel="noopener">military deployment to the Caribbean Sea</a><strong> </strong>in August.</p><p>He said it is clear the U.S. wants "to impose themselves through threats, intimidation and force."</p><p>The interview was recorded on New Year’s Eve, the same day the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/terror/bombings" target="_blank" rel="noopener">U.S. military announced a kinetic strike</a> that killed five people aboard two vessels operated by designated terrorist organizations involved in narcotics trafficking.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media/sen-kennedy-doubles-down-venezuela-crackdown-urges-sanctions-choke-off-funds" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>SEN. KENNEDY DOUBLES DOWN ON VENEZUELA CRACKDOWN, URGES SANCTIONS TO 'CHOKE OFF' FUNDS</strong></a></p><p>At least 114 people have been killed since the U.S. began bombing alleged drug-trafficking boats in the Caribbean and eastern Pacific in early September.</p><p>The U.S. may be signaling a possible expansion of its Venezuela-focused campaign, including potential ground operations.</p><p>President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Donald Trump</a> confirmed that a strike last week targeted what he described as a Venezuelan port used for drug trafficking, but declined to say whether the operation was carried out by the U.S. military or another entity, such as the CIA.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/southcom-says-8-narco-terrorists-killed-latest-eastern-pacific-lethal-kinetic-strikes" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>SOUTHCOM SAYS 8 NARCO-TERRORISTS KILLED IN LATEST EASTERN PACIFIC LETHAL KINETIC STRIKES</strong></a></p><p>Maduro declined to comment on <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/state-and-local/federal-relations" target="_blank" rel="noopener">that strike</a> during the interview, but said he could "talk about it in a few days."</p><p>In recent weeks, Trump has intensified pressure on Maduro, ordering a total blockade of oil tankers entering or leaving Venezuela, designating his regime a foreign terrorist organization, and accusing it of using stolen U.S. assets to fund terrorism, drug trafficking and other criminal activity.</p><p>U.S. authorities have also seized two ships carrying sanctioned oil.</p><p><i>Fox News Digital's Greg Wehner, Morgan Phillips, Jasmine Baehr and The Associated Press contributed to this report.</i></p>
