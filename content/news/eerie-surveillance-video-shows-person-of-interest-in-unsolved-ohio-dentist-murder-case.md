<p>Officials have released eerie surveillance footage capturing a ‘person of interest’ in the unsolved murders of an <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/midwest/ohio" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Ohio dentist and his wife</a>, who were gunned down inside their home.</p><p> Fox News Digital previously reported that a<a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/manhunt" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><u> manhunt is underway</u></a> for the perpetrator, with the police requesting video from any neighbors and individuals who may have been in the area.</p><p>The new footage, released Monday by the Columbus Police Department on <a href="https://www.instagram.com/reel/DTJYxwVD7R6/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">Instagram</a>, shows an individual walking slowly through a snowy alley near the Weinland Park home of Spencer Tepe, 37, and his wife, Monique Tepe, 39.</p><p>The person is seen in what looks like light-colored pants and a dark, hooded coat.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/son-suspected-killing-beloved-doctor-wife-before-torching-car-taking-his-own-life" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>SON SUSPECTED IN KILLING OF BELOVED DOCTOR AND WIFE BEFORE TORCHING CAR AND TAKING HIS OWN LIFE</strong></a></p><p>Their head is bowed down and their hands are tucked into their pockets as they walk in the early hours.</p><p>Detectives think <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/ohio-police-hunt-killer-after-dentist-wife-found-shot-death-children-safe-inside-home-timeline" target="_blank" rel="noopener">the couple was murdered</a> sometime between 2 a.m. and 5 a.m. on Dec. 30, inside the upstairs of their North 4th Street residence.</p><p>Police said the surveillance video places the person of interest in the alley near the Tepe home during that critical timeframe.</p><p>"Detectives believe the murder of Mr. and Mrs. Tepe occurred sometime between 2:00 and 5:00 a.m. on the morning of December 30th in the upstairs of their residence," police said in a statement online.</p><p>"Recovered video footage shows a person of interest walking in the alley near the Tepe’s residence during that timeframe.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/ohio-police-hunt-killer-after-dentist-wife-found-shot-death-children-safe-inside-home-timeline" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>OHIO POLICE HUNT KILLER AFTER DENTIST, WIFE FOUND SHOT TO DEATH WITH CHILDREN SAFE INSIDE HOME: TIMELINE</strong></a></p><p>"We have received and are continuing to receive numerous tips from the community, which our detectives are following up on," <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/police-and-law-enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">police said.</a></p><p>Spencer and Monique Tepe were found dead after a coworker became alarmed when Spencer failed to show up for work at Athens Dental Depot and did not respond to calls.</p><p>Police carried out a wellness check but left after no one answered the door.</p><p>Officers returned later when coworkers and a friend reported hearing children inside the home and seeing Spencer’s body near a bed.</p><p>The friend had called 911 at 9:57 a.m., telling the dispatcher that he "thought he heard one of [the kids] yelling," according to the call log obtained by Fox News Digital.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/arkansas-woman-children-found-dead-mansion-home-day-after-final-divorce-hearing-estranged-husband" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>ARKANSAS WOMAN AND CHILDREN FOUND DEAD AT MANSION HOME DAY AFTER FINAL DIVORCE HEARING WITH ESTRANGED HUSBAND</strong></a></p><p>The couple’s <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/health/healthy-living/childrens-health" target="_blank" rel="noopener">two young children</a> — a 1-year-old boy and a 4-year-old girl — were found unharmed, along with the family’s dog. The children have since been placed in the care of relatives.</p><p>Police said Spencer suffered <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/homicide" target="_blank" rel="noopener">multiple gunshot wounds</a>, while Monique was shot at least once in the chest.</p><p>No weapon was recovered at the scene, and authorities have ruled out a murder-suicide. There were also no signs of forced entry.</p><p>The Franklin County Coroner’s Office confirmed Monday to Fox News Digital that both deaths were caused by "apparent homicide by gunshot wounds."</p><p>The Tepes, married for five years, were remembered by family as devoted parents who built a life "rooted in love." </p><p>A <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/elections/fundraising" target="_blank" rel="noopener">GoFundMe created to support their children</a> has raised more than $146,000 as the investigation continues.</p>
