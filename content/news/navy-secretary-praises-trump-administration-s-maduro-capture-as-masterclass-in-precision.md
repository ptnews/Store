<p>The Trump administration's successful operation to capture Venezuelan dictator Nicolás Maduro was heralded as a "masterclass in precision" on Sunday, as <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/military/navy" target="_blank" rel="noopener">U.S. Navy Secretary</a> John Phelan spotlighted the might and capabilities of American forces.</p><p>"You don't want to mess with the United States military," Phelan said on "Sunday Night in America."</p><p>"In particular, what I see coming from the Department of the Navy, [it shows] the importance of naval ships, which is something the president really knows a lot about and has emphasized greatly, and it's something we're moving out on, which is building the best-in-class, best-in-breed ships, making sure that both the Marines and the sailors have the assets they need in order to move out on operations like this."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/venezuelan-leader-maduro-lands-new-york-after-being-captured-us-forces-drug-conspiracy-charges" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>VENEZUELAN LEADER MADURO LANDS IN NEW YORK AFTER BEING CAPTURED BY US FORCES ON DRUG CONSPIRACY CHARGES</strong></a></p><p>Phelan's praise comes on the heels of U.S. forces capturing Maduro on Saturday, a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/defense/joint-chiefs-of-staff" target="_blank" rel="noopener">joint-force effort</a> that drew <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media/gop-lawmaker-says-democrats-have-egg-face-after-trumps-capture-venezuelas-maduro" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><u>mixed reactions as Democrats</u></a> and Republicans debated legality concerns and what the future might hold for the South American country.</p><p>The operation followed months-long efforts to halt alleged <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/terrorism/narco-terror" target="_blank" rel="noopener">narco-terrorism schemes</a> tied to the country, including strikes on suspected drug boats in Caribbean waters.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/from-bus-driver-dictator-nicolas-maduros-rise-fall-venezuela" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>FROM BUS DRIVER TO DICTATOR: NICOLÁS MADURO’S RISE AND FALL IN VENEZUELA</u></strong></a></p><p>Phelan told Fox News the mission "couldn't have been more successful," emphasizing its message to U.S. adversaries that President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Donald Trump</a> is willing to take action when all else fails.</p><p>"He [Trump] is really a person of peace, and he gave Maduro countless numbers of opportunities to call it a day, and instead, unfortunately, he had to face the full might of the United States military," he said. </p><p>"I think any of our adversaries who saw that mission and understood the complexity, the coordination, and… [the] masterclass in precision and lightning execution… you don't want to mess with the United States military."</p><p>Maduro faces arraignment in federal court in New York City on Monday alongside his wife, Cilia Flores.</p>
