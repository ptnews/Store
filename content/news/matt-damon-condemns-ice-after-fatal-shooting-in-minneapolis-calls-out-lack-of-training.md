<p>Actor Matt Damon criticized U.S. <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/immigration/enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Immigrations and Customs Enforcement (ICE)</a> on Tuesday during an interview at the premiere of his new movie and said the agency lacked training.</p><p>Attending the Netflix premiere of "The Rip" — his new movie on the streaming platform — Damon wore a "Be Good" pin in protest of the fatal ICE shooting of Renee Good in <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/minneapolis-st-paul" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Minneapolis</a>.</p><p><a href="https://x.com/APEntertainment/status/2011529692898099474" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">In a video posted to X</a> by Associated Press Entertainment, Damon explained why he was wearing the pin.</p><p>"Yeah, I mean, I think that that incident was incredibly alarming," Damon said. "I think a lot of people are on edge and worried about this and paying very close attention and really worried about this type of behavior out in our streets and the lack of training that we’re reading about and hearing about with some of these people who are going out there."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/protesters-clash-federal-officers-after-another-ice-shooting-minneapolis" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>PROTESTERS CLASH WITH FEDERAL OFFICERS AFTER ANOTHER ICE SHOOTING IN MINNEAPOLIS</strong></a></p><p>"Clearly, you know, I’ve heard from a lot of friends in law enforcement that that was not the way they would have handled the situation. I think as concerned citizens, we need to be talking about this and talking about what kind of country we want to live in," Damon continued.</p><p>Fox News Digital has reached out to ICE for comment.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/entertainment/celebrity-anti-ice-pins-golden-globes-spark-mixed-reactions-online-after-renee-good-fatal-shooting" target="_blank" rel="noopener">The "Be Good" pins</a> were also seen on celebrities at the Golden Globes.</p><p>Stars like <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/mark-ruffalo" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><u>Mark Ruffalo</u></a>, Wanda Sykes, Jean Smart, Ariana Grande, Natasha Lyonne and others wore pins with slogans that read, "Be Good" and "ICE Out."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/new-video-shows-minutes-leading-up-deadly-minneapolis-ice-shooting" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>NEW VIDEO SHOWS MINUTES LEADING UP TO DEADLY MINNEAPOLIS ICE SHOOTING</strong></a></p><p><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/media" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE FOR MORE COVERAGE OF MEDIA AND CULTURE</strong></a></p><p>"Of course, this is for the mother who was murdered by an <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/immigration" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><u>ICE agent</u></a>, and it's really sad. I know people are out marching and all today, and we need to speak up," <a href="https://x.com/variety/status/2010488950238904576?s=46" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">Sykes told Variety</a> on the red carpet prior to the show. "We need to be out there and shut this rogue government down, because it's just awful what they're doing to people."</p><p>Good was shot and killed during an encounter with an ICE officer last week. Since then, protests have erupted in Minneapolis and across the U.S. Local officials, including Minneapolis Mayor Jacob Frey, have criticized the ICE officer's actions.</p><p><i>Fox News' Christina Dugan Ramirez contributed to this report.</i></p>
