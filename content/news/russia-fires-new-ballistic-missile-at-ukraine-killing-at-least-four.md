<p>Russia's Defense Ministry said it used a new Oreshnik intermediate-range ballistic missile Friday in a deadly strike on Ukraine.</p><p>Four people were killed and at least 22 were injured, according to Ukrainian authorities.</p><p>Russia said the attack was retaliation for what Moscow claimed was a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/putin-residence-attack-video-slammed-us-officials-say-ukraine-target-leader" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Ukrainian drone strike</a> on Russian President Vladimir Putin’s residence last month. Both Ukraine and President Donald Trump have disputed that claim.</p><p>The attack occurred hours after Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy alerted the nation that Russia intended to launch a large-scale offensive.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/zelenskyy-denies-russian-claim-ukraine-attacked-putin-residence" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>ZELENSKYY DENIES RUSSIAN CLAIM THAT UKRAINE ATTACKED PUTIN RESIDENCE</strong></a></p><p>Zelenskyy said that Russia was aiming to take advantage of the frigid weather in the capital, which made roads and streets icy.</p><p>The missile traveled at a speed of more than 8,000 miles per hour, according to the Western Command of Ukraine's Air Force.</p><p>Several districts in Kyiv were hit in the attack and an emergency medical aid worker was among those killed, according to Kyiv City Military Administration head Tymur Tkachenko.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/russia-alleges-attack-putin-residence-ukraine-denies-claim-ahead-trump-talks" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>RUSSIA ALLEGES ATTACK ON PUTIN RESIDENCE AS UKRAINE DENIES CLAIM AHEAD OF TRUMP TALKS</strong></a></p><p>Ukraine's security service said that five rescue workers suffered injuries while responding to the site of ongoing attacks.</p><p>A drone crashed onto the roof of a multistory building in the Desnyanskyi district and the first two floors of a residential building were also damaged as a result of the attack.</p><p>Running water and electricity were disrupted in parts of the capital, Kyiv's Mayor Vitali Klitschko said.</p><p>The latest attack also involved other ground-and-sea-launched missiles targeting Ukraine’s critical infrastructure the western city of Lviv, according to its mayor, Andriy Sadoviy.</p><p><i>The Associated Press contributed to this report.</i></p>
