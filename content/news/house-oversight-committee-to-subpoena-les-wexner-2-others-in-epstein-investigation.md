<p>The House Oversight Committee is moving to subpoena billionaire retail mogul Les Wexner, the longtime friend and financial patron of disgraced financier <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/jeffrey-epstein" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Jeffrey Epstein</a>, along with two former associates of the late convicted sex offender.</p><p>The move marks the latest phase of the panel’s long-running investigation into Epstein’s finances and the powerful figures who surrounded him.</p><p>"We made enourmous [sic] progress in our <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/finance/investigations" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Epstein investigation</a> today. We secured key subpoenas for billionaire benefactor Les Wexner, and the executors of the Epstein estate," ranking member Rep. Robert Garcia, D-Calif., said in a statement late Tuesday.</p><p>The panel also moved to subpoena Darren Indyke and Richard Kahn, Epstein’s longtime legal and financial advisors, according to <a href="https://www.politico.com/live-updates/2026/01/07/congress/more-epstein-subpoenas-00715597" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">Politico.</a></p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/doj-publishes-trove-epstein-files-says-more-come-after-friday-deadline" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>DOJ PUBLISHES TROVE OF EPSTEIN FILES, SAYS MORE TO COME AFTER FRIDAY DEADLINE</strong></a></p><p>Wexner employed Epstein as his <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/finance" target="_blank" rel="noopener">financial manager</a> from the late 1980s until 2007 and named him a trustee of the Wexner Foundation, his charitable organization that supports leadership and educational initiatives, particularly within the Jewish community.</p><p>Wexner, a former CEO and founder of <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/entertainment/events/victorias-secret" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Victoria’s Secret owner L Brands</a>, has not been accused of criminal wrongdoing.</p><p>His ties to Epstein have drawn heightened scrutiny in the wake of Epstein’s criminal cases, and Wexner has faced questions in prior congressional inquiries examining those relationships.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/lapsed-epstein-deadline-underscores-challenge-reviewing-files-30-days" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>LAPSED EPSTEIN DEADLINE UNDERSCORES CHALLENGE OF REVIEWING TROVES OF FILES IN 30 DAYS</strong></a></p><p>The House Oversight Committee has been investigating how Epstein amassed his wealth and influence, including whether powerful individuals or institutions enabled his criminal conduct or benefited from it.</p><p>President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Donald Trump's</a> administration has ordered the Justice Department to release files tied to criminal probes of Epstein in compliance with a transparency law passed by Congress. </p><p>So far, <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/justice-department" target="_blank" rel="noopener">the DOJ</a> has released approximately 12,285 documents comprising around 125,575 pages, which is less than 1% of all records potentially related to the case, <a href="https://www.axios.com/2026/01/06/epstein-files-justice-department-review-2-million-documents" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow">according to Axios.</a> While there are stacks of unreleased files remaining, the DOJ said in the letter that it believes "a meaningful portion" of the documents are duplicates.</p><p>The department said in a letter to U.S. District Judge Paul Engelmayer of the Southern District of New York that "there are <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/more-than-2m-epstein-files-still-unpublished-doj-confirms" target="_blank" rel="noopener">more than 2 million documents</a> potentially responsive to the Act that are in various phases of review." </p><p><i>Fox News’ Rachel Wolf and Reuters contributed to this report.</i></p>
