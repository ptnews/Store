<p>Actor George Clooney, along with his wife Amal Alamuddin Clooney, and their two children, have become <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/world-regions/france" target="_blank" rel="noopener">French citizens</a>.</p><p>The couple and their 8-year-old twins, Ella and Alexander, were granted citizenship, according to a naturalization decree.</p><p>The couple has been living in France with their children after uprooting the family to get away from the culture of Hollywood, the Academy Award-winning actor and director said in October. </p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/entertainment/amanda-seyfried-bails-tricky-hollywood-quiet-farm-life" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>AMANDA SEYFRIED BAILS ON 'TRICKY' HOLLYWOOD FOR QUIET FARM LIFE</u></strong></a></p><p>In an interview with Esquire, Clooney, 64, opened up about his life in France and explained why they chose to move his family to the countryside. </p><p>"You know, we live on a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/entertainment/george-amal-clooneys-globetrotting-lifestyle-potentially-jeopardy-under-trump-sanctions" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><u>farm in France</u></a>. A good portion of my life growing up was on a farm, and as a kid I hated the whole idea of it. But now, for them, it’s like – they’re not on their iPads, you know? They have dinner with grown-ups and have to take their dishes in. They have a much better life."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/entertainment/candace-cameron-bure-embraces-small-town-life-away-hollywood" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CANDACE CAMERON BURE EMBRACES SMALL-TOWN LIFE AWAY FROM HOLLYWOOD</strong></a></p><p>"I was worried about raising our kids in L.A., in the culture of <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/entertainment/celebrity-news" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><u>Hollywood</u></a>. I felt like they were never going to get a fair shake at life," he continued. </p><p>In addition to his family home in France, George reportedly owns an estate in England, a villa on Lake Como in Italy, and another property near his family in Kentucky, People reported. </p><p>Fox News Digital has reached out to Clooney's representatives for comment. </p><p>During his Esquire interview, Clooney talked about living in France, away from the glamor of Hollywood. </p><p>"France – they kind of don’t give a s--- about fame," he said. "I don’t want them to be walking around worried about paparazzi. I don’t want them being compared to somebody else’s famous kids."</p>
