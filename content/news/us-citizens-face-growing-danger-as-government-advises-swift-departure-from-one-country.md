<p>The <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/foreign-policy/state-department" target="_blank" rel="noopener">State Department</a> has renewed its "do not travel" advisory for Russia.</p><p>The existing <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/travel" target="_blank" rel="noopener">travel advisory</a> was reissued this week without edits, with the State Department citing a myriad of high risks related to terrorism, wrongful detention and "arbitrary enforcement of local laws."</p><p>The advisory comes as the nearly four-year war between Russia and Ukraine continues to escalate.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/travel/us-officials-warn-snowbirds-violent-crime-winter-destination-hot-spot"><strong>US OFFICIALS WARN SNOWBIRDS OF 'VIOLENT CRIME' IN WINTER DESTINATION HOT SPOT</strong></a></p><p>Officials have limited ability to help Americans, as the embassy in Moscow has reduced its staff and consulates in Russia have suspended operations, the advisory notes.</p><p>"U.S. citizens in Russia should leave immediately," it states. "<a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/world-regions/russia" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Russian officials</a> often question and threaten U.S. citizens without reason."</p><p>The advisory says there is precedent for Russian security services arresting Americans on false charges.  </p><p>"They have denied them [Americans] fair treatment and convicted them without credible evidence."</p><p>Travelers are also told to reconsider bringing electronic devices into the country, as it should be assumed that all electronic communications and devices are monitored.</p><p>Due to sanctions, American <a href="https://www.foxbusiness.com/category/credit-debit" target="_blank" rel="noopener">debit and credit cards</a> no longer work in Russia and "sending electronic money transfers from the United States to Russia is nearly impossible," it says.</p><p>The alert says tourists who decide to <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/world-regions/russia" target="_blank" rel="noopener">go to Russia</a> should "be ready for the possibility of detention for an unknown amount of time."</p><p>"Log out of all social media accounts and do not access your social media accounts while in Russia," the advisory also instructs. "Prepare a will and designate appropriate insurance beneficiaries or power of attorney."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/lifestyle" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE FOR MORE LIFESTYLE STORIES</strong></a></p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/personalities/vladimir-putin" target="_blank" rel="noopener">President Putin</a> recently claimed that Ukraine launched a large-scale drone attack early Monday against a presidential residence in the Novgorod region, involving 91 long-range drones that were intercepted by Russian air defenses.</p><p>Russia’s defense ministry shared footage showing a masked soldier standing next to drone wreckage, saying the wreckage was recovered from the attack.</p><p>The ministry claimed the drone carried a high-explosive warhead "filled with a large number of striking elements" intended to hit civilian targets.</p><p>Russia’s border areas with Ukraine have been under <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/police-and-law-enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">martial law</a> since October 2022, according to the advisory.</p><p>Under martial law, authorities can set curfews, seize property, restrict movement, detain foreigners, forcibly relocate residents and limit public gatherings.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/lifestyle/quizzes" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TEST YOURSELF WITH OUR LATEST LIFESTYLE QUIZ</strong></a></p><p>"Russian authorities have questioned, detained and arrested people for ‘acting against Russia's interests,’" the advisory reads.</p><p>The U.S. government has issued <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/travel/general/travel-safety" target="_blank" rel="noopener">other travel advisories</a> in recent weeks.
