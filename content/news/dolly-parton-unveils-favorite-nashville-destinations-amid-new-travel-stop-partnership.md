<p>The queen of country music is bringing her brand of Southern hospitality to Tennessee highways — making <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/travel/vacation-destinations" target="_blank" rel="noopener">the trip to Nashville</a> feel as memorable and fun as the destination itself.</p><p>Dolly Parton recently announced the roll-out of Dolly’s Tennessean Travel Stops. The rest-stop areas will feature new and reimagined entertainment elements.</p><p>"I have spent the bulk of my life <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/travel/general" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow">on the road</a>, and more specifically on a bus," said Parton in a release.</p><p>Parton has partnered with the Tennessean Travel Stop brand to relaunch their flagship stop in Cornersville, Tennessee, with additional locations to be announced in 2026. </p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/travel/dolly-parton-reimagines-travel-truckers-families-roadtrippers-venture" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>DOLLY PARTON REIMAGINES TRAVEL FOR TRUCKERS, FAMILIES AND ROADTRIPPERS IN HOT NEW VENTURE </strong></a></p><p>Parton recently discussed her favorite place in Music City in an interview <a href="https://airmail.news/arts-intel/highlights/dolly-partons-guide-to-nashville" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">with Airmail</a>. </p><p>In a nod to her own musical talents and career, she mentioned the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/travel/grand-ole-oprys-100-year-legacy-brought-compelling-life-meet-american" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Grand Ole Opry.</a> </p><p>George D. Hay is credited with founding the musical treasure, having started <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/nashville" target="_blank" rel="noopener">radio station WSM</a> — which went live for the first time in October 1925. The program, originally called "WSM's Barn Dance," also launched at that time.</p><p>The prominent announcer and program director launched the show with Uncle Jimmy Thompson, a 77-year-old fiddle player. </p><p>The name of the now-iconic brand — which includes a program, a live performance and a location — came from Hay in a burst of inspiration. </p><p><a
