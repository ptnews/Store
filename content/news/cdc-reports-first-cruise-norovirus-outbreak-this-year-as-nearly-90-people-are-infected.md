<p>The U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) has confirmed a norovirus outbreak aboard a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/travel/general/cruises" target="_blank" rel="noopener">cruise ship</a> — the first one of the year that's been reported. </p><p>The Holland America Line was hit with a norovirus outbreak that sickened nearly 90 passengers and crew during its Dec 28-Jan. 9 voyage.</p><p>The cruise notified the CDC of the outbreak on Jan. 8, according to the agency’s report.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/travel/cdc-reports-21st-cruise-ship-norovirus-outbreak-year-nearly-100-infected" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CDC REPORTS 21ST CRUISE SHIP NOROVIRUS OUTBREAK THIS YEAR, WITH NEARLY 100 PEOPLE INFECTED</strong></a></p><p>The outbreak spread on the cruise line’s Rotterdam ship.</p><p>The vessel departed from Fort Lauderdale, Florida, with stops in Curaçao, Colombia, Costa Rica, Jamaica and <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/travel/vacation-destinations" target="_blank" rel="noopener">other destinations</a>, according to CruiseMapper.</p><p>Eighty-one passengers plus eight crew members reported diarrhea and vomiting, the CDC's data shows.</p><p>Over 2,593 passengers, plus 1,005 crew members, were on board at the time.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/newsletters?cmpid=fnfirstnl" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE TO SIGN UP FOR OUR LIFESTYLE NEWSLETTER</strong></a></p><p>A Holland America Line spokesperson told Fox News Digital on Monday that "during its previous voyage, a number of guests onboard Rotterdam reported symptoms of gastrointestinal illness."</p><p>"The cases were mostly mild and quickly resolved," the spokesperson added. </p><p>"The health of our guests and crew is a top priority, and consistent with CDC protocols, we conducted a comprehensive sanitization of the ship when the cruise ended Friday in Fort Lauderdale."</p><p>Since the outbreak, the cruise line has increased its cleaning and disinfection procedures, according to their <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/health/infectious-disease/outbreaks" target="_blank" rel="noopener">outbreak prevention and response plan</a>, the CDC noted.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/lifestyle" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE FOR MORE LIFESTYLE STORIES</strong></a></p><p>Holland America Line collected stool specimens from <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/health/infectious-disease" target="_blank" rel="noopener">gastrointestinal illness cases</a> for testing and isolated ill passengers and crew, the CDC reports.</p><p>In order to track <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/health/digestive-health" target="_blank" rel="noopener">gastrointestinal issues</a>, medical staff evaluated symptoms of three or more loose stools from individuals within a 24-hour period, or that which is more than normal for individual travelers. </p><p>Scott Weisenberg, M.D., medical director of the NYU Langone Infectious Disease Associates and the NYU Travel Medicine Program, previously told Fox News Digital that <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/science/wild-nature/viruses" target="_blank" rel="noopener">the virus</a> is highly contagious — and it can spread quickly in crowded settings such as cruise ships.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/lifestyle/quizzes" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TEST YOURSELF WITH OUR LATEST LIFESTYLE QUIZ</strong></a></p><p>"If people have acute vomiting, they should avoid public areas, since even if they don't touch anything, the virus can spread through the air," he said.</p><p>Weisenberg also noted that proper hygiene plays a key role in preventing transmission. </p><p>Shared surfaces can easily become contaminated, putting anyone who touches them at risk of infection.</p>
