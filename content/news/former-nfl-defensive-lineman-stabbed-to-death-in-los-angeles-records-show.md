<p>Kevin Johnson, a former NFL defensive lineman who played for the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/nfl/philadelphia-eagles" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Philadelphia Eagles</a> and Oakland Raiders in the 1990s, was stabbed to death in Los Angeles, authorities said. He was 55.</p><p>Johnson’s body was discovered at a homeless encampment Wednesday, according to the Los Angeles County Medical Examiner’s Office. </p><p>The <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/homicide" target="_blank" rel="noopener">cause of death</a> was listed as blunt head trauma and stab wounds, records showed.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE FOR MORE SPORTS COVERAGE ON FOXNEWS.COM</strong></a></p><p>His death was being investigated as a homicide. Steve De Jong <a href="https://www.newsweek.com/sports/nfl/former-eagles-raiders-standout-dies-at-55-11403192" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">told Newsweek</a>. "It looks like he was probably living there."</p><p>Johnson’s friends and family mourned him on social media.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports/fbi-probes-jim-irsay-death-over-prescription-drugs-ketamine-use-doctor-relationship-report" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>FBI PROBES JIM IRSAY DEATH OVER PRESCRIPTION DRUGS, KETAMINE USE AND DOCTOR RELATIONSHIP: REPORT</strong></a></p><p>He was a standout at Texas Southern before he was selected in the fourth round by the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/nfl/new-england-patriots" target="_blank" rel="noopener">New England Patriots</a> in 1993. He never played a down with the Patriots and didn’t appear in an NFL game until 1995, when he was with the Eagles.</p><p>He played 23 games for the Eagles over two seasons. He recorded 43 tackles and seven sacks and returned one fumble for a touchdown.</p><p>Johnson joined the Raiders in 1997 and had 11 tackles in 15 games.</p><p>The defensive tackle took a chance at Arena Football when his time in the NFL was over.
