<p>Deputies <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/southeast/florida" target="_blank" rel="noopener">in Florida</a> raced to save the life of a 4-year-old child on Interstate 275 after the boy suffered a medical emergency, authorities said Sunday.</p><p>Hillsborough County sheriff’s deputies responded at around 5:20 p.m. Saturday to a "person down" call involving an unresponsive child on the highway.</p><p>When deputies arrived, <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/health/healthy-living/childrens-health" target="_blank" rel="noopener">the child</a> was not breathing and had no pulse.</p><p>Bodycam footage shows deputies rushing to the vehicle on the side of the interstate and pulling the child out of the back seat.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media/officers-look-back-how-revived-baby-trapped-under-car-the-lords-not-done-her" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>OFFICERS LOOK BACK ON HOW THEY REVIVED BABY TRAPPED UNDER CAR: 'THE LORD'S NOT DONE WITH HER'</strong></a></p><p>As one deputy picked up the boy, a woman who had been performing CPR could be heard saying, "He’s breathing!"</p><p>The deputy carried the child to the side of the interstate, laid him on the ground and began CPR.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/man-runs-florida-street-save-two-young-children-who-wandered-away-from-rental-home" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>MAN RUNS INTO FLORIDA STREET TO SAVE TWO YOUNG CHILDREN WHO WANDERED AWAY FROM RENTAL HOME</strong></a></p><p>"There’s something in the mouth. Pull it out!" one rescuer said.</p><p>It was unclear what was blocking the child's breathing, but once it was cleared, first responders continued CPR until Tampa Fire Rescue crews arrived and took over treatment.</p><p>Officials said the child was transported to St. Joseph’s Hospital and is now listed as being in stable condition.</p><p>The sheriff’s office credited the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/police-and-law-enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">deputies’ quick actions</a> with saving the child’s life.</p>
