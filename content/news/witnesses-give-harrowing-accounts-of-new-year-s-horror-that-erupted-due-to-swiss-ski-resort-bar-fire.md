<p>Eyewitnesses have been recounting the nightmare they saw after a fiery blaze erupted at a bar in <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/world-regions/europe" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Switzerland</a> leaving dozens dead and others wounded earlier this week.</p><p>"I thought my little brother was inside so I came and tried to break the window to help people to exit, and after that I went in," a man, aged 18, said, according to the <a href="https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/clyml77ml7jo" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">BBC</a>. "I saw people burning... I found people burning from head to foot, no clothes anymore," he recalled, noting, "It was very shocking." </p><p>The man's brother was not harmed, the outlet reported.</p><p>"I went in this bar every day this week — the day I didn't go, it burned," he noted, according to the outlet.</p><p>An individual named Daniella was heading home with her husband from a dinner when she witnessed some of the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/disaster-response" target="_blank" rel="noopener">horror</a>, the BBC reported.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/officials-race-identify-mostly-young-victims-deadly-swiss-alps-bar-fire" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>OFFICIALS RACE TO IDENTIFY MOSTLY YOUNG VICTIMS OF DEADLY SWISS ALPS BAR FIRE</strong></a></p><p>"People were running in all directions, screaming and crying. I saw several people being carried out on stretchers," she told the outlet. "A young man came up to me and said he'd seen hell — things he would never forget. And then I just froze."</p><p>Gianni Campolo, 19, headed to the bar to assist first responders after getting a call from a friend who escaped the fire, according to the <a href="https://apnews.com/article/switzerland-crans-montana-bar-fire-1bcad7b8d8fddabe1e1c59bafc71e689" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">Associated Press</a>.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/explosive-fire-kills-multiple-people-swiss-alps-bar-during-new-years-celebrations" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>AT LEAST 40 DEAD IN FIRE AT SWISS ALPS BAR DURING NEW YEAR'S CELEBRATIONS, POLICE SAY</strong></a></p><p>"I have seen horror, and I don’t know what else would be worse than this," Campolo informed TF1, according to the AP.</p><p>Ferdinand Du Beaudiez, a 19-year-old who escaped from the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/disasters" target="_blank" rel="noopener">fire</a> in the bar, said that he went back in to look for his brother, his girlfriend and others, said he "couldn't think to let them stay in the fire when I was alive outside." </p><p>He said the first time he headed back in, he found a person "laying in the stairs" who "was completely burned, clothes were burned" — he explained that he could not tell if the individual was a man or a woman. He "slid" the person "on the ground, and he was being taken care of, um, outside … " the survivor recalled. </p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/travel/massive-blaze-engulfs-historic-new-england-wharf-firefighters-race-contain-flames" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>MASSIVE BLAZE ENGULFS HISTORIC NEW ENGLAND WHARF; FIREFIGHTERS RACE TO CONTAIN FLAMES</strong></a></p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/download" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>CLICK HERE TO GET THE FOX NEWS APP</u></strong></a></p><p>The second time he headed back in, he experienced "more smoke," and he "could breathe less," he said, explaining he "went in, couldn't see anything and went straight out."</p><p>He eventually found his girlfriend, who told him where his brother was.</p>
