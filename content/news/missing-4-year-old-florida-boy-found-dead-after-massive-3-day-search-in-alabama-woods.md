<p>A 4-year-old Florida boy who <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/topic/missing-persons" target="_blank" rel="noopener">disappeared while visiting family</a> over New Year’s has been found dead in Alabama.</p><p>Johnathan Everett Boley, known to family members as "John John," was reported missing on New Year's Eve after he wandered away from a rural property along Highway 195 near Jasper, Alabama, while playing outside with his dog, Walker County Sheriff Nick Smith said during a news conference.</p><p>Hundreds of law enforcement officers, volunteers, K-9 teams, drones and aircraft were deployed in the search, which covered several square miles of wooded land. </p><p>Searchers found the child’s body Jan. 2 roughly two miles from the home, officials said. The dog was located alive nearby. Authorities did not immediately release a cause of death, with an official autopsy pending.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/teen-girl-vanishes-from-south-carolina-home-police-fear-shes-serious-danger-after-monthlong-search"><strong>TEEN GIRL VANISHES FROM SOUTH CAROLINA HOME, POLICE FEAR SHE'S IN SERIOUS DANGER AFTER MONTHLONG SEARCH</strong></a></p><p>"This is not the outcome anyone hoped for," Smith said. "Our hearts go out to this family and everyone who worked tirelessly to bring this child home."</p><p>Authorities said that the young boy and his siblings <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/southeast/florida" target="_blank" rel="noopener">live with their mother in Florida</a> and were visiting their father for the holidays under a custody arrangement.</p><p>The boy was last seen wearing a yellow Mickey Mouse shirt, black pants and Paw Patrol shoes before he disappeared from the home.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/texas-sheriff-strongly-believes-remains-found-belong-missing-teen-camila-mendoza-olmos"><strong>TEXAS SHERIFF ‘STRONGLY’ BELIEVES REMAINS FOUND BELONG TO MISSING TEEN CAMILA MENDOZA OLMOS</strong></a></p><p>During the news conference, <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/police-and-law-enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">investigators announced the arrest</a> of the boy’s father, Jameson Kyle Boley, 40, which occurred amid the search.</p><p>Officials said Boley was charged with unlawful manufacture of a destructive device after investigators discovered precursor materials at the property.</p><p><strong>READ THE CRIMINAL COMPLAINT – APP USERS, </strong><a href="https://static.foxnews.com/foxnews.com/content/uploads/2026/01/boley-manu-destrictive-device-6957f8ff11090.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE</strong></a></p><p>According to a criminal complaint filed in Walker County District Court, Boley is accused of possessing precursor substances with the intent to unlawfully manufacture a destructive device or bacteriological weapon on or about Dec. 31, 2025, at the Highway 195 address. The charge is a felony under <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/southeast/alabama" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Alabama law</a>, court records show.</p><p>Police said that the father’s charge is unrelated to the circumstances surrounding the child’s disappearance and death.</p><p>"At this time, there is no evidence indicating foul play in the child’s death, and these charges should not be conflated with the search for Johnathan," Smith said.</p><p>In Parrish, Florida, community members gathered for a candlelight vigil for the young boy.</p><p>"For so many of us, our hearts are broken over the tragic loss," Reverend Douglas Scharf, bishop of the Diocese of Southwest Florida, told <a href="https://www.fox13news.com/news/parrish-community-mourns-death-missing-child-found-dead-alabama" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">FOX 13.</a></p><p>Authorities asked the public to <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/personal-freedoms/privacy" target="_blank" rel="noopener">allow the family privacy</a> as they grieve and said additional information would be released when available.</p>
