<p>A federal appeals court on Wednesday temporarily lifted restrictions on Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) agents’ use of force against protesters in Minnesota, handing a short-term win to President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Donald Trump’s</a> administration as it challenges a lower-court ruling.</p><p>The 8th Circuit U.S. Court of Appeals issued an unsigned order placing an administrative stay on limits imposed by a district judge after protesters filed suit. The move pauses those restrictions while the appeals court considers the government’s request to block the injunction during the appeal.</p><p>The ruling comes as federal <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/immigration" target="_blank" rel="noopener">immigration enforcement</a> tactics face growing legal scrutiny nationwide.</p><p>Attorney General Pam Bondi praised the court’s decision, calling it a "victory."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/federal-prosecutors-open-investigation-walz-frey-over-alleged-impeding-law-enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>FEDERAL PROSECUTORS OPEN INVESTIGATION INTO WALZ, FREY OVER ALLEGED IMPEDING OF LAW ENFORCEMENT</strong></a></p><p>"A liberal judge in Minnesota tried to handcuff ICE agents who are enforcing the Nation’s immigration laws and responding to obstructive and violent interference from agitators," Bondi said on X. "The 8th Circuit just granted an administrative stay HALTING these restrictions, which were designed to undermine federal law enforcement.</p><p>"<a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/justice-department" target="_blank" rel="noopener">This DOJ</a> will protect federal law enforcement agents from criminals in the streets AND activist judges in the courtroom."</p><p>The Associated Press reported that ICE is operating under an <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/immigration/enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">internal memo</a> asserting broader authority to use force during arrests, including entering homes with administrative warrants rather than warrants signed by a judge.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/doj-launches-civil-rights-investigation-after-minnesota-agitators-allegedly-storms-church" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>DOJ LAUNCHES CIVIL RIGHTS INVESTIGATION AFTER MINNESOTA AGITATORS STORM CHURCH</strong></a></p><p>In a Jan. 16 ruling, <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/judiciary" target="_blank" rel="noopener">U.S. District Judge Kate Menendez</a> issued the preliminary injunction at the center of the appeal, siding with protesters and legal observers who sued the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) and ICE over their treatment during immigration enforcement operations.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media/acting-ice-director-defends-agencys-focus-targeting-criminal-illegal-aliens-details-threat-agents" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>ACTING ICE DIRECTOR DEFENDS AGENCY'S FOCUS ON TARGETING CRIMINAL ILLEGAL ALIENS, DETAILS THREAT TO AGENTS</strong></a></p><p>Menendez found the plaintiffs were likely to succeed on claims that federal agents violated their <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/personal-freedoms/first-amendment" target="_blank" rel="noopener">First and Fourth Amendment rights</a> during protests and observation of ICE activity tied to Operation Metro Surge in the Twin Cities.</p><p>The judge cited a pattern of confrontations in which ICE agents allegedly used pepper spray, pointed weapons, made arrests and conducted traffic stops against individuals who were peacefully observing or protesting immigration enforcement.</p><p>Menendez’s ruling temporarily barred agents from using force or making arrests against peaceful protesters and observers absent probable cause, prompting the Trump administration to seek emergency relief from the 8th Circuit. The decision on Wednesday put a pause on those restrictions as the appeal moves forward.</p><p><i>The Associated Press contributed to this report.</i></p>
