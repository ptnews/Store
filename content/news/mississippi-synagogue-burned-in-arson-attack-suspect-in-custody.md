<p>A suspect was in custody Saturday after an apparent arson attack set a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/southeast/mississippi" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Mississippi synagogue</a> ablaze, destroying Torahs and prompting an investigation involving the FBI and the Joint Terrorism Task Force.</p><p>The blaze broke out shortly after 3 a.m. at Beth Israel Congregation in Jackson, the state's largest synagogue. No congregants were injured, officials said.</p><p>Photos from the scene showed severe damage to an administrative office and the synagogue’s library, where several Torahs were destroyed or damaged.</p><p>Jackson Mayor John Horhn confirmed that a person was taken into custody following an investigation that also included the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/tech/topics/fbi" target="_blank" rel="noopener">FBI</a> and the Joint Terrorism Task Force.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/nyc-sex-offender-indicted-threatening-kill-jews-police-federal-officials" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>NYC SEX OFFENDER INDICTED FOR THREATENING TO KILL JEWS, POLICE AND FEDERAL OFFICIALS</strong></a></p><p>"Acts of antisemitism, racism, and religious hatred are attacks on Jackson as a whole and will be treated as <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/hate-crime" target="_blank" rel="noopener">acts of terror</a> against residents’ safety and freedom to worship," Horhn said in a statement.</p><p>Officials did not immediately release the name of the suspect or what charges the person was facing.</p><p>Beth Israel Congregation was previously targeted in a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/terror/bombings" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Ku Klux Klan bombing</a> in 1967, an attack linked to the synagogue's involvement in civil rights efforts, according to the Institute for Southern Jewish Life, which also operates from the building.</p><p>"As Jackson’s only synagogue, Beth Israel is a beloved institution, and it is the fellowship of our neighbors and extended community that will see us through," the institute said in a statement.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/mamdani-disputes-antisemitism-definition-amid-blowback-jewish-community-executive-orders" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>MAMDANI DISPUTES ANTISEMITISM DEFINITION AMID BLOWBACK FROM JEWISH COMMUNITY ABOUT DAY 1 EXECUTIVE ORDERS</strong></a></p><p>The American Jewish Committee also released a statement saying it was "outraged" by the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/topic/anti-semitism" target="_blank" rel="noopener">alleged attack</a>.</p><p>"This hateful act is only the most recent symptom of the dangerous rising antisemitism facing Jewish communities across the country and around the world," it said.</p><p>The Anti-Defamation League called the incident a "deliberate, targeted attack on the Jewish community."</p><p>"Beth Israel survived a KKK bombing in 1967," ADL CEO Jonathan Greenblatt said in a statement. "That it has been attacked again, amid a surge of antisemitic incidents across the US, is a stark reminder: antisemitic violence is escalating, and it demands total condemnation and swift action from everyone — including community leaders, law enforcement, and government officials."</p><p>The synagogue's president, Zach Shemper, said the congregation was still assessing the damage and had received outreach from other houses of worship, according to <a href="https://mississippitoday.org/2026/01/10/fire-damages-mississippi-largest-synagogue" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Mississippi Today.</a> Shemper added that services will be suspended indefinitely.</p><p>One <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/topic/holocaust" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Torah that survived the Holocaust</a> was not damaged in the fire, the outlet reported.</p><p><i>The Associated Press contributed to this report.</i></p>
