<p>Multiple <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/conflicts/iran" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Iranian state TV channels</a> were hacked on Sunday amid a near-total internet shutdown to air footage of exiled Crown Prince Reza Pahlavi and images of anti-government protests that have rocked Tehran in recent weeks.</p><p>Two clips of Pahlavi were shown as well as a graphic calling on Iranian security forces to side with the public, The Associated Press reported.</p><p>"Don't point your weapons at the people. Join the nation for the freedom of Iran," one graphic read, <a href="https://newsroom.ap.org/detail/HackerstargetIranstateTVssatellitetransmissiontobroadcastexiledcrownprince/65a7a3ee84748531387b72fa15b61456/text" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow"><u>according to</u></a> a translation from the outlet.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/exiled-iranian-crown-prince-reveals-6-step-plan-exert-pressure-tehrans-regime" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Pahlavi himself called on Iran’s military</a> to break with the Islamic Republic and side with the people.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media/trumps-leadership-creates-rare-opportunity-change-iran-former-iranian-political-prisoner-says" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TRUMP’S LEADERSHIP CREATES 'RARE OPPORTUNITY' FOR CHANGE IN IRAN, FORMER IRANIAN POLITICAL PRISONER SAYS</strong></a></p><p>"I have a special message for the military. You are the national army of Iran, not the Islamic Republic army," he said in the hacked broadcast. "You have a duty to protect your own lives. You don’t have much time left. Join the people as soon as possible."</p><p>The U.S.-based Human Rights Activists News Agency (HRANA), which tracks human rights violations in Iran, <a href="https://www.en-hrana.org/day-twenty-two-of-the-protests-the-scale-of-the-killings-grows-as-the-internet-shutdown-continues/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow"><u>said on Sunday</u></a> that nationwide protests continued into the 22nd day as President Donald Trump weighs possible U.S. military action.</p><p>The group’s aggregated figures showed 624 recorded protests, the arrest of at least 24,669 people and the confirmed deaths of 3,919 individuals.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/khamenei-calls-trump-criminal-blames-him-deadly-protests-sweeping-iran" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>KHAMENEI CALLS TRUMP A ‘CRIMINAL,’ BLAMES HIM FOR DEADLY PROTESTS SWEEPING IRAN</strong></a></p><p>HRANA said 3,685 of <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/foreign-policy/human-rights" target="_blank" rel="noopener">those killed were protesters</a>, including 25 children under the age of 18.</p><p>Nearly 9,000 deaths remain under investigation.</p><p>Iran International <a href="https://www.iranintl.com/en/202601196444" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow"><u>reported that</u></a> witnesses across multiple cities told them security forces stormed hospitals, removed injured protesters and interfered with medical care, while reports from other areas described overwhelmed morgues and a strong security presence around medical facilities.</p><p>The outlet also reported that witnesses described injured protesters being left without medical care after shootings, as ambulances failed to arrive and phone networks were unavailable.</p><p>Others said hospitals were inaccessible or refused treatment, resulting in some wounded protesters bleeding to death while taking shelter in nearby buildings.</p>
