<p>Denmark on Monday ramped up its military presence in Greenland, deploying extra troops to the strategic Arctic territory amid escalating tensions with President Donald Trump.</p><p>Local Danish broadcaster <a href="https://tv2.dk/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">TV 2</a> said the Danish Armed Forces confirmed a new contingent of troops, described as "a substantial contribution," were arriving at Greenland’s main international airport Monday night.</p><p>Maj. Gen. Søren Andersen, head of Denmark’s Arctic Command, said about 100 Danish soldiers have already arrived in <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/world-regions/greenland" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Nuuk, Greenland</a>’s capital, with others later deployed to Kangerlussuaq in western Greenland.</p><p>The new military move comes in the wake of comments made by Trump over the region's strategic and military importance. </p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media/white-house-urges-cooler-heads-prevail-eu-fires-back-trump-tariffs-over-greenland" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>WHITE HOUSE URGES 'COOLER HEADS TO PREVAIL' AS EU FIRES BACK ON TRUMP TARIFFS OVER GREENLAND</strong></a></p><p>In a Truth Social post Jan. 18, Trump warned that Denmark had failed to secure Greenland against foreign threats.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/nato" target="_blank" rel="noopener">"NATO has been telling Denmark</a>, for 20 years, that ‘you have to get the Russian threat away from Greenland,’" Trump wrote. </p><p>"Unfortunately, Denmark has been unable to do anything about it. Now it is time, and it will be done!!!" he said.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/us-congressional-delegation-visits-denmark-amid-backlash-over-trumps-push-acquire-greenland" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>US CONGRESSIONAL DELEGATION VISITS DENMARK AMID BACKLASH OVER TRUMP'S PUSH TO ACQUIRE GREENLAND</strong></a></p><p>On Monday, a text message exchange between Trump and Norwegian Prime Minister Jonas Gahr Støre over Greenland and the Nobel Peace Prize was released in a statement.</p><p>"Denmark cannot protect that land from Russia or China, and why do they have a ‘right of ownership’ anyway?" Trump said before adding that there were "no written documents; it’s only that a boat landed there hundreds of years ago, but we had boats landing there, also," he said in part of the exchange.</p><p>"I have done more for NATO than any other person since its founding, and now, NATO should do something for the United States. The world is not secure unless we have complete and total control of Greenland. Thank you! President DJT," he added.</p><p>Before now, according to <a href="https://www.reuters.com/world/china/danish-general-says-there-are-no-chinese-or-russian-ships-near-greenland-2026-01-16/" </a>reported.</p><p>Danish forces already stationed in Greenland could remain for a year or more, with additional rotations planned in the coming years.</p><p>Meanwhile, White House press secretary <a
