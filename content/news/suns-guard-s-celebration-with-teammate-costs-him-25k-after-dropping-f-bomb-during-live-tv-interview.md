<p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/nba/phoenix-suns" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Phoenix Suns</a> guard Jalen Green's moment of celebration with teammate Collin Gillespie proved to be a costly one. </p><p>The <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/nba" target="_blank" rel="noopener">NBA</a> fined Green $25,000 after he dropped an f-bomb during Gillespie’s live postgame interview following a dominant performance in the Suns’ 115-101 victory over the Washington Wizards on Monday night. </p><p>Gillespie scored 25 points to help the Suns win five of their past six games. During an on-court interview, Gillespie was giving credit to his teammates when a few, including Green, interrupted to celebrate with him. </p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE FOR MORE SPORTS COVERAGE ON FOXNEWS.COM</strong></a></p><p>"They can’t f--- with you," Green appeared to say into Gillespie’s ear, but the broadcast’s microphone picked up the remark. </p><p>"Oh my God," Gillespie responded. "On live TV, bro?" </p><p>"I don’t care," Green said in response as he exited the interview. </p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports/pelicans-guard-connects-right-hook-fight-suns-opponent" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>PELICANS GUARD CONNECTS WITH RIGHT HOOK IN FIGHT WITH SUNS OPPONENT</u></strong></a></p><p>The league felt differently, and on Tuesday, Executive Vice President and Head of Basketball Operations James Jones announced that Green was fined $25,000 for "using profanity when interrupting a teammate’s live television interview."</p><p>Green, who did not play Monday, has appeared in just two games this season as he deals with a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/injuries" target="_blank" rel="noopener">hamstring injury</a>. The 23-year-old guard was initially injured during training camp.</p><p>Green played in all 82 regular-season games in each of the past two seasons, and was a part of the trade that sent 15-time All-Star <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/kevin-durant" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Kevin Durant</a> to the Houston Rockets.</p><p><i>The Associated Press contributed to this report. </i></p><p><i><strong>Follow Fox News Digital’s</strong></i><a href="https://twitter.com/FoxNewsSports_" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><i><strong><u> sports coverage on X</u></strong></i></a><i><strong>, and subscribe to</strong></i><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/newsletters" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><i><strong><u> the Fox News Sports Huddle newsletter</u></strong></i></a><i><strong>.</strong></i></p>
