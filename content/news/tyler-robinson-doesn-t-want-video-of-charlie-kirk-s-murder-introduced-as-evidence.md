<p>Lawyers for <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/tyler-robinson" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Tyler Robinson</a>, the 22-year-old accused assassin of Turning Point USA founder Charlie Kirk, have asked a Utah judge to block video of the murder from being introduced as evidence at a hearing scheduled for Feb. 3.</p><p>Kirk, a 31-year-old father of two, suffered a fatal gunshot wound to the neck at Utah Valley University in September, when he was participating in a public speaking event sponsored by the campus chapter of Turning Point.</p><p>In a memorandum filed Tuesday, Robinson's attorneys claimed the video is irrelevant to their motion to have the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/trials" target="_blank" rel="noopener">county prosecutor's office disqualified</a> from handling the case and would violate his right to a fair trial.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/prosecutors-child-witnessed-charlie-kirk-assassination-can-he-take-part-case" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>A PROSECUTOR'S CHILD WITNESSED CHARLIE KIRK'S ASSASSINATION — CAN HE TAKE PART IN THE CASE?</strong></a></p><p>Robinson's lawyers have accused the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/west/utah" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Utah County Attorney</a>'s Office of having a conflict of interest because the adult child of a deputy prosecutor was present during the assassination.
