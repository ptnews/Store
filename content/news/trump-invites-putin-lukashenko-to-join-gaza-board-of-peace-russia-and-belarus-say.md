<p>The Trump administration extended invitations to <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/world-regions/russia" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Russia and Belarus</a> to join a proposed Gaza "Board of Peace," officials in both countries said Monday. </p><p>Kremlin spokesman Dmitry Peskov told reporters in Moscow that Russian President Vladimir Putin is reviewing the invitation.</p><p>"President Putin has indeed received an offer through diplomatic channels to join this Board of Peace. We are currently studying all the details of this proposal," Peskov said, according to Russian state media outlet TASS. "We hope to contact the U.S. side to clarify all the details."</p><p>Belarus’ Ministry of Foreign Affairs also confirmed receiving an invitation, saying in a post on X that President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Donald Trump</a> sent Belarusian leader Alexander Lukashenko a letter proposing the country become a founding member of the "Board of Peace."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/trump-threatens-hamas-if-gaza-ceasefire-collapses-jd-vance-visit-israel" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TRUMP THREATENS HAMAS IF GAZA CEASEFIRE COLLAPSES AS JD VANCE TO VISIT ISRAEL</strong></a></p><p>"We are ready to take part in the activities of the Board of Peace, taking into account and hoping that this organization will expand its scope and authority far beyond the mandate proposed in the initiative," the ministry said.</p><p>Other countries over the weekend, including <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/world-regions/india" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Argentina, Jordan, Canada, India</a>, Egypt, Hungary and Vietnam, announced they too had received invitations from the White House.</p><p>Neither the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/foreign-policy/state-department" target="_blank" rel="noopener">State Department</a> nor the White House immediately responded to Fox News Digital’s request for comment about a full tally of all countries invited.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/opinion/trumps-grand-peace-strategy-bearing-fruit-middle-east-around-world" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TRUMP’S GRAND PEACE STRATEGY IS BEARING FRUIT, IN naming senior international figures to oversee governance, reconstruction and long-term development of the enclave.</p><p>"The Board of Peace will play an essential role in fulfilling all 20 points of
