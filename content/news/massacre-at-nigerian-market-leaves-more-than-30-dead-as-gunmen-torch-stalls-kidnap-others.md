<p>Dozens of people were killed and several others kidnapped when <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/terrorism" target="_blank" rel="noopener">armed attackers</a> raided a rural market in central Nigeria, police said Sunday.</p><p>"Over 30 victims lost their lives during the attack, and some persons were also kidnapped. Efforts are ongoing to rescue the kidnapped victims," police spokesperson Wasiu Abiodun said, according to Reuters.</p><p>Abiodun said the gunmen stormed the Daji market in Demo village in Nigeria’s Niger state at around 4:30 p.m. Saturday, torching stalls and seizing food supplies.</p><p>Witnesses reported seeing the gunmen <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/auto/style/motorcycles" target="_blank" rel="noopener">ride into the area on motorcycles</a> before opening fire.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-administration-expands-efforts-stop-christian-violence-nigeria-aid-threat" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TRUMP ADMINISTRATION EXPANDS EFFORTS TO STOP CHRISTIAN VIOLENCE IN NIGERIA WITH AID THREAT</strong></a></p><p>Locals said the assault followed earlier raids in neighboring villages, including Agwarra and Borgu, that began Friday.</p><p>Dauda Shakulle, who was wounded while fleeing, <a href="https://www.reuters.com/world/africa/least-30-killed-nigeria-market-attack-police-say-2026-01-04/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow"><u>told Reuters</u></a> that not even women or children were spared.</p><p>"There has been no <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/police-and-law-enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">presence of security forces</a> since the attacks began. We are currently recovering corpses," said Shakulle.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/lawmakers-sound-alarm-deadliest-place-earth-christian-nigeria-violence-escalates" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>LAWMAKERS SOUND ALARM ON ‘DEADLIEST PLACE ON EARTH TO BE A CHRISTIAN’ AS NIGERIA VIOLENCE ESCALATES</strong></a></p><p>The Nigerian Police Force did not immediately respond to Fox News Digital's request for comment about the incident.</p><p>The attack comes amid a wave of mass kidnappings across Nigeria in recent months, including the abduction of 25 girls from a boarding school in <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/armed-attackers-nigeria-kidnap-25-girls-from-boarding-school" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow"><u>Kebbi State</u></a> in November and the seizure of more than <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/number-children-abducted-from-catholic-school-nigeria-explodes-over-300" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow"><u>300 children</u></a> and 12 teachers from St. Mary’s Private Catholic School in Niger state.</p><p>The Sisters of Our Lady of Apostles (OLA), a Catholic religious congregation, <a href="https://www.vaticannews.va/en/africa/news/2026-01/nigeria-your-compassion-became-a-lifeline-the-gratitude-of-th.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow"><u>later confirmed</u></a> that all those abducted from the school have since been safely released. </p><p>"From the first hours of the abduction, the OLA family—together with the local Church in the Diocese of Kontagora, the families of Papiri, and communities across Nigeria—was upheld by a remarkable global solidarity of prayer and advocacy. We extend our deepest and most heartfelt gratitude to all who stood with us during this painful time," said Sister Mary Barron, the congregation leader of the OLA Sisters.</p><p>Growing insecurity and insurgent violence in northern Nigeria are driving hunger to record levels, with nearly 35 million people projected to face severe food insecurity during the 2026 lean season, according to the United Nations World Food Programme (WFP).</p><p>The <a href="https://www.wfp.org/news/increased-insurgent-attacks-nigeria-threaten-regional-stability-and-drive-sharp-spike-hunger" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow"><u>WFP warned</u></a> in November that unchecked hunger during the lean season, the pre-harvest period when food supplies are often at their lowest, could further destabilize the region by allowing insurgent groups to exploit desperation and expand their influence.</p>
