<p>The United States accused Russia on Monday of a "dangerous and inexplicable escalation" of the war in Ukraine after Moscow launched new attacks on energy and civilian infrastructure in Kyiv, despite ongoing U.S.-led negotiations aimed at ending the conflict.</p><p>U.S. deputy ambassador to the United Nations Tammy Bruce said at an emergency <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/united-nations/security-council" target="_blank" rel="noopener">U.N. Security Council</a> session in New York that Russia’s actions risk expanding and intensifying the war, as the United States continues to work with Ukraine, Europe and Russia to reach a negotiated peace.</p><p>Bruce condemned Russia for its repeated missile and drone strikes on <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/conflicts/ukraine" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Ukraine’s energy facilities</a>, including the launch of its nuclear-capable "Oreshnik" ballistic missile that targeted an area of Ukraine near the border with Poland and NATO.</p><p>"These attacks make a mockery of the cause of peace, a cause of paramount importance to the world and to President Trump," said Bruce.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/only-trump-can-stop-russia-millions-face-freezing-winter-ukraine-energy-executive-warns" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>‘ONLY TRUMP CAN STOP RUSSIA’: MILLIONS FACE FREEZING WINTER, UKRAINE ENERGY EXECUTIVE WARNS</strong></a></p><p>"I would like to remind the Russian Federation of its vote in favor of Security Council Resolution 2774, nearly one year ago. That resolution called for a swift end to the conflict and lasting peace," she added. "It would be nice if Russia matched their words with deeds."</p><p>The UN Human Rights Monitoring Mission in Ukraine (HRMMU) said on Monday that 2025 marked the deadliest year for civilians since Russia launched its full-scale invasion in 2022.</p><p>In its December report, the HRMMU said at least 2,514 civilians were killed and more than 12,000 injured in Ukraine in 2025, a sharp increase over the previous two years.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/russia-alleges-attack-putin-residence-ukraine-denies-claim-ahead-trump-talks" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>RUSSIA ALLEGES ATTACK ON PUTIN RESIDENCE AS UKRAINE DENIES CLAIM AHEAD OF TRUMP TALKS</strong></a></p><p>Long-range missile and drone strikes by Russian forces accounted for a significant share of the casualties, often hitting urban areas far from the front lines, the report said.</p><p>Ukrainian President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/volodymyr-zelenskyy" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Volodymyr Zelenskyy</a> said on Tuesday that Russia launched nearly 300 attack drones, 18 ballistic and 7 cruise missiles at Ukraine, targeting the Dnipro, Zhytomyr, Zaporizhzhia, Kyiv, Odesa, Sumy, Kharkiv, and Donetsk regions.</p><p>He said 4 people were killed in Kharkiv after a missile struck a postal terminal.</p><p>The Ukrainian president wrote on social media that Kyiv is continuing intensive talks <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/foreign-policy/alliances" target="_blank" rel="noopener">with its U.S. and European partners</a>, including the preparation of documents on U.S. security guarantees and plans for reconstruction.</p><p>Russia’s U.N. Ambassador Vassily Nebenzia told the Security Council that his country has never turned away from dialogue.</p><p>Nebenzia said that until Zelenskyy "comes to his senses and agrees to realistic conditions for negotiations, we'll continue solving the problems by military means."</p>
