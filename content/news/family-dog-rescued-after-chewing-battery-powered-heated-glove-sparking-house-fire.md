<p>Firefighters rescued a dog from a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/fires" target="_blank" rel="noopener">burning home</a> in Canada on Saturday morning after the family pet accidentally started a fire by chewing on a battery-powered glove, authorities said.</p><p>Ottawa Fire Services said crews were dispatched just after 9 a.m. to a home in <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/world-regions/canada" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Orl√©ans</a> after a monitoring company reported an active fire alarm.</p><p>The homeowner, who was on the way to the house, confirmed through interior security cameras that a fire was burning inside. Several passing motorists also called 911 to report flames visible in the residence.</p><p>Firefighters arrived within four minutes and found heavy smoke and flames venting from a first-floor bay window. Crews launched an aggressive attack, knocking down flames that had breached the window before entering the home to continue extinguishment efforts.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/fire-chiefs-dog-escapes-unharmed-after-chewing-lithium-ion-battery-sparking-house-fire" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>ASSISTANT FIRE CHIEF'S DOG ESCAPES UNHARMED AFTER CHEWING ON LITHIUM-ION BATTERY, SPARKING HOUSE FIRE</strong></a></p><p>Firefighters searched the house and <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/lifestyle/pets" target="_blank" rel="noopener">found a dog</a>, carrying it safely outside. The pet was not injured, and no people were inside at the time of the fire, officials said.</p><p>No injuries were reported, but one adult and one child were displaced, the department said.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/lifestyle/dramatic-video-shows-firefighters-racing-across-frozen-pond-save-dog-fell-through-ice" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>DRAMATIC VIDEO SHOWS FIREFIGHTERS RACING ACROSS FROZEN POND TO SAVE DOG THAT FELL THROUGH ICE</strong></a></p><p>Officials declared the fire under control at 9:23 a.m., about 10 minutes after crews arrived, preventing further spread. Two searches confirmed no people were inside.</p><p>An Ottawa Fire Services investigator determined the fire started after the family dog chewed on a heated skiing glove containing a lithium-ion battery. Security camera footage showed the dog pulling the glove onto a couch, damaging the battery, which then ignited.</p><p>Fire officials warned that <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/tech" target="_blank" rel="noopener">lithium-ion batteries</a> can pose serious fire risks if damaged and urged residents to store battery-powered items safely and keep them away from pets and children.</p>
