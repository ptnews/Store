Putting our votes where it needs to be. But most importantly, putting our dollars in the right place. And supporting candidates that will fight to protect our interests," one of the Somalian community members says in the recording.</p><p>"That's right," Ellison responds.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/from-congress-vp-nominee-disgraced-former-governor-look-rise-fall-tim-walz" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>FROM CONGRESS, TO VP NOMINEE TO DISGRACED FORMER GOVERNOR: A LOOK AT THE RISE AND FALL OF TIM WALZ</strong></a></p><p>Ellison has denied any wrongdoing regarding the recording, saying he was completely unaware of the fraudsters' crimes at the time of the meeting.</p><p>"I took a meeting in good faith with people I didnâ€™t know and some turned out to have done bad things. I did nothing for them and took nothing from them," Ellison wrote in an April op-ed for the Minnesota Star Tribune.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/house-gop-bill-could-trigger-self-deportation-somali-refugees-amid-minnesota-fraud-probe" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>HOUSE GOP BILL COULD TRIGGER SELF-DEPORTATION FOR SOMALI REFUGEES AMID MINNESOTA FRAUD PROBE</u></strong></a></p><p>Ellison did receive campaign donations from some of the convicted fraudsters, according to the Center for the American Experiment, but he later returned those donations after they were convicted.</p><p>The recording was first unearthed by Minnesota attorney Kenneth Udoibok, who represents Aimee Bock, one of many convicted in the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/judge-clears-the-way-for-minnesota-welfare-fraud-ringleader-to-forfeit-porsche-millions-held-in-accounts" target="_blank" rel="noopener">$250 million "Feeding Our Future"</a> scam last year.
