<p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/candace-cameron-bure" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Candace Cameron Bure</a> is sounding the alarm on what she sees as a growing disconnect in modern relationships.</p><p>Bure, 49, acknowledged how much has changed in the modern dating scene since she found her happily ever after but has given advice based on her kids' romantic experiences.</p><p>"What I believe is lost today is human connection because of our phones, because of social media, because of dating apps," Bure said on <a href="https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/the-lies-we-believe-and-how-to-break-free/id1649600581?i=1000744957016" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">"The Candace Cameron Bure Podcast"</a> during a conversation with Madison Prewett, a "Bachelor" alum. "And I'm not saying that those aren't good tools. I know lots of people that have met on dating apps, or even you, on a dating show.</p><p>"A person's not gonna drop on your front door like an Amazon package," she noted. "So, you do have to put yourself out there and not hide behind your phone or the screen or be afraid to have a real conversation with someone to talk to them on the phone and not just text them."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/entertainment/candace-cameron-bure-admits-surprising-parenting-regret-from-her-decade-stay-at-home-mom" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CANDACE CAMERON BURE ADMITS SURPRISING PARENTING REGRET FROM HER DECADE AS A STAY-AT-HOME MOM</strong></a></p><p>Bure claimed she's seen a growing trend of men being scared to talk to or approach women in today's dating world.</p><p>"I just find that people — and I've seen it in men, I hate to say it — but I have found that there are more guys that I feel like they're scared to talk to women," she explained.
