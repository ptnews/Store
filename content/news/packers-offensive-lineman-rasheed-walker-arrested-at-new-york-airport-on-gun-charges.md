<p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/nfl/green-bay-packers" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Green Bay Packers</a> offensive lineman Rasheed Walker was arrested Friday morning at a New York airport after authorities say he attempted to check a bag containing a firearm.</p><p>The 25-year-old NFL player was charged with two counts of second-degree criminal possession of a weapon and criminal possession of a firearm after he was taken into custody at LaGuardia Airport, the <a href="https://nypost.com/2026/01/23/us-news/green-bay-packers-player-rasheed-walker-arrested-on-gun-charge-at-nycs-laguardia-airport-sources/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow"><u>New York Post</u></a> first reported, citing a criminal complaint. </p><p>Walker allegedly presented a firearm for inspection while attempting to check in at Delta Air Lines at around 10:30 a.m., but he did not have the "proper credentials," a spokesperson with the Port Authority Police Department told Fox News Digital. </p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE FOR MORE SPORTS COVERAGE ON FOXNEWS.COM</strong></a></p><p>According to the criminal complaint, Walker allegedly told an employee that his bag contained a 9 mm Glock pistol in a locked box. Police later found the firearm with 36 rounds of ammunition, also secured in the locked case.</p><p>Walker’s attorney told the Post that the arrest was a result of misunderstanding and that Walker had a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/personal-freedoms/second-amendment" target="_blank" rel="noopener">licensed firearm</a>.  </p><p>"It was in a locked box and he disclosed it to the people at the airport," Arthur Aidala said.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports/vikings-star-arrested-florida-casino-trespassing-charge" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>VIKINGS STAR ARRESTED AT FLORIDA CASINO ON TRESPASSING CHARGE</u></strong></a></p><p>"He told a person from Delta he was traveling with a firearm.
