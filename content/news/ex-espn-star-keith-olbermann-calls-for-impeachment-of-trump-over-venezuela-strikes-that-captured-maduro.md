<p>Former ESPN host Keith Olbermann called for President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Donald Trump's</a> impeachment on Saturday following a U.S. "large scale strike" against Venezuela that targeted the country’s strongman leader, Nicolás Maduro.</p><p>Olbermann, an outspoken critic of Trump, took to social media shortly after Trump confirmed that the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/from-bus-driver-dictator-nicolas-maduros-rise-fall-venezuela" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Venezuelan president</a> and his wife have been taken into custody. </p><p>"Trump has now endangered every man, woman, and child in this nation by his insane and illegal personal war against Venezuela," he said in a post on X. "He must be impeached and removed from office immediately."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE FOR MORE SPORTS COVERAGE ON FOXNEWS.COM</strong></a></p><p>Olbermann’s remarks are similar to those of several <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/democrats" target="_blank" rel="noopener">House Democrats</a> who accused Trump of breaking with the U.S. Constitution in green-lighting the strikes.</p><p>The <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/military" target="_blank" rel="noopener">U.S. military</a> conducted surprise strikes in Venezuela overnight Saturday.
