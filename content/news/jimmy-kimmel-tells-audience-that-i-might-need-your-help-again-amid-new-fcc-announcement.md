<p>Late-night host Jimmy Kimmel told his audience Wednesday that he might need their help again amid a new threat from the Federal Communications Commission (FCC) <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media/fcc-crack-down-liberal-late-night-shows-the-view-not-giving-equal-airtime-gop-guests" target="_blank" rel="noopener">directed at the broadcast</a> networks.</p><p>"The president took time, from 6,000 miles away, to continue his war on <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/entertainment/politics-on-late-night" target="_blank" rel="noopener">talk shows</a>. He shared this story about how his minions at the FCC are planning to make it difficult for shows like ours and 'The View' to interview politicians they don’t align with," Kimmel said. "For real."</p><p>The FCC announced Wednesday it is providing guidance to the three broadcast networks to adhere to the "statutory equal opportunities requirement," citing the Communications Act of 1934, "including their airing of late night and daytime talk shows."</p><p>"Under section 315, if a broadcast station permits any legally qualified candidate for public office to use its facilities, it shall provide an equal opportunity to all other legally qualified candidates for that office," the FCC wrote in a press release.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media/fcc-scrubs-own-reference-independent-agency-from-website-after-dems-testy-exchange-chairman" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>FCC SCRUBS OWN REFERENCE AS 'INDEPENDENT' AGENCY FROM WEBSITE AFTER DEM'S TESTY EXCHANGE WITH CHAIRMAN</strong></a></p><p>"We are once again getting threatened by the FCC. I might need your help again," Kimmel told his audience.</p><p>The statement was met with loud applause.</p><p>The FCC did not immediately respond to a request for comment from Fox News Digital. </p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media/abc-extends-jimmy-kimmels-contract-another-year-following-dramatic-network-saga" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>ABC EXTENDS JIMMY KIMMEL'S CONTRACT ANOTHER YEAR FOLLOWING DRAMATIC NETWORK SAGA</strong></a></p><p>Kimmel went on to acknowledge it was the one-year anniversary of President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Donald Trump</a>'s inauguration on Jan. 20.</p><p>"In honor of his anniversary, I invited President Trump to appear on our show tonight.
