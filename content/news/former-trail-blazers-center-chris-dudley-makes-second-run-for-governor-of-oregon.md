<p>A former <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/nba/portland-trail-blazers" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Portland Trail Blazers</a> player is taking his best shot at being the next governor of Oregon.</p><p>Chris Dudley, a Republican and 16-year <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/nba" target="_blank" rel="noopener">NBA</a> veteran, announced his candidacy for the role in a video posted online.</p><p>"I love Oregon, and even though we have some serious problems, there are solutions and I believe our best days are ahead of us," he said in the video.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE FOR MORE SPORTS COVERAGE ON </strong></a><a href="http://FOXNEWS.COM" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>FOXNEWS.COM</strong></a></p><p>"However, in order to solve these challenges, the empty promises, the name calling, finger pointing and fear mongering that has solved nothing must stop. You deserve better."</p><p>Dudley spent six of his 16 NBA seasons in Portland as the team’s center.</p><p>Dudley, who was born in Connecticut and went to high school in San Diego, <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/west/california" target="_blank" rel="noopener">California</a>, played in Portland from 1993-97 before returning for the 2001-02 season. He played his final two seasons with the Trail Blazers before retirement.</p><p>Dudley came within less than two percentage points of becoming Oregon’s governor in 2010, falling to then-incumbent Democrat John Kitzhaber, 49.3% to 47.8%.</p><p>He joins a field now of 14 Republicans who have all announced they will be running to defeat current Gov. Tina Kotek. Kotek and five other Democrats also announced they are running.</p><p>The Oregon primary election is set for May 19.</p><p>Dudley began his NBA career with the Cleveland Cavaliers, who selected him in the fourth round of the 1987 NBA Draft. He played parts of three seasons there before a trade to the New Jersey Nets.</p><p>Dudley spent four seasons with the Nets before joining the Trail Blazers. After his original four-season stint, he landed with the New York Knicks and Phoenix Suns before his return to Portland.</p><p><i><strong>Follow Fox News Digital’s </strong></i><a href="https://twitter.com/FoxNewsSports_" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><i><strong>sports coverage on X</strong></i></a><i><strong> and subscribe to </strong></i><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/newsletters" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><i><strong>the Fox News Sports Huddle newsletter</strong></i></a><i><strong>.</strong></i></p>
