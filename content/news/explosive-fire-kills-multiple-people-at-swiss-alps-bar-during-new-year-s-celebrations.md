<p>An explosive <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/fires" target="_blank" rel="noopener">fire at a bar</a> in the Swiss Alps killed dozens of people and injured at least 100 others during New Year’s celebrations, police said.</p><p>The blaze happened in the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/travel/general/resorts" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Alpine ski resort municipality</a> of Crans-Montana, Switzerland, police said.</p><p>"The fire started around 1:30 a.m. this morning in a bar called 'Le Constellation,'" police spokesperson Gaëtan Lathion said. "More than a hundred people were in the building, and we are seeing many injured and many dead."</p><p>Swiss police later said "several tens of people" are presumed dead and that around 100 people had been injured – many of them seriously. Authorities could not immediately provide more precise casualty figures. </p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/explosion-pennsylvania-senior-home-prompts-mass-casualty-response" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>EXPLOSION AT PENNSYLVANIA SENIOR HOME PROMPTS MASS CASUALTY RESPONSE</strong></a></p><p>Investigators were working to determine the cause of the fire, police said.</p><p>Valais Canton Attorney General said it was too early to determine the cause of the fire but authorities have ruled out terrorism. </p><p>"We're just at the beginning of our investigation, but this is an internationally renowned ski resort with lots of tourists," Lathion said.</p><p>The number of injured overwhelmed medical resources, with intensive care unit and operating theater at the regional hospital reaching full capacity. Victims were transferred to other nearby hospitals, officials said. </p><p>A reception center and helpline have been established for <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/aftermath" target="_blank" rel="noopener">impacted families</a>, Lathion said.</p><p>Authorities urged local residents and visitors to exercise caution in the coming days to avoid accidents that could further strain emergency medical services during the busy ski season. </p><p>The community is in the heart of the Swiss Alps, about 25 miles north of the Matterhorn.</p><p><i>The Associated Press contributed to this report. </i></p>
