<p><strong>FIRST ON FOX: </strong>An Iowa House Republican is announcing a record-setting <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/elections/gubernatorial" target="_blank" rel="noopener">fundraising haul</a> in his quest for higher office.</p><p>Rep. Randy Feenstra, R-Iowa, raised $4.3 million in seven months of campaigning for governor through 2025, with roughly 2,000 individual contributions.</p><p>It's the most a Republican candidate in Iowa has raised in an off-year without any major elections, his campaign told Fox News Digital.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/republican-sen-joni-ernst-iowa-makes-official-shes-not-seeking-re-election-next-year" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>REPUBLICAN SEN. JONI ERNST OF IOWA MAKES IT OFFICIAL: SHE'S NOT SEEKING RE-ELECTION NEXT YEAR</strong></a></p><p>Whoever wins the governorship in Iowa this November will be a key player in the next presidential election cycle, when national attention will surge to Des Moines ahead of the Iowa caucuses in the race to replace President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Donald Trump</a>.</p><p>"Our campaign set this record of $4.3 million raised because Iowans believe in our vision to take our state to new heights and advance President Trump’s America First agenda in Iowa," Feenstra said in a statement.</p><p>Feenstra, a member of the influential House Agriculture and Ways and Means committees, was first elected to Congress in 2020.</p><p>He currently represents <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/midwest/iowa" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Iowa's heavily rural 4th Congressional District</a>.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/top-gop-senator-steps-up-efforts-protect-republican-majority-2026-midterms" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TOP GOP SENATOR STEPS UP EFFORTS TO PROTECT REPUBLICAN MAJORITY IN 2026 MIDTERMS</strong></a></p><p>The <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/midwest" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Midwest lawmaker</a> is considered the polling and fundraising frontrunner for the GOP gubernatorial nomination in this year’s race to succeed retiring longtime GOP Gov. Kim Reynolds. </p><p>The conservative governor, who has served in office since 2017, announced last April that she wouldn’t seek re-election in 2026.</p><p>Also running for the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/elections" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Republican nomination</a> are Adam Steen, former director of the Iowa Department of Administrative Services, state Rep. Eddie Andrews, and former state Rep. Brad Sherman.</p><p>Iowa Auditor Rob Sand is seen as the frontrunner to become the gubernatorial Democratic nominee.</p><p>The Cook Report, a leading nonpartisan political handicapper, rates the Iowa gubernatorial race as Lean Republicans. Another top handicapper, Inside Elections, ranks the race as Likely Republican.</p>
