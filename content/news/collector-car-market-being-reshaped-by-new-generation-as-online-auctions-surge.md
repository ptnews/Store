<p>The <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/auto/attributes/collector-cars" target="_blank" rel="noopener">collector car market</a> is evolving, with recent trends reshaping how enthusiasts buy and sell classic cars.</p><p>Purchasing habits, auction formats, even the types of cars commanding top prices are shifting, recent data shows.</p><p>New analysis by Michigan-based automotive data and insurance company Hagerty examined auction sales, buyer demographics and high-end transactions across global markets over the past year.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/travel/dolly-parton-unveils-her-favorite-nashville-destinations-amid-new-travel-stop-partnership" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>DOLLY PARTON UNVEILS FAVORITE NASHVILLE DESTINATIONS AMID NEW TRAVEL STOP PARTNERSHIP</strong></a></p><p>Online auctions extended their advantage in 2025, selling more than 50,000 collector vehicles, up 6% from a year earlier. </p><p>Live auction volume remained flat, at roughly 21,000 vehicles, <a href="https://www.hagerty.com/media/market-trends/hagerty-insider/by-the-numbers-the-collector-car-market-in-2025/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">the same analysis</a> said.</p><p>The highest-priced transaction in the United States involved a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/auto/make/ferrari" target="_blank" rel="noopener">2025 Ferrari Daytona SP3</a> at $26 million, followed by a 1961 Ferrari 250 California SWB at $25.3 million.</p><p>The shift goes beyond where cars are sold; it also reflects those who are buying them.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/newsletters?cmpid=fnfirstnl"><strong><u>CLICK HERE TO SIGN UP FOR OUR LIFESTYLE NEWSLETTER</u></strong></a></p><p>Kenneth Ahn, president of Hagerty Marketplace, told Fox News Digital the collector car world is undergoing a generational handoff as younger buyers enter the market.</p><p>"While the overall global collector car auction market — live and online combined — has remained relatively flat in terms of total transaction value since 2022, we have been closely following several interesting changes occurring within that market," Ahn said.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/lifestyle"><strong><u>CLICK HERE FOR MORE LIFESTYLE STORIES</u></strong></a></p><p>One of the biggest changes, he said, is the growing demand for newer collectible vehicles, particularly <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/auto/attributes/performance" target="_blank" rel="noopener">modern supercars</a> and so-called "youngtimer" models from the 1980s through early 2000s.</p><p>That trend is increasingly visible in top sales, with newer supercars taking more <a href="https://www.foxbusiness.com/money" target="_blank" rel="noopener">high-dollar spots</a> than before.</p><p>Models like the Ferrari F40 and F50, the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/auto/make/maserati" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Maserati MC12</a> and the Pagani Huayra are among the modern-era vehicles drawing strong interest from collectors.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/lifestyle/quizzes"><strong><u>TEST YOURSELF WITH OUR LATEST LIFESTYLE QUIZ</u></strong></a></p><p>Hagerty data shows that while modern cars are gaining momentum, top-tier classic cars continue to command strong prices when rarity and condition align.</p><p>"Over the past five years, the average age of seven-figure ($1M+) cars sold at auction has become nearly 12 years newer," Ahn said.</p><p>Looking ahead, long-term shifts in ownership and wealth expected to unfold through 2048 are likely to shape the next era of collector cars, Ahn said.</p>
