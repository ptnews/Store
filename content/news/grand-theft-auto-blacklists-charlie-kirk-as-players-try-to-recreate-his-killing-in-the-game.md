<p>Rockstar Games, the company behind the popular video game Grand Theft Auto, has reportedly added "<a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/media/charlie-kirk" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Charlie Kirk</a>" to a list of blocked terms for user-generated content in the online game after some players created missions recreating Kirk's assassination.</p><p>Kirk, the founder of Turning Point USA, was shot and killed while speaking at a campus event at Utah Valley University Sept. 10, 2025.</p><p>In December, <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/tech/topics/video-games" target="_blank" rel="noopener">GTA Online</a> rolled out a new Mission Creator tool allowing players to design and share their own missions for others to play. </p><p>Variety and gaming outlets reported Tuesday that some players used the feature to create assassination-style missions that appear to recreate Kirk’s murder.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/chicago-schools-silent-on-punishment-or-public-condemnation-of-teacher-who-mocked-charlie-kirks-death" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CHICAGO SCHOOLS SILENT ON PUNISHMENT OR PUBLIC CONDEMNATION OF TEACHER WHO MOCKED CHARLIE KIRK'S DEATH</strong></a></p><p><a href="https://kotaku.com/gta-online-charlie-kirk-killing-assassination-missions-rockstar-deleting-censor-block-2000659038" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">A viral video</a> cited by gaming outlet Kotaku shows one such mission, "We are Charlie Kirk," which has since reportedly been taken down. </p><p>The mission puts a player with a sniper rifle on a rooftop overlooking a college campus setting, similar to that of the Utah Valley University setting where Kirk was shot. </p><p>The player's mission is to shoot a character named Charlie Kirk, who is standing under a tent in front of a crowd of people. </p><p>Rockstar Games has reportedly responded by adding "Charlie Kirk" to its "profanity filter" in the game's code.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media/federal-appeals-court-sides-washington-professor-punished-mocking-university-land-acknowledgment" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>FEDERAL APPEALS COURT SIDES WITH WASHINGTON PROFESSOR PUNISHED FOR MOCKING UNIVERSITY LAND ACKNOWLEDGMENT</strong></a></p><p><a href="https://variety.com/2026/gaming/news/gta-online-charlie-kirk-assassination-mission-rockstar-1236630176/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">According to Variety</a>, the gaming company plans to change the filter's name to more appropriately describe content it does not want users to bring into the game, such as real-life events like Kirk's assassination.</p><p>Kotaku reported that some players have tried to get around this filter by using the name "Charlie Pink" or other variations of his name to continue making these missions referring to his killing.</p><p>Rockstar Games did not immediately respond to Fox News Digital's request for comment.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE FOR MORE COVERAGE OF MEDIA AND CULTURE</strong></a></p><p>After Kirk was killed, some figures on the left took to social media to mock or celebrate his murder, <a some cases.</p><p>Last week, a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/ncaa/clemson-tigers" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><u>Clemson University</u></a> professor who was fired over a social media post calling Kirk's murder "karma" reached a settlement with the university. </p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media/tennessee-professor-fired-over-charlie-kirk-post-reinstated-reportedly-receives-large-financial-settlement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Austin Peay State
