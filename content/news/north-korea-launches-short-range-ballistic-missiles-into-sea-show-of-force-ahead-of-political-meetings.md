<p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/conflicts/north-korea" target="_blank" rel="noopener">North Korea</a> is flexing its muscles and firing short-range ballistic missiles toward its waters ahead of a major political meeting.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/world-regions/south-korea" target="_blank" rel="noopener">South Korea</a>'s military detected the launches of several ballistic missiles from an area northeast of Pyongyang, North Korea, adding that each missile flew approximately 217 miles, <a href="https://apnews.com/article/north-south-korea-projectile-launched-9bb07a5ffb0782a27d0dbdf7b6871978" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">The Associated Press</a> reported. </p><p>Meanwhile, the Japanese Defense Ministry said that two ballistic missiles launched from North Korea and landed off the coast of the Korean Peninsula, according to the AP.</p><p>These launches represent the first time North Korea fired weapons since testing hypersonic missiles in early January, the AP noted. In December, the country tested long-range strategic cruise missiles and new anti-air missiles and released photos of the apparent construction of a nuclear-powered submarine, which would be the first of its kind for Pyongyang, the AP reported.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/north-korea-test-launches-hypersonic-missile-system-front-kim-nation-says" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>NORTH KOREA TEST-LAUNCHES HYPERSONIC MISSILE SYSTEM IN FRONT OF KIM, NATION SAYS</strong></a></p><p>The missile launches come as the ruling party prepares to hold its first full congress in five years, according to <a href="https://apnews.com/article/north-korea-kim-party-congress-8ea11e99798794e237d6856e1f3ad58c" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">the AP</a>, which cited state media. So far, North Korean leader <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/kim-jong-un" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Kim Jong Un</a> has met with other top officials to prepare for the meeting. The top-level meeting will reportedly be used to set new political and economic priorities as the U.S. and South Korea seek renewed talks with North Korea.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/north-korea-releases-images-development-nuclear-powered-submarine" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>NORTH KOREA RELEASES IMAGES OF DEVELOPMENT OF NUCLEAR-POWERED SUBMARINE</strong></a></p><p>The Korean Central News Agency (KCNA), which is controlled by the North Korean government, reported in December that Kim presided over a plenary meeting of the Workers Party’s Central Committee in which participants discussed issues related to the party congress and this year's state policies, the AP reported. The outlet noted that Kim revived the congress in 2016 after a 36-year hiatus.</p><p>Additionally, North Korea recently accused South Korea of carrying out surveillance drone flights across the border. Seoul has denied operating drones during the times Pyongyang specified and said it began investigating the possibility that civilians sent them.</p><p><i>The Associated Press contributed to this report.</i></p>
