<p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/new-york-city" target="_blank" rel="noopener">New York City</a> Mayor Eric Adams took to social media Wednesday to share a unique farewell tribute on his final day in office, reading some of his "go-to quotes" off a coffee mug.</p><p>"<a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/ericadams" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Mayor Adams</a>' greatest hits, you know," he said while waving around an inscribed cup gifted to him by his staff.</p><p>He then attempted to read some of the quotes aloud, giggling in between each phrase.</p><p>"'I wake up in the morning sometimes and look at myself and give myself the finger,'" he read. "‘Stay focused, no distractions, and grind.’ … 'It's not what's in the tweet, it's what's in the streets.'</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/20-nyc-mayor-elect-mamdani-transition-appointees-have-anti-zionist-ties-adl" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>20% OF NYC MAYOR-ELECT MAMDANI TRANSITION APPOINTEES HAVE ANTI-ZIONIST TIES: ADL</strong></a></p><p>"'There are two types of Americans, those who live in New York and those who wish they could,'" he continued. "'Arrested, rejected, and <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/elections" target="_blank" rel="noopener">now I'm elected</a>.' ‘I am Gandhi-like.’ ‘I had a shorty that lived out there.’ ‘Every morning I wake up and turn on my GPS, my God positioning satellite.' … Get your mug, man!"</p><p>He thanked his City Hall team for the parting gift in an accompanying X post and noted, "It’s been an honor, New York!"</p><p>Adams was sworn in as the 110th Mayor of New York City Jan. 1, 2022, after serving as Brooklyn borough president and a New York state senator.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media/adl-chief-warns-nyc-mayor-elect-zohran-mamdani-poses-clear-present-danger-jewish-community" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>ADL CHIEF WARNS NYC MAYOR-ELECT ZOHRAN MAMDANI POSES A 'CLEAR AND PRESENT DANGER' TO JEWISH COMMUNITY</u></strong></a></p><p>Mayor-elect <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/zohran-mamdani" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Zohran Mamdani</a> will be sworn in at midnight Thursday during a private ceremony held in a decommissioned subway station, with a second public inaugural ceremony taking place Thursday afternoon at City Hall.</p><p>Mamdani, a 34-year-old Ugandan-born socialist, will be the first Muslim mayor of New York City and the first mayor to be sworn in using <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/religion/islam" target="_blank" rel="noopener">the Quran</a>, the central religious text of Islam.</p><p>It is unclear if Adams' adorned <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/food-drink/drinks/coffee" target="_blank" rel="noopener">coffee mugs</a> will be listed for sale to the public.</p>
