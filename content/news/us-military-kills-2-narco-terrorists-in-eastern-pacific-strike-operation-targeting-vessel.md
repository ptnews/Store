<p>U.S. Southern Command said Joint Task Force Southern Spear carried out its first lethal strike since Dec. 22 on Dec. 29, killing <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/terrorism/narco-terror" target="_blank" rel="noopener">two narco-terrorists</a> aboard a vessel operated by designated terrorist organizations in international waters.</p><p>"On Dec. 29, at the direction of [Defense Secretary <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/pete-hegseth" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Pete Hegseth</a>,] Joint Task Force Southern Spear conducted a lethal kinetic strike on a vessel operated by Designated Terrorist Organizations in international waters," SOUTHCOM wrote on X, adding that intelligence confirmed the vessel was traveling along known narco-trafficking routes in the Eastern Pacific.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-admin-announces-deadly-attack-low-profile-vessel-perpetrating-narco-trafficking-operations" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TRUMP ADMIN CONDUCTS ANOTHER DEADLY ATTACK ON ‘LOW-PROFILE VESSEL' PERPETRATING ‘NARCO-TRAFFICKING OPERATIONS’</strong></a></p><p>SOUTHCOM said two male narco-terrorists were killed and no U.S. military forces were harmed.</p><p>The strike was the 30th since Sept. 2, bringing the total number of people killed in such operations to at least 106, according to U.S. Southern Command.</p>
