<p>A woman's death in <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/west/colorado" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Colorado</a> is suspected to have been caused by a mountain lion attack, according to Colorado Parks and Wildlife, which notes that the last fatal mountain lion attack in the state occurred in 1999.</p><p>"Around 12:15 this afternoon, hikers on the Crosier Mountain trail in Larimer County observed a mountain lion near a person lying on the ground from about 100 yards away," a Colorado Parks and Wildlife spokesperson <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jmfQI2gB8V0" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">explained</a> during a news conference on Thursday. </p><p>The <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/travel/general/camping-hiking" target="_blank" rel="noopener">hikers</a> then scared the mountain lion off by tossing rocks at it, she said.</p><p><a WALKING WITH FAMILY AT WASHINGTON'S OLYMPIC NATIONAL PARK</strong></a></p><p>"One of the witnesses is a physician," but "did not find a pulse," she noted, adding that the incident is being Larimer County Sheriff deputies, Estes Park police and Glen Haven Area Volunteer firefighters responded to the area to launch an extensive search for any mountain lions.
