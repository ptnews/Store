<p>New York City’s chapter of the Democratic Socialists of America (DSA) is organizing a large-scale effort to mobilize thousands of activists to respond to anticipated federal immigration enforcement actions, The New York Post reported.</p><p><a href="https://nypost.com/2026/01/18/us-news/nyc-socialists-mustering-army-of-4000-anti-ice-activists-to-bring-minnesota-tactics-to-big-apple/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow"><u>The Post said</u></a> DSA leaders outlined plans at a recent meeting to train roughly 4,000 volunteers, both members and non-members, to participate in what they describe as "rapid response" actions aimed at monitoring and, in some cases, disrupting Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) operations across the city.</p><p>"This has been in the past in New York specifically enough to deter ICE detentions," said a DSA leader who only went by the name Marina, according to the outlet.</p><p>Organizers also pointed to protests and confrontations involving ICE agents <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/midwest/minnesota" target="_blank" rel="noopener">in Minnesota</a> as a model for the tactics they hope to bring to New York.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/opinion/gregg-jarrett-trump-has-authority-send-troops-minneapolis-stop-attacks-ice" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>GREGG JARRETT: TRUMP HAS AUTHORITY TO SEND TROOPS TO MINNEAPOLIS TO STOP ATTACKS ON ICE</strong></a></p><p>Those tactics include using whistles and other noise-making tools to alert neighborhoods to ICE activity and quickly draw activists to the scene.</p><p>"The <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/immigration/immigrant-rights" target="_blank" rel="noopener">whistles carry far and wide</a>," said Marina, according to the Post.</p><p>The Department of Homeland Security did not immediately respond to Fox News Digital’s request for comment.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media/minnesota-faculty-union-calls-economic-blackout-protest-ice-operations-minneapolis" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>MINNESOTA FACULTY UNION CALLS FOR 'ECONOMIC BLACKOUT' TO PROTEST ICE OPERATIONS IN MINNEAPOLIS</strong></a></p><p>Protests in Minnesota have erupted since the fatal shooting of 37-year-old Renee Nicole Good by an ICE agent during an immigration enforcement operation.</p><p>Across the state, protests against ICE have drawn demonstrators holding signs reading, "ICE out for good," "Quit your job," and "Resist," while federal law enforcement officers have <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/federal-judge-restricts-ice-agents-amid-ongoing-minneapolis-area-protests-over-fatal-shooting" target="_blank" rel="noopener">deployed tear gas to</a> disperse crowds outside the Bishop Henry Whipple Federal Building in St. Paul.</p><p>President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Donald Trump</a> has threatened to invoke the Insurrection Act, sending U.S. military troops to the Twin Cities.</p><p>Trump on Friday appeared to downplay his remarks, telling reporters that it wasn’t needed at the moment.</p><p>"I don't think there's any reason right now to use it, but if I needed it, I'd use it. It's very powerful," he said.</p><p>Fox News confirmed on Sunday that the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/trump-admin-preparing-1500-soldiers-potential-minnesota-deployment-report" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Army’s 11th Airborne Division</a> was recently placed on prepare-to-deploy orders for Minnesota, though it remains unclear whether the unit will ultimately be sent.</p><p><i>Fox News' Jennifer Griffin contributed to this report.</i></p>
