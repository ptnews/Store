<p>Iranian protesters issued a direct appeal for help from <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener">President Donald Trump</a> on Tuesday as unrest continued to spiral across the nation for the tenth day.</p><p>The appeal, shared on X, showed a woman holding up a sign with the words "Trump, a symbol of peace. Don't let them kill us," on it.</p><p>The woman's cry for help came amid reports of at least 29 deaths and more than 1,200 arrests, according to the <a href="https://www.en-hrana.org/detailed-report-on-the-tenth-day-of-protests-36-dead-in-285-demonstrations/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">Human Rights Activists News Agency</a> (HRANA).</p><p>HRANA also reported an escalation in force by security units, including the use of pellet guns, tear gas and direct assaults on demonstrators.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/iran-protests-turn-deadly-anti-regime-demonstrations-enter-fifth-day" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>IRAN PROTESTS TURN DEADLY AS ANTI-REGIME DEMONSTRATIONS ENTER FIFTH DAY</strong></a></p><p>The National Council of Resistance of Iran (NCRI) also claimed the cities of Abdanan (Ilam province) and Malekshahi were effectively "taken over" by protesters.</p><p>"Today there was a major development in two cities in Western Iran that have been effectively taken over and people are actually celebrating in the streets," Ali Safavi, told Fox News Digital. "They were <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/iran-shutdown-protests-intensify-amid-leadership-shake-up-deepening-crisis" target="_blank" rel="noopener">chanting, ‘Death to Khamenei!’</a>"</p><p>"Despite everything the regime has done, the fear factor seems to have shifted because people have made the suppressive forces flee," Safavi added.</p><p>Maryam Rajavi, NCRI president-elect also shared a post on X saluting the protesters in Malakshahi and Abdanan who she said, "drove the regime's enforcers into retreat."</p><p>Demonstrations, strikes and street gatherings have also been reported across dozens of cities in multiple provinces.</p><p>In Ilam province, witnesses and rights groups reported that tear gas was fired inside Imam Khomeini Hospital as authorities attempted to arrest wounded protesters transferred from nearby towns, according to <a href="https://www.iranintl.com/en/202601061698" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">reports.</a></p><p>Amnesty International said on Tuesday, "The Iranian security forces’ attack on a hospital in Ilam, where injured protesters are seeking medical care or shelter, <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/judiciary/international-law" target="_blank" rel="noopener">violates international law</a>."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/iranian-protesters-clash-security-forces-tear-gas-fills-tehran-streets-amid-nationwide-unrest" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>IRANIAN PROTESTERS CLASH WITH SECURITY FORCES AS TEAR GAS FILLS TEHRAN STREETS AMID NATIONWIDE UNREST</strong></a></p><p>Similar accounts emerged from Tehran, where security forces were reportedly seen entering Sina Hospital, prompting fear among patients and families.</p><p>As previously reported by Fox News Digital, the unrest has been driven by a mix of economic desperation and political anger.</p><p>What started as labor strikes and merchant protests over currency collapse and inflation has expanded into <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/world-protests" target="_blank" rel="noopener">widespread street demonstrations</a> and student protests.</p><p>Parts of Tehran’s Grand Bazaar and major commercial centers in Mashhad were also partially or fully closed, according to recent reports.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-signs-make-iran-great-again-hat-alongside-lindsey-graham" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TRUMP SIGNS 'MAKE IRAN GREAT AGAIN' HAT ALONGSIDE LINDSEY GRAHAM</strong></a></p><p>"What makes the 2025 protests different to before is that the knife has reached the bone for Iranian citizens," Safavi continued.</p><p>"People feel they have nothing anymore and have reached a breaking point," he added.</p><p>Exiled Crown Prince Reza Pahlavi also <a href="https://x.com/IranIntl_En/status/2008611423103185098" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">urged Iranians to chant</a> protest slogans nationwide on Thursday and Friday evenings.</p><p>Sen. <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/lindsey-graham" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Lindsey Graham,</a> R-S.C., warned Iran’s leadership that continued violence against protesters could carry serious consequences, writing on X that regime leaders should understand they act "at their own peril."</p><p>His remarks came after a recent warning from Trump, who said on Truth Social that the U.S. was "<a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/defense" target="_blank" rel="noopener">locked and loaded and ready</a> to go," in the wake of the rising protests.</p>
