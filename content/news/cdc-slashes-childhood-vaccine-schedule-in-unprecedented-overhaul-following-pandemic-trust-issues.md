<p>The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) announced Monday an <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-signs-off-nationwide-vaccine-schedule-review-cdc-withdraws-infant-hep-b-guidance" target="_blank" rel="noopener">overhaul of the U.S.</a> childhood vaccine schedule, slashing the number of recommended immunizations.</p><p>Under the new guidelines, which are to take effect immediately, children across the U.S. will be recommended to get vaccines for 11 diseases, down from 18 under the previous schedule.</p><p>Senior officials at the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/health/health-care" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Department of Health and Human Services</a> said the change is meant to rebuild confidence in public health institutions after trust was lost during the COVID-19 pandemic.</p><p>Health Secretary <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/rfk-jr-defends-firing-spree-cdc-vows-new-blood-agency" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Robert F. Kennedy Jr. discussed</a> the shift during remarks at the White House, framing it as part of a broader effort to address declining vaccination adherence.</p><p>"The loss of trust during the pandemic not only affected COVID-19 vaccine uptake," a scientific assessment cited by the CDC said.</p><p><i>This is a developing story. Check back for updates. The Associated Press contributed to this report.</i></p>
