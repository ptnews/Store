<p>The Department of Justice (DOJ) on Wednesday outlined a list of its accomplishments during President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Donald Trump</a>’s first year back in office, arguing that the agency has ended the political weaponization it says existed under the Biden administration.</p><p>The DOJ claimed in a statement posted on X that it has "turned around" the agency, restoring fairness and law enforcement priorities.</p><p>"Instead of keeping Americans safe, the Biden DOJ weaponized its power against political opponents: conservatives, parents, pro-lifers, Christians, and most of all, President Trump," <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/justice-department" target="_blank" rel="noopener">the DOJ</a> stated.</p><p>The DOJ said that after President Trump inherited a justice system it described as "in chaos," he charged the department with restoring "integrity, accountability and equal justice under the law."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/fbi-director-kash-patel-backs-trumps-dc-police-takeover-when-you-let-good-cops-cops-deliver" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>FBI DIRECTOR KASH PATEL BACKS TRUMP'S DC POLICE TAKEOVER: 'WHEN YOU LET GOOD COPS BE COPS, THEY DELIVER'</strong></a></p><p>"In 2025, the DOJ returned to its core mission: upholding the rule of law, vigorously prosecuting criminals, and keeping the American people safe," the department wrote.</p><p>The announcement comes as the Trump administration continues to face legal challenges and the Justice Department faces potential legal action after missing a statutory deadline<strong> </strong>deadline to <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/dojs-epstein-disclosure-draws-fire-website-glitches-missing-documents-redactions" target="_blank" rel="noopener">release documents related to Jeffrey Epstein</a> under the Epstein Files Transparency Act.</p><p>The DOJ outlined 10 "wins" since President Trump took office on Jan. 20, including efforts to pursue major fraud cases, particularly in Minnesota, which it described as "rife with fraud."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/opinion/mike-davis-justice-coming-perpetrators-arctic-frost" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>MIKE DAVIS: JUSTICE IS COMING FOR PERPETRATORS OF ARCTIC FROST</strong></a></p><p>According to the DOJ, 98 people have been charged — including 85 individuals identified as being of Somali descent — in Medicaid fraud and related case programs, leading to 64 convictions to date.</p><p>The statement outlines actions taken to roll back policies it said were targeting conservatives and parents, reduce crime nationwide, <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/police-and-law-enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">increase law enforcement activity</a> in major cities, seize record amounts of illegal drugs and secure favorable rulings at the Supreme Court.</p><p>On Wednesday, FBI Director <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/kash-patel" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Kash Patel</a> wrote on X that the bureau is working to restore trust in federal law enforcement.</p><p>"Dismantling <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/corruption" target="_blank" rel="noopener">public corruption</a> is a top priority of our leadership team here — we’ve worked day and night on that mission and will continue to do so until justice is done," he wrote.</p><p>The Justice Department said more enforcement actions are planned in 2026, signaling an escalation of arrests, court victories and action "against those who threaten the safety and well-being of the American people."</p>
