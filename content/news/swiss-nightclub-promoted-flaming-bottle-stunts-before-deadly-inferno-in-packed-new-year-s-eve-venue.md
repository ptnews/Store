<p>The luxury Swiss Alps nightclub where at least 40 people were killed and nearly 120 others were injured in a New Year’s Eve inferno, had previously released a promotional video advertising <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/fires" target="_blank" rel="noopener">flaming champagne bottles</a>, which authorities deemed the cause of the tragedy.</p><p>Le Constellation, a bar in the resort town of Crans-Montana, Switzerland, posted the video on YouTube showing waitresses in helmets moving through the dark, crowded club carrying bottles topped with sparkling candles.</p><p>In <a href="https://www.youtube.com/shorts/OCgRHRpe6OA" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">the footage</a>, the bottles appear to be held high, at times nearly touching the ceiling.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/explosion-pennsylvania-senior-home-prompts-mass-casualty-response" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>EXPLOSION AT PENNSYLVANIA SENIOR HOME PROMPTS MASS CASUALTY RESPONSE</strong></a></p><p>The video surfaced following a deadly blaze that tore through the basement nightclub at about 1:30 a.m. Thursday during a New Year’s Eve celebration.</p><p>At least 40 people were killed, and 119 others were injured, many seriously, when flames and smoke spread rapidly through the packed venue.</p><p>According to two witnesses, a bartender was carrying a female staff member on his shoulders while she held a champagne bottle fitted with a lit sparkler or flare near the wooden ceiling above the bar area.</p><p>Moments later, fire erupted overhead.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/overnight-fire-india-nightclub-leaves-25-dead" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>OVERNIGHT FIRE AT INDIA NIGHTCLUB LEAVES 25 DEAD</strong></a></p><p>Valais Attorney General Beatrice Pilloudsaid confirmed on Friday the boozy fireworks were likely the cause, with the flames triggering a violent ignition of combustible gases known as a flashover, causing the flames to spread suddenly.</p><p>"The whole ceiling was in flames, and the fire spread really fast. It happened in seconds," two survivors later told the French broadcaster BFMTV.</p><p>Axel Clavier, 16, of Paris, said he felt as though he was suffocating as smoke filled the club.</p><p>Clavier told The <a href="https://apnews.com/article/switzerland-crans-montana-bar-fire-ff049a656af75e65703c5febb908786c" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">Associated Press</a> he escaped by using a table to force a plexiglass pane out of its frame amid what he described as "total chaos." </p><p>He also said he saw waitresses arrive with champagne bottles and sparklers.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/fire-tears-through-hong-kong-housing-complex-killing-least-others-reported-trapped" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>FIRE TEARS THROUGH HONG KONG HOUSING COMPLEX, KILLING AT LEAST 13 WITH OTHERS REPORTED TRAPPED</strong></a></p><p>As of Friday afternoon, <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/news-events" target="_blank" rel="noopener">no arrests have been confirmed.</a></p><p>Pilloud said investigators will work to determine if anyone bears criminal liability for fire by negligence, homicide by negligence, or injuries by negligence.</p><p>Since the tragedy, hundreds of people have gathered outside the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/aftermath" target="_blank" rel="noopener">burned-out venue</a> in Crans-Montana, laying flowers and candles as families waited for news of missing loved ones.</p><p>Italian authorities said the death toll could be as high as 47, <a href="https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/swiss-face-arduous-task-identifying-victims-deadly-bar-fire-2026-01-01/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener"><u>Reuters reported</u></a>, citing information provided by Swiss officials, who continue working to identify bodies.</p><p>Swiss President Guy Parmelin described <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/disasters" target="_blank" rel="noopener">the disaster</a> as "one of the worst tragedies our country has ever known," noting that many of the victims were young people celebrating the New Year.</p><p>While the majority of those hurt were Swiss, four Serbians were injured, along with others from Bosnia, Belgium and Portugal.</p><p>Italy and France confirmed some of their nationals remain missing.</p><p>Le Constellation did not immediately respond to Fox News Digital's request for comment.</p><p><i>Fox News Digital's Michael Dorgan contributed to this report.</i></p>
