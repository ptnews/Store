<p>The U.S. State Department updated its <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/travel/vacation-destinations/jamaica" target="_blank" rel="noopener">travel advisory for Jamaica,</a> changing the guidance from a Level 3 to a Level 2.</p><p>Officials decreased the advisory from "reconsider travel" to "exercise increased caution" on Jan. 17.</p><p>"There were no changes to the risk indicators," the advisory noted. It advised individuals to "exercise increased caution due to crime, health and natural disaster risks."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/travel/influencers-face-backlash-tragedy-tourism-hurricane-melissa" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>INFLUENCERS FACE BACKLASH FOR 'TRAGEDY TOURISM' DURING HURRICANE MELISSA</strong></a></p><p>Jamaica is popular among <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/travel/spring-break-nation" target="_blank" rel="noopener">spring breakers</a> for its all-inclusive resorts that cater to lively partiers in Negril and Montego Bay.</p><p>About 4.1 million people visited the island in 2023 alone, according to Jamaica’s Ministry of Tourism.</p><p>In October, <a href="https://www.foxweather.com/extreme-weather/hurricane-melissa-registers-unearthly-252-mph-wind-reading" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Hurricane Melissa</a> devastated Jamaica, causing widespread damage to the western part of the island.</p><p>While all major airports have reopened since, "some areas are still experiencing post-storm impacts on infrastructure and services," according to the advisory.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/newsletters?cmpid=fnfirstnl" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE TO SIGN UP FOR OUR LIFESTYLE NEWSLETTER</strong></a></p><p>Some health care facilities in western Jamaica are still recovering after the hurricane damage.</p><p>"Violent crime is a risk throughout Jamaica.
