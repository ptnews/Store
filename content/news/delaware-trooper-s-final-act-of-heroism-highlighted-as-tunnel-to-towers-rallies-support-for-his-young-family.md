<p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/police-and-law-enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Delaware State Police</a> Cpl. Matthew "Ty" Snook was shot and killed at a DMV office while working an overtime shift just two days before Christmas. </p><p>Now, a national charity says it will pay off his mortgage for the wife and 1-year-old daughter he left behind.</p><p>Frank Siller, CEO of the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/season-hope-t2ts-gift-independence-dignity-injured-heroes" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Tunnel to Towers Foundation, </a>shared the story of the 10-year veteran state trooper's heroic final moments and announced the initiative to support the family.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/delaware-state-trooper-killed-dmv-shooting-incident-gunman-dead-no-active-threat-officials" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>DELAWARE STATE TROOPER KILLED IN DMV SHOOTING INCIDENT, GUNMAN ALSO DEAD WITH NO ACTIVE THREAT: OFFICIALS</strong></a></p><p>Siller said Snook sacrificed himself by pushing another person to safety, urging them to run as he was <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/blue-lives-lost" target="_blank" rel="noopener">shot a second time</a> by his attacker in what Fox News Channel's Lawrence Jones described as an "execution-style" killing.</p><p>"He's a hero," said Siller, who founded Tunnel to Towers after losing his brother <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/terror/september-11" target="_blank" rel="noopener">on 9/11</a>. The nonprofit supports first responders, military service members and their families.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/tech/teens-medical-invention-saves-lives-seconds" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TEEN'S MEDICAL INVENTION SAVES LIVES IN SECONDS</strong></a></p><p>"We lost 343 firefighters on 9/11. Our goal next year is 343 mortgage payoffs," said Siller on <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media" target="_blank" rel="noopener">"Fox &amp; Friends."</a> </p><p>"Let this be the first one."</p><p>He added that he aimed to pay off Snook's mortgage by Jan. 5, the day of the state trooper's funeral service.</p><p>Siller said he would be visiting Snook's widow, Lauren, to show support and tell her that "America cares." He wants Tunnel to Towers to "be with [Snook's family] for the rest of their lives," not just in the wake of their great loss.</p><p>Siller also said another widow, whose <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/conflicts/afghanistan" target="_blank" rel="noopener">husband was killed in Afghanistan</a>, would reach out to Lauren as part of the foundation’s effort to bring Snook’s family "into the Tunnel to Towers family."</p>
