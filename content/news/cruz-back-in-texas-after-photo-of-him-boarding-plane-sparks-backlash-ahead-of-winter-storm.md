<p>Sen. <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/ted-cruz" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Ted Cruz</a>, R-Texas, returned to the Lone Star State Friday after a photo went viral on social media of him boarding a plane as the state prepares for a potentially historic winter storm.</p><p>The senator appeared to poke fun at himself on Friday as online chatter swirled over the past few days about whether he would once again be absent as his constituents hunkered down during a major weather event.</p><p>"I’ve returned home from my work trip. It’s 66 degrees &amp; beautiful. A storm is expected tomorrow night," Cruz wrote in an X post Friday.</p><p>"But I am reliably informed by Twitter that if I simply raise up my hand on <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/southwest/texas" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Texas soil</a>, the storm will turn around &amp; sunshine, rainbows &amp; unicorns will emerge. Let it be," he continued.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-mocks-environmental-insurrectionists-americans-brace-massive-winter-storms-global-warming" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TRUMP MOCKS 'ENVIRONMENTAL INSURRECTIONISTS' AS AMERICANS BRACE FOR MASSIVE WINTER STORMS: 'GLOBAL WARMING?'</strong></a></p><p>In 2021, Cruz was slammed for <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/travel/vacation-destinations/cancun" target="_blank" rel="noopener">traveling to Cancun, Mexico</a>, with his family the same week that Texas dealt with a winter storm that left millions of people without power and ultimately caused 246 deaths, according to the <a Services.</a></p><p>Cruz later admitted that the decision was "obviously a mistake."</p><p>His comments Friday came after a photo was posted on X on Tuesday showing Cruz aboard a plane that was reportedly heading to <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/west/california" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Laguna Beach, California.</a></p><p><a WINTER STORM IMPACTING THE NATION</strong></a></p><p>The post quickly went viral, gaining more than 9 million views, with users both defending and blasting the senator.</p><p>"I’m noticing a pattern,"
