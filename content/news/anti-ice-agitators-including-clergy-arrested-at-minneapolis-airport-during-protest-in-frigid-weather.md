<p>Multiple anti-ICE agitators were arrested at the Minneapolis-St. Paul International Airport (<a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/minneapolis-st-paul" target="_blank" rel="noopener">MSP</a>) Friday after a crowd of protesters showed up despite frigid temperatures demanding airlines take a stand against federal immigration enforcement.</p><p>Faith in Minnesota, a group of religious activists "calling for racial and economic justice in Minnesota," posted a video of the arrests on social media, claiming more than 100 <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/religion" target="_blank" rel="noopener">clergy and faith leaders</a> were arrested.</p><p>Agitators were seen lined up on their knees, as police led them one by one to a bus for transport to jail.</p><p>"In -20° weather, over 100 clergy and faith leaders were arrested at MSP Airport as they gathered in peaceful, prayerful resistance to demand airlines—especially Delta and Signature Aviation—stand with Minnesotans and say #ICEOutOfMN," the organization wrote. "… This must end."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/vice-president-jd-vance-visit-minneapolis-source-says-amid-unrest-over-ice-operations" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>VICE PRESIDENT JD VANCE TO VISIT MINNEAPOLIS, SOURCE SAYS, AMID UNREST OVER ICE OPERATIONS</strong></a></p><p>Law enforcement has not yet confirmed the number of arrests.</p><p>Faith in Minnesota alleged <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/immigration/deportation" target="_blank" rel="noopener">more than 2,000 deportations</a> have run through the airport, claiming MSP employees have been detained by ICE at work and while commuting.</p><p>The Metropolitan Airports Commission told affiliate <a href="https://www.fox9.com/news/ice-out-protests-mn-jan-23-2026" target="_blank" rel="nofollow MSP Airport."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/minnesota-police-chiefs-allege-some-ice-agents-racially-profiled-us-citizens-including-off-duty-officers" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>MINNESOTA POLICE CHIEFS ALLEGE SOME ICE AGENTS RACIALLY PROFILED US CITIZENS, INCLUDING OFF-DUTY OFFICERS</u></strong></a></p><p>The <a
