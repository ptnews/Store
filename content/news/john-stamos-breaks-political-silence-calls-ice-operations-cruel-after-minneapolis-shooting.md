<p>"Full House" star <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media" target="_blank" rel="noopener">John Stamos</a> broke his silence on Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) operations as backlash against Border Patrol’s fatal Minneapolis shooting on Saturday grows.</p><p>In a handwritten note posted to Stamos’ Instagram story on Monday, the actor condemned ICE as "cruel."</p><p>"I usually stay out of politics," Stamos’ note began. "This doesn’t feel like that. What I’m seeing from ICE feels cruel."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/minneapolis-st-paul" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Minneapolis residents</a> Renee Good and Alex Pretti were fatally shot by federal agents during immigration enforcement operations in January, sparking thousands to protest in the city.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media/ny-post-wsj-ny-times-washington-post-align-against-trump-admin-over-ice-operation-minneapolis" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>NY POST, WSJ, NY TIMES AND WASHINGTON POST ALIGN AGAINST TRUMP ADMIN OVER ICE OPERATION IN MINNEAPOLIS</strong></a></p><p>"<a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/border-patrol-commander-gregory-bovino-leave-minnesota-tom-homan-takes" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Border czar" Tom Homan</a> will meet with Minneapolis Mayor Jacob Frey, President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Donald Trump</a> announced, amid multiple reports some Border Patrol agents will be leaving Minnesota "imminently."</p><p>Stamos doubled down on his criticism of ICE, adding he trusts his "own eyes."</p><p>"When people are treated like threats instead of human beings, when fear is the tactic; something is broken," he argued.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports/ex-nba-star-rips-federal-agents-after-shooting-minnesota" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>EX-NBA STAR RIPS FEDERAL AGENTS AFTER SHOOTING IN MINNESOTA</strong></a></p><p>While the actor did not explicitly name members of the Trump administration, nor those killed by law enforcement, he ended the statement on an ominous note.</p><p>"Lately it feels like the bad guys are winning," Stamos said.</p><p>Stamos also responded to conservatives’ common criticism of liberal actors to "stay in your lane."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media/stephen-king-compares-ice-nazi-germanys-gestapo-amid-growing-celebrity-criticism" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>STEPHEN KING COMPARES ICE TO NAZI GERMANY'S GESTAPO AMID GROWING CELEBRITY CRITICISM</strong></a></p><p>"[That line] doesn’t apply when <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/immigration/immigrant-rights" target="_blank" rel="noopener">basic humanity</a> is the lane," he said. "This is all of our lane!"</p><p>The 62-year-old went on to criticize "the hypocrisy," again failing to mention any names.</p><p>"The speed of the cycle has erased shame," he added. "This doesn’t feel right, and staying quiet feels worse."</p><p>"Maybe the least we can do is not look away," he ended his note.</p><p>Investigations into Good’s and Pretti’s deaths remain ongoing.</p><p>Fox News Digital reached out to Stamos’ publicist for comment but did not receive a comment in time for publication.</p>
