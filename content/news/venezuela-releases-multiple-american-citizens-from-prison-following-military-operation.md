<p>The interim government in Venezuela has released at least four U.S. citizens who were imprisoned under President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/nicolas-maduro" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Nicolás Maduro</a>'s regime, Fox News confirmed.</p><p>The release marks the first known release of Americans in the South American country since the U.S. military completed an <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/inside-lightning-us-strike-overwhelmed-venezuelas-defenses-seized-maduro" target="_blank" rel="noopener">operation to capture authoritarian Venezuelan President</a> Nicolás Maduro, who is now facing federal drug trafficking charges in New York.</p><p>"We welcome the release of detained Americans in Venezuela," a State Department official said Tuesday. "This is an important step in the right direction by the interim authorities."</p><p>The release of American citizens was first reported by Bloomberg.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-signs-order-protect-venezuela-oil-revenue-held-us-accounts" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TRUMP SIGNS ORDER TO PROTECT VENEZUELA OIL REVENUE HELD IN US ACCOUNTS</strong></a></p><p>President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Donald Trump</a> said Saturday that Venezuela had begun releasing political prisoners.</p><p>"Venezuela has started the process, in a BIG WAY, of releasing their political prisoners," Trump wrote on Truth Social. "Thank you! I hope those prisoners will remember how lucky they got that the USA came along and did what had to be done."</p><p>Venezuela's interim government has reported that 116 prisoners have been released, although only about 70 have been verified by the non-governmental organization Justicia, Encuentro y Perdón, according to Bloomberg.</p><p>National Assembly President Jorge Rodríguez said prisoner releases would continue, according to the outlet.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-administration-files-seizure-warrants-targeting-ships-tied-venezuelan-oil-trade-report" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TRUMP ADMINISTRATION FILES SEIZURE WARRANTS TARGETING SHIPS TIED TO VENEZUELAN OIL TRADE: REPORT</strong></a></p><p>The U.S. government issued a new security alert Saturday <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/us-warns-americans-leave-venezuela-immediately-armed-militias-set-up-roadblocks" target="_blank" rel="noopener">urging Americans in Venezuela</a> to leave the country immediately, citing security concerns and limited ability to provide emergency assistance, the U.S. Embassy in Caracas said.</p><p>"U.S. citizens in Venezuela should leave the country immediately," the embassy said in the alert.</p><p>The warning pointed to reports of armed groups operating on Venezuelan roads.</p><p>Following the military operation, Trump suggested that <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/us-may-involved-venezuela-years-trump-says" target="_blank" rel="noopener">the U.S. would "run" Venezuela</a> for an extended period.</p><p>"We’re going to run the country until such time as we can do a safe, proper and judicious transition," he said.</p>
