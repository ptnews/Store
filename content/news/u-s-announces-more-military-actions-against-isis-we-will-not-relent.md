<p>U.S. Central Command announced on Tuesday that the U.S. and partner forces have terminated or captured nearly 25 <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/terrorism/isis?page=4" target="_blank" rel="noopener">ISIS</a> figures since a December 19 strike in Syria.</p><p>"U.S. Central Command (CENTCOM) and partners across Syria killed at least seven ISIS members and captured the remainder during 11 missions conducted Dec. 20-29. The operations also led to the elimination of four ISIS weapons caches," an article posted by the CENTCOM X account said.</p><p>"These recent missions followed the launch of Operation Hawkeye Strike on Dec. 19 when U.S. and Jordanian forces struck over 70 targets with more than 100 precision munitions. The massive strike executed by dozens of fighter aircraft, attack helicopters and artillery destroyed ISIS infrastructure and weapons sites across central Syria," the article explained.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-targets-isis-nigeria-amid-warnings-sahel-region-becoming-epicenter-terrorism" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TRUMP TARGETS ISIS IN NIGERIA AMID WARNINGS SAHEL REGION IS BECOMING ‘EPICENTER OF TERRORISM’</strong></a></p><p>The U.S. and partner forces in Syria have carried out activity in the last 12 months that led to more than 300 terrorists getting detained and more than 20 being killed, according to CENTCOM.</p><p>The CENTCOM post noted that this year, there have been "at least 11 plots or attacks against targets in the United States" that were inspired by ISIS.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/war-sec-hegseth-issues-statement-after-two-u-s-soldiers-killed-syria-identified" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>WAR SEC HEGSETH ISSUES STATEMENT AFTER TWO US SOLDIERS KILLED IN SYRIA ARE IDENTIFIED</strong></a></p><p>"We will not relent," CENTCOM Commander Adm. Brad Cooper said in a statement. "We are steadfast in commitment to working with regional partners to root out the ISIS threat posed to U.S. and regional security."</p><p>"Continuing to hunt down terrorist operatives, eliminate ISIS networks, and work with partners to prevent an ISIS resurgence makes America, the region, and the world safer," Cooper added.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-orders-isis-strikes-syria-iowa-national-guards-soldiers-killed" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>US LAUNCHES ‘VENGEANCE’ ATTACK ON ISIS TARGETS AFTER NATIONAL GUARD SOLDIERS KILLED</strong></a></p><p>Two Iowa <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/military" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Army National Guard</a> soldiers and a civilian interpreter were killed earlier this month in Syria. The soldiers were later identified as Sgt. Edgar Brian Torres-Tovar, 25, of Des Moines, Iowa, and Sgt. William Nathaniel Howard, 29, of Marshalltown, Iowa. The interpreter was Ayad Mansoor Sakat, 54.</p>
