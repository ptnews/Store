<p>Iran appeared to reopen its airspace on Thursday after a sudden overnight closure disrupted flights across the region, even as <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/travel/general/airlines" target="_blank" rel="noopener">airlines largely continued to avoid</a> the country amid heightened security warnings and ongoing regional tensions. </p><p>The overnight closure lasted around five hours after a Notice to Air Missions, or NOTAM, <a href="https://safeairspace.net/iran/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow"><u>was issued</u></a>, according to the aviation risk-monitoring site Safe Airspace. </p><p>Safe Airspace warns that the main danger for civil aircraft in Iranian airspace is misidentification by air defense systems during heightened tensions, rating the overall risk level currently as "One — Do Not Fly."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media/trump-says-u-s-take-very-strong-action-against-iran-regime-starts-hanging-protesters" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TRUMP SAYS THE U.S. WILL TAKE 'VERY STRONG ACTION' AGAINST IRAN IF THE REGIME STARTS HANGING PROTESTERS</strong></a></p><p>President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Donald Trump</a> told reporters in the Oval Office on Wednesday that he was informed the killing of protesters in Iran was "stopping."</p><p>"We have been notified and really strongly, but we'll find out what that all means. But, we've been told that the killing in Iran is stopping," he said.</p><p>Sen. <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/lindsey-graham" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Lindsey Graham</a>, R-S.C., disagreed with Trump, writing on X that every indication he’s seen shows that the Iranian regime’s killing of anti-government protesters is "still very much in full swing."</p><p>"The death toll is mounting by the hour. Hoping that help is on the way," he wrote.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/trump-envoy-reportedly-meets-exiled-iranian-prince-regime-faces-protests" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TRUMP ENVOY REPORTEDLY MEETS WITH EXILED IRANIAN PRINCE AS REGIME FACES PROTESTS</strong></a></p><p>The U.S.-based Human Rights Activists News Agency (HRANA), <a href="https://www.en-hrana.org/day-eighteen-of-the-protests-global-focus-on-casualties-and-mass-arrests-amid-communications-blackout/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow"><u>which tracks</u></a> human rights violations in Iran, said on Wednesday that nationwide protests continued into the 18th day as authorities maintained a near-total internet shutdown.</p><p>The group’s aggregated figures showed 617 protest gatherings in 187 cities across the country, the arrest of at least 18,470 people and the confirmed deaths of 2,615 individuals.</p><p>HRANA said 2,435 of those killed were protesters, including 13 children under the age of 18.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/top-iranian-official-downplays-death-toll-blames-israeli-plot-us-considers-strikes" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TOP IRANIAN OFFICIAL DOWNPLAYS DEATH TOLL, BLAMES 'ISRAELI PLOT' AS US CONSIDERS STRIKES</strong></a></p><p>Trump has <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-announces-final-25-tariff-countries-doing-business-iran-regime" target="_blank" rel="noopener">threatened action against the regime</a>, warning Tehran in multiple Truth Social posts to stop killing its people.</p><p>"We are locked and loaded and ready to go," he wrote on New Year’s Day.</p><p>The president said on Tuesday that he had canceled all meetings with Iranian officials and called on protesters to "TAKE OVER YOUR INSTITUTIONS!!!"</p><p>"Save the names of the killers and abusers. They will pay a big price," Trump wrote, adding, "HELP IS ON ITS WAY."</p>
