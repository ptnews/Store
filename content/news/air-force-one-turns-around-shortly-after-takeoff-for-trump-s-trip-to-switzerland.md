<p>Air Force One was forced to return to Joint Base Andrews shortly after takeoff Tuesday evening with President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Donald Trump</a> aboard, the White House said.</p><p>The crew experienced a "minor electrical issue" after takeoff at 10:20 p.m. and returned "out of an abundance of caution," according to <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/executive/white-house" target="_blank" rel="noopener">White House</a> Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt.</p><p>The lights in the press cabin briefly went out after takeoff, reporters on board said.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-says-youll-find-out-when-asked-how-far-hell-go-greenland-takeover" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TRUMP SAYS 'YOU’LL FIND OUT' WHEN ASKED HOW FAR HE’LL GO ON GREENLAND TAKEOVER</strong></a></p><p>Trump was en route to Davos, Switzerland, to <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-head-davos-greenland-dispute-sparks-rising-tensions-nato-allies" target="_blank" rel="noopener">attend the World Economic Forum</a>.</p><p>The plane returned to Maryland around 45 minutes into the roughly 7.5 hour flight.</p><p>Leavitt joked aboard Air Force One that a Qatari jet sounded "much better" at the moment.</p><p>Air Force One landed at Joint Base Andrews at 11:07 pm. The president boarded a different aircraft and continued on to Switzerland shortly after 12 a.m.</p><p>Trump is traveling with White House staff and cabinet members, including Secretary of State <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/person/r/sen-marco-rubio" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Marco Rubio</a> and White House Chief of Staff Susie Wiles.</p>
