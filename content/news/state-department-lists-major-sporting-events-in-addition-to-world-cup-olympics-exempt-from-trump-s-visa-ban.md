<p>The Trump administration has revealed various "major sporting events" in addition to the 2026 World Cup and the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/olympics" target="_blank" rel="noopener">2028 Olympic Games</a> in which athletes and coaches will be exempt from a broad visa ban on nearly 40 countries, allowing them to travel to the U.S. to compete.</p><p>In a cable sent Wednesday to all U.S. embassies and consulates, the State Department said athletes, coaches and support staff for the World Cup, the Olympics and events endorsed or run by a lengthy list of collegiate and professional sporting leagues and associations would be excluded from the full and partial travel bans subject to citizens of 39 countries and the Palestinian Authority.</p><p>But foreign spectators, media and corporate sponsors who wish to attend the events would still be impacted by the ban unless they qualify for another exemption.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports/trump-touts-2026-world-cup-draw-success-claims-massive-viewership" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TRUMP TOUTS 2026 WORLD CUP DRAW SUCCESS, CLAIMS MASSIVE VIEWERSHIP</strong></a></p><p>"Only a small subset of travelers for the World Cup, Olympics and Paralympics, and other major sporting events will qualify for the exception," the message said.</p><p>The federal government has issued several immigration and travel bans as well as other <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/us-freezes-all-visa-processing-75-countries-including-somalia-russia-iran" target="_blank" rel="noopener">visa restrictions</a> as part of President Donald Trump's efforts to curb immigration, although the administration still wants athletes, coaches and fans to be able to attend major sporting events in the U.S.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-expands-travel-ban-hit-5-countries-sweeping-new-restrictions-citing-security-concerns" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Trump's proclamation last month</a> banning the issuance of visas to the 39 countries and the Palestinian Authority had included an exception for athletes and staff competing in some sporting events such as the World Cup and the Olympics, and a decision on the other sporting events that would be covered would be made by Secretary of State Marco Rubio.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/travel/world-cup-fans-banned-from-us-travel-may-unable-root-teams-person" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>WORLD CUP FANS BANNED FROM US TRAVEL MAY BE UNABLE TO ROOT FOR TEAMS IN PERSON</strong></a></p><p>The events covered, according to the cable, include all competitions and qualifying events for the Olympic Games, Paralympic Games, Pan American Games and Parapan American Games; events hosted, sanctioned or recognized by a U.S. National Governing Body; all competitions and qualifying events for the Special Olympics; and official events and competitions hosted or endorsed by FIFA or its confederations.</p><p>Official events and competitions hosted by the International Military Sports Council, the International University Sports Federation and the National Collegiate Athletic Association as well as those hosted or endorsed by U.S. professional sports leagues such as the National Football League, the National Basketball Association and Women’s National Basketball Association, Major League Baseball and Little League, National Hockey League, Professional Women’s Hockey League, NASCAR, Formula 1, the Professional Golf Association, Ladies Professional Golf Association, LIV Golf, Major League Rugby, Major League Soccer, World Wrestling Entertainment, Ultimate Fighting Championship and All Elite Wrestling are also covered under the exemption.</p><p>Other events and leagues could be added to the list in the future, the cable said.</p><p>Under the new visa restrictions, a full travel ban covers citizens of Afghanistan, Burkina Faso, Chad, the Republic of the Congo, Equatorial Guinea, Eritrea, Haiti, Iran, Laos, Libya, Mali, Myanmar, Niger, Sierra Leone, Somalia, Sudan, South Sudan, Syria, Yemen and individuals holding Palestinian Authority–issued passports.</p><p>A partial ban applies to citizens of Angola, Antigua and Barbuda, Benin, Burundi, Cuba, Dominica, Gabon, Gambia, Ivory Coast, Malawi, Mauritania, Senegal, Tanzania, Tonga, Togo, Venezuela, Zambia and Zimbabwe.</p><p><i>The Associated Press contributed to this report.</i></p>
