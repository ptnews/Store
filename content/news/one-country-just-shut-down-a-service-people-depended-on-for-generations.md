<p>Sending and receiving a letter is now a thing of the past in one country. </p><p>PostNord, Denmark's state-run postal service, officially ended all letter deliveries at the start of the new year, citing a more than 90% decline in volume from 2000 to 2024.</p><p>The <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/travel/regions/europe" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Nordic nation</a> is believed to be the only country in the world to discontinue its letter deliveries, though package distribution is ongoing.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/lifestyle/usps-postmark-timing-may-change-some-mail-shipping-prices-also-set-rise" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>USPS POSTMARK TIMING MAY CHANGE FOR SOME MAIL, SHIPPING PRICES ALSO SET TO RISE</strong></a></p><p>"Although this is a difficult decision, it is an important step toward a strong PostNord for the future, so we can continue our journey towards becoming the Danes' preferred parcel courier — and which will help ensure even better products in the parcel market," PostNord said in a message on its website.</p><p>PostNord said it will switch its focus to parcel deliveries and that any stamps purchased in 2024 or 2025 can be refunded for a limited time in 2026.</p><p>"We have been the Danes' postal service for 400 years, and therefore it is a difficult decision to put a bow on that part of our story," PostNord Denmark Chief Executive Kim Pedersen said in a news release announcing the company's plans.</p><p>He added that the "market for letters is no longer profitable."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/travel/american-hot-spot-slapped-worst-tourist-destination-ranking-global-analysis"><strong><u>AMERICAN HOT SPOT SLAPPED WITH 'WORST' TOURIST DESIGNATION IN NEW GLOBAL ANALYSIS</u></strong></a></p><p>The decision likely impacts <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/health/geriatric-health" target="_blank" rel="noopener">elderly residents</a> the most, according to Marlene Rishoj Cordes with Aeldre Sagen (DaneAge).</p><p>Although PostNord said the "vast majority" of Danes use the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/tech" target="_blank" rel="noopener">digital post</a> service, many elderly residents are "very dependent" on letters being delivered regularly, Cordes told Denmark's TV2.</p><p>"These include <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/health/health-care" target="_blank" rel="noopener">hospital appointments, vaccinations</a> or decisions regarding home care," she said.</p><p>PostNord said it started removing the 1,500 mailboxes located throughout the country over the summer.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/newsletters?cmpid=fnfirstnl" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE TO SIGN UP FOR OUR LIFESTYLE NEWSLETTER</strong></a></p><p>"Look closely at the picture here.
