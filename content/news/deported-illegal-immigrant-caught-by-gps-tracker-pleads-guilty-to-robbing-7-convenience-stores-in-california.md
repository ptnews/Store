<p>A previously deported <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/immigration/illegal-immigrants" target="_blank" rel="noopener">illegal immigrant</a> pleaded guilty Tuesday to a series of armed robberies targeting multiple convenience stores in California, according to the U.S. Attorney’s Office for the Central District of California.</p><p>Jesús Soto-Parada, 27, from El Salvador, was arrested on June 13, 2024, while fleeing a robbery at a 7-Eleven near <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/los-angeles" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Los Angeles</a>, after a clerk placed a hidden GPS device with the cash that they stole. Soto-Parada and an accomplice allegedly robbed stores across eight cities over a five-month period last year, the Department of Justice said last year.</p><p>Soto-Parada is suspected of 11 thefts but admitted to committing seven of the charged cases across Los Angeles and Orange counties from January to June 2024, authorities said.</p><p>Federal prosecutors noted that Soto-Parada pleaded guilty to eight felonies, including one count of conspiracy to interfere with commerce by robbery and seven counts of robbery under the Hobbs Act, a federal law that makes it a crime to rob or extort businesses in a way that affects commerce.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/worst-worst-10-most-violent-illegal-immigrants-nabbed-2025" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>'WORST OF THE WORST': THE 10 MOST VIOLENT ILLEGAL IMMIGRANTS NABBED IN 2025</strong></a></p><p>Soto-Parada also had <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/immigration" target="_blank" rel="noopener">multiple prior immigration violations</a> before his robbery spree, including arrests by federal authorities in 2016 and 2022, according to federal prosecutors.</p><p>Authorities said that during his robbery spree in March 2024, Soto-Parada was arrested and subsequently deported to El Salvador the following month. He later returned to the United States and reportedly continued to commit robberies.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/previously-deported-illegal-immigrant-charged-california-armed-robbery-spree-doj" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>PREVIOUSLY DEPORTED ILLEGAL IMMIGRANT CHARGED IN CALIFORNIA ARMED ROBBERY SPREE: DOJ</strong></a></p><p>Police were able to apprehend Soto-Parada and his accomplice, Daniel Pavon, during their most recent robbery at a 7-Eleven on June 13, thanks to a hidden GPS device the clerk had placed in the $250 they stole, which allowed officers to track their movements.</p><p>During the robbery, Soto-Parada, wearing a blue surgical mask and black sweatshirt, emptied the register while Pavon aimed a black replica handgun at the clerk, according to a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/police-and-law-enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">criminal complaint</a>.</p><p>In total, the suspects stole around $13,950 in cash and $7,415 in merchandise.</p><p>Sentencing for Soto-Parada is scheduled for April 15, 2026. He could face up to 20 years in federal prison for each count, prosecutors said.</p><p><i>Fox News' Louis Casiano contributed to this report.</i></p>
