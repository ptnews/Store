<p>Billy Ray Barnes, a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/nfl/philadelphia-eagles" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Philadelphia Eagles</a> legend, died at age 90 Wednesday, the team announced.</p><p>The Eagles released a statement Friday night saying Barnes died in <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/southeast/north-carolina" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Landis, North Carolina,</a> surrounded by his family and friends.</p><p>"He was 90 and had a fabulous life," Barnes’ daughter, Billi Barnes Akins, said in the Eagles’ announcement.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>CLICK HERE FOR MORE SPORTS COVERAGE ON </strong></a><a href="http://FOXNEWS.COM"><strong>FOXNEWS.COM</strong></a></p><p>Barnes was the team’s second-round pick in the 1957 NFL Draft out of Wake Forest, and he was a key member of the Eagles’ 1960 championship team.</p><p>During his career, he earned three Pro Bowl honors in his five seasons with the franchise.</p><p>"One of the things that he loved about the Eagles is, in that championship game, they were the underdogs," Akins added. "Dad always liked being the underdog. He said it made him tougher, made him stronger, made him play harder.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports/tom-brady-doesnt-want-healthy-players-resting-week-18-iron-sharpens-iron" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TOM BRADY DOESN'T WANT HEALTHY PLAYERS RESTING IN WEEK 18: ‘IRON SHARPENS IRON’</strong></a></p><p>"He said the only thing that allowed them to win was their team. They were a team that was really tight and believed in each other."</p><p>Barnes was a major contributor to that championship team’s offense, leading the team in rushing and receiving as a halfback.</p><p>Through his five seasons in Philadelphia, Barnes rushed for 2,391 yards with 20 touchdowns, while hauling in 120 passes for 1,275 yards with eight scores through the air.</p><p>"The fondest memory of everything up there is the fans," Barnes said in the 2021 feature, "Where Are They Now?" "I played there for five years, but I lived there year-round for about eight years. The people were just great to me in Philadelphia."</p><p>After his time in Philadelphia, Barnes played two seasons each with the Minnesota Vikings and the Washington Redskins.</p><p>Then, after his playing days, Barnes served as an assistant coach for the New Orleans Saints and <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/nfl/atlanta-falcons" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Atlanta Falcons</a>.</p><p>The Eagles had Barnes and his teammates return to Philadelphia for their 50-year reunion of the 1960 championship during the 2010 campaign.</p><p>"He never realized how people thought of these guys as the greatest ever," Akins recalled. "People you wouldn’t even know would just call him and reach out to him.</p><p>"The biggest thing was the friendships that he had made there. They were lifelong. He talks to a bunch of guys on the phone for years. He enjoyed everything about Philadelphia."</p><p><i><strong>Follow Fox News Digital’s </strong></i><a href="https://twitter.com/FoxNewsSports_" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><i><strong>sports coverage on X</strong></i></a><i><strong> and subscribe to </strong></i><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/newsletters" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><i><strong>the Fox News Sports Huddle newsletter</strong></i></a><i><strong>.</strong></i></p>
