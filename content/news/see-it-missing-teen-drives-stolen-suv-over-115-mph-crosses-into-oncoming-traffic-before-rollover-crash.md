<p>Dashcam video captured the moment a stolen SUV, driven by a 17-year-old, barreled through a Georgia residential area and flipped in a rollover crash after leading deputies on a 115-mph chase.</p><p>Video released by the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/police-and-law-enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Fayette County Sheriff’s Office</a> shows the teen refusing to stop for deputies, speeding along busy roadways and driving into oncoming traffic during the pursuit.</p><p>The sheriff’s office said deputies were alerted to a stolen vehicle reported out of Newnan, Georgia, a city approximately 40 miles southwest of Atlanta, shortly before 2:45 p.m. on Wednesday. After hearing the description, Lt. Josh Akin began searching for a red SUV.</p><p>After the red SUV blew past Akin and Deputy Sheriff Nelson near Antioch Road, the driver, police said, ignored an attempted traffic stop and initiated a high-speed flight.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/georgia-teen-arrested-after-father-turns-him-following-pair-shootings-police-say"><strong>GEORGIA TEEN ARRESTED AFTER FATHER TURNS HIM IN FOLLOWING PAIR OF SHOOTINGS, POLICE SAY</strong></a></p><p>Dashcam video shows the stolen SUV swerving into oncoming traffic and hitting speeds over 115 mph on narrow, winding roads.</p><p>Recognizing the "extreme risk" to the public, deputies said they were on the verge of calling off the pursuit just as the vehicle lost control and overturned.</p><p>After the crash, video shows deputies surrounding the overturned vehicle with guns drawn and ordering the driver to put his hands up before taking him into custody.</p><p>Authorities arrested 17-year-old Taylor Simpson following the crash. Simpson had been <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/topic/missing-persons" target="_blank" rel="noopener">reported missing</a> by the Newnan Police Department. The teen was removed from the vehicle and did not suffer major injuries, according to the sheriff’s office.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/newsletters" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>SIGN UP TO GET TRUE CRIME NEWSLETTER</strong></a></p><p>Simpson was charged with multiple traffic offenses — including speeding, reckless driving, running a stop sign, passing on a solid yellow line, driving without a valid license and driving too fast for conditions — as well as giving a false name to an officer, obstruction of an officer, theft by receiving stolen property and fleeing or attempting to elude police, according to jail records.</p><p>Bond was set at $650 for each of six traffic charges, $1,300 for giving a false name to an officer and $1,300 for obstruction of an officer, totaling $6,500.
