<p>Golden, retrieved.</p><p>Dramatic photos and video show firefighters crawling across dangerously thin ice to rescue a golden retriever that fell through a frozen pond during a morning walk.</p><p>Firefighters in <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/northeast/rhode-island" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Rhode Island</a> raced across dangerously thin ice to rescue a pooch that fell through a frozen pond during a morning walk with its owner, dramatic photos and <a href="https://www.facebook.com/reel/922207240486262" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">video show</a>.</p><p>The rescue unfolded just before 9 a.m. on New Year's Day at Little Masachaug Pond, according to the Misquamicut Fire Department.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/la-deputies-caught-camera-racing-foggy-ocean-rescue-disoriented-paragliders" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>LA DEPUTIES CAUGHT ON CAMERA RACING INTO FOGGY OCEAN TO RESCUE DISORIENTED PARAGLIDERS</strong></a></p><p>Officials said the dog — identified by the fire department in a Facebook post as "Phoenix, a golden retriever" — had wandered onto the pond and fallen through a thin layer of ice near the center, leaving the animal struggling in freezing water while its owner watched from the shore.</p><p>Firefighters from multiple departments responded within minutes, donning specialized ice rescue suits and carefully crawling across the unstable surface to reach the dog.</p><p>According to the Misquamicut Fire Department, crews were dispatched alongside the Westerly Ambulance Corps and Westerly Police Department after receiving reports of a dog trapped in the icy pond.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/nypd-officer-leaps-freezing-river-save-teenage-girl-from-drowning" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>NYPD OFFICER LEAPS INTO FREEZING RIVER TO SAVE TEENAGE GIRL FROM DROWNING</strong></a></p><p>"Volunteers were on scene within minutes and located Phoenix, a golden retriever, struggling in the water and unable to move with his owner safely on shore," the department said in a Facebook post.</p><p>Deputy Chief Howard requested additional help from the Watch Hill Fire Department, which brought specialized ice rescue equipment to the scene.</p><p>Firefighters wearing bright orange and red ice rescue suits were seen crawling across the frozen pond while tethered with safety ropes as chunks of ice broke around them.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/unsung-heroes-2025-first-responders-everyday-americans-who-saved-lives-across-us" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>UNSUNG HEROES OF 2025: FIRST RESPONDERS AND EVERYDAY AMERICANS WHO SAVED LIVES ACROSS US</strong></a></p><p>"Members from both fire departments quickly donned ice rescue suits and entered the pond, making a swift and successful rescue," the department said.</p><p>Additional personnel staged equipment on shore, including an inflatable rescue boat, as crews worked to pull <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/lifestyle/pets" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Phoenix from the icy water</a>.</p><p>Phoenix and the firefighters were safely brought back to shore and "were all doing well when on scene," officials said.</p><p>Firefighters from both departments were evaluated by emergency medical services for possible hypothermia but did not require treatment.</p><p>The dramatic rescue quickly drew praise on social media.</p><p>"God bless you for saving that pup. Responders like you remind us that there is good and kindness," a Facebook user wrote.</p><p>Another commenter added, "We are all so lucky to have such great fire and rescue departments. A very lucky dog!"</p><p>Fire officials used the incident to remind the public of the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/great-outdoors" target="_blank" rel="noopener">dangers posed by frozen ponds</a>.</p><p>"No ice is ever safe," the department warned, noting that while firefighters train extensively for ice and cold-water rescues, the situations remain extremely dangerous.</p><p>The rescue marked the Misquamicut Fire Department’s first call of 2026, with officials praising the quick response and teamwork displayed by all agencies involved.</p><p>"All of the Chiefs from MFD and WHFD extend kudos to everyone involved for their quick response and teamwork this morning," the department said.</p><p>The Misquamicut Fire Department did not immediately respond to Fox News Digital's request for additional comment.</p>
