<p>Ueli Kestenholz, the Swiss snowboard pioneer who won bronze at snowboarding’s Olympic debut, has died after an <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/disasters/avalanche" target="_blank" rel="noopener">avalanche in Switzerland</a>. He was 50.</p><p>Kestenholz was snowboarding Sunday in the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/world-regions/europe" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Lötschental valley of Valais</a> when he was swept away and buried in the avalanche, the Swiss ski federation said Tuesday.</p><p>He was with a friend who was skiing in the valley when the avalanche started at an altitude of 7,900 feet (2,400 meters), Valais police said in a statement. Officials said it remained unclear what triggered the avalanche.</p><p>The friend dug out the trapped Kestenholz before a helicopter airlifted them to a hospital, police said.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports/us-olympic-gold-medal-snowboarder-chloe-kims-milan-cortina-hopes-doubt-after-shocking-injury" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>US OLYMPIC GOLD MEDAL SNOWBOARDER CHLOE KIM'S MILAN-CORTINA HOPES IN DOUBT AFTER SHOCKING INJURY</strong></a></p><p>Kestenholz helped write Olympic snowboarding history when he was thrust into the spotlight at the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/olympics" target="_blank" rel="noopener">1998 Winter Games</a> in Nagano, Japan, at just 22 years old.</p><p>The Games that year marked snowboarding’s debut as an Olympic event. Kestenholz took home the bronze medal for Switzerland in the parallel giant slalom — the first snowboard decision in Olympic history, the Swiss ski federation said.</p><p>The Swiss racer competed at two more Winter Games, was twice snowboardcross champion at the X-Games and continued a professional career in extreme sports.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports/fbi-says-mexican-authorities-seize-40m-motorcycles-from-olympic-snowboarder-turned-fugitive" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>MEXICAN AUTHORITIES SEIZE $40M IN MOTORCYCLES FROM OLYMPIC SNOWBOARDER TURNED FUGITIVE, FBI SAYS</strong></a></p><p>The ski federation said Kestenholz should be remembered not only for his love of snowboarding, but for being "a <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports" target="_blank" rel="noopener">true crossover athlete</a>."</p><p>"After retiring from competitive sports in 2006, he remained a professional outdoor athlete — until his last breath," <a href="https://www.swiss-ski.ch/newsroom/news/in-tiefer-trauer-um-ueli-kestenholz" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">the federation said</a>, noting that Kestenholz was a freerider, speed rider, paraglider pilot, kite surfer, skydiver, surfer, wingfoiler and mountain biker.</p><p>The Olympic medalist’s Instagram account showed numerous photos of the athlete taking part in extreme sports outdoors.</p><p>"To enjoy those rare moments when nature’s wonders align you need to be ready to drop everything and go!" he wrote in one post of a video showing him paragliding and landing on frozen lakes near St. Moritz.</p><p><i>The Associated Press contributed to this report.</i></p>
