<p>A surgeon accused of killing his ex-wife and her dentist husband in Ohio was extradited from Illinois and is in jail in <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/midwest/ohio" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Columbus, Ohio</a>, according to a spokesperson for the Franklin County Sheriff's Office.</p><p>Michael McKee was arrested on Jan. 10 after allegedly shooting and killing Spencer Tepe, 37, and <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/ohio-police-hunt-killer-after-dentist-wife-found-shot-death-children-safe-inside-home-timeline" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><u>Monique Tepe</u></a>, 39, in the couple's home in Columbus' Weinland Park neighborhood between 2 a.m. and 5 a.m. on Dec. 30. McKee is Monique's ex-husband.</p><p>A sheriff's spokesman said Tuesday that McKee was taken to the Franklin County Jail and faces upgraded premeditated aggravated murder charges in relation to Spencer and Monique's deaths. McKee waived his extradition rights during an Illinois court hearing on Jan. 12, when he was being held at the Winnebago County Jail in Illinois.</p><p>McKee was booked into the James A. Karnes Corrections Center. McKee's next court date was not listed online.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/police-chief-touts-gun-link-ohio-dentist-murders-records-show-three-9mm-casings-found-killings"><strong>POLICE CHIEF TOUTS GUN LINK IN OHIO DENTIST MURDERS; RECORD SHOW THREE 9 MM CASINGS FOUND IN KILLINGS</strong></a></p><p>Charging documents indicate police were able to identify McKee by <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/critical-clue-led-police-suspect-chicago-doctor-deaths-ohio-dentist-wife" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><u>linking him to a car</u></a> that arrived in the Tepes' neighborhood shortly before the murders and left just after the homicides. The car was then located in Rockford, Illinois, and police found evidence it belonged to McKee.</p><p>McKee was indicted Friday on four counts of aggravated murder, each with firearm specifications, according to an indictment filed in Franklin County Common Pleas Court.</p><p>Three of the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/homicide" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><u>aggravated murder counts</u></a> include specifications alleging the use or display of an automatic firearm or silencer, while the fourth alleges a firearm was displayed or brandished. McKee is also charged with one count of aggravated burglary, a first-degree felony.</p><p>Columbus Police Chief Elaine Bryant revealed that investigators recovered firearms from McKee's home, one of which is believed to have been used in <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/critical-clue-led-police-suspect-chicago-doctor-deaths-ohio-dentist-wife" target="_blank" rel="noopener">the homicides.</a></p><p>"At this time, again, unfortunately, I’m unable to answer that question," Bryant told Fox News Digital when asked whether the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/police-and-law-enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><u>gun was legally obtained</u></a>. She said investigators were "still doing some investigation as it relates to this case."</p><p>Bryant added that multiple weapons were taken from McKee’s property and that there is a preliminary link through the National Integrated Ballistic Information Network (NIBIN) tying one of those firearms to the killings.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/chilling-surveillance-video-shows-person-interest-unsolved-ohio-dentist-murder-case" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>EERIE SURVEILLANCE VIDEO SHOWS 'PERSON OF INTEREST' IN UNSOLVED OHIO DENTIST MURDER CASE</strong></a></p><p>Police records reviewed by Fox News Digital show investigators recovered three spent 9mm shell casings from the scene.</p><p>The casings were logged into evidence and turned over to the Columbus police property room, according to a preliminary investigation report.
