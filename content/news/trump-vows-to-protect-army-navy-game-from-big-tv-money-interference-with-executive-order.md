<p>President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Donald Trump</a> said Saturday that he plans to sign an executive order protecting the broadcast slot for the Army-Navy football game.</p><p>Trump's announcement on Truth Social comes amid a push to expand the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/ncaa-fb" target="_blank" rel="noopener">NCAA College Football Playoff</a>, which could potentially affect the Army-Navy game’s traditional time slot.</p><p>"The <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/ncaa/army-black-knights" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Army-Navy Game</a> is one of our Greatest American Traditions — Unmatched Patriotism, Courage, and Honor!" Trump wrote. "This incredible Tradition is now at risk of being pushed aside by more College Playoff Games, and Big TV Money. NOT ANYMORE!"</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/sports/protests-erupt-outside-army-navy-game-amid-trumps-attendance" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>PROTESTS ERUPT OUTSIDE ARMY-NAVY GAME AMID TRUMP'S ATTENDANCE</strong></a></p><p>The president said his executive order will secure an exclusive four-hour broadcast window in December for the event that cannot be challenged by another postseason football game.</p><p>"Under my Administration, the second Saturday in December belongs to Army-Navy, and ONLY Army-Navy!" Trump said. "I will soon sign a Historic Executive Order securing an EXCLUSIVE 4 hour Broadcast window, so this National Event stands above Commercial Postseason Games. No other Game or Team can violate this Time Slot!!!"</p><p>Trump praised the rivalry, adding that "on the battlefield they are America’s unstoppable Patriots, defending our Country with tremendous Strength and Heart."</p><p>"We must protect the Tradition, and the Players, who protect us," he added. "Please let this serve as Notice to ALL Television Networks, Stations, and Outlets."</p><p>Trump attended the 126th installment of the rivalry game at M&amp;T Bank Stadium in Baltimore on Dec. 13, his seventh appearance at the game. The <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/sports/ncaa/navy-midshipmen" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Navy Midshipmen</a> captured the Commander-in-Chief's Trophy with a 17-16 victory over Army.</p><p>The president said he will also attend the College Football Playoff championship Monday in Miami, alongside Secretary of State <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/marco-rubio" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Marco Rubio</a>.</p>
