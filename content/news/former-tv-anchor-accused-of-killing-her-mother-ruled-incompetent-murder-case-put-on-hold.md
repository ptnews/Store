<p>The <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/homicide" target="_blank" rel="noopener">murder case</a> against a former television news anchor accused of killing her mother is now on hold after a judge ruled she is not mentally competent to stand trial.</p><p>On Thursday, Angelynn "Angie" Mock, 48, appeared in Sedgwick County District Court <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/us-regions/midwest/kansas" target="_blank" rel="noopener">in Kansas,</a> where Judge Jeffrey Goering ruled she is not mentally fit to proceed. </p><p>According to the Sedgwick County District Attorney’s Office, Mock has since been ordered to a state psychiatric hospital for further evaluation and treatment, and the criminal proceedings have been stayed while she undergoes treatment.</p><p>Mock is charged with <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/former-tv-anchor-charged-first-degree-murder-stabbing-death-80-year-old-mother-da" target="_blank" rel="noopener">first-degree murder in the stabbing</a> death of her mother, 80-year-old Anita Avers, on Halloween.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/former-tv-anchor-accused-stabbing-80-year-old-mother-death-telling-police-she-did-save-herself" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>FORMER TV ANCHOR ACCUSED OF STABBING 80-YEAR-OLD MOTHER TO DEATH, TOLD NEIGHBOR SHE DID IT TO 'SAVE HERSELF'</strong></a></p><p>Police say officers were called to the Wichita home Mock and Avers shared around 7:50 a.m. on Oct. 31. When officers arrived, they found Mock outside with cuts on her hands, according to the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/us/crime/police-and-law-enforcement" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Wichita Police Department</a>.</p><p>Avers was found inside the home with multiple stab wounds and was taken to a hospital, where she later died.</p><p>A probable cause affidavit states that Mock made statements while talking to herself indicating she <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/health/mental-health/psychosis" target="_blank" rel="noopener">believed her mother was the devil</a>. Dispatch records also show Mock allegedly told authorities she stabbed her mother "to save herself."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/ex-kentucky-sheriff-admits-shooting-judge-claims-he-had-no-control-over-actions-report" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>EX-KENTUCKY SHERIFF ADMITS TO SHOOTING JUDGE BUT CLAIMS HE 'HAD NO CONTROL' OVER ACTIONS: REPORT</strong></a></p><p><a href="https://www.kake.com/home/former-news-anchor-accused-of-killing-mother-incompetent-for-trial-judge-rules/article_c1fac030-838e-4d34-a3a6-29c7c0f1f2f8.html" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">According to court documents obtained by KAKE,</a> Mock’s stepfather told investigators she had previously been diagnosed with schizoaffective disorder and suffered from delusions and depression.</p><p>A neighbor also told the outlet that Mock approached her vehicle with blood on her hands and body, asking for help calling 911.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/us/slender-man-stabber-morgan-geyser-ordered-back-psychiatric-hospital-after-group-home-escape" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>SLENDER MAN STABBER MORGAN GEYSER ORDERED BACK TO PSYCHIATRIC HOSPITAL AFTER GROUP HOME ESCAPE</strong></a></p><p>Mock previously worked as a morning news anchor at KTVI in St. Louis from 2011 to 2015 and later at KOKH in Oklahoma City. She was arrested the same day, treated at a hospital for her injuries, and then booked into the Sedgwick County Jail.</p><p>At the time of her arrest, she was working in sales at a data management software company, according to her LinkedIn profile.</p><p>The District Attorney’s Office said a finding of incompetency does not dismiss the case or result in a defendant’s release, and that proceedings will resume if Mock is later deemed competent.</p><p><i>Fox News Digital’s Sarah Rumpf-Whitten contributed to this report.</i></p>
