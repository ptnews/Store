<p>Pro-regime demonstrators in Iran chanted "death to America" and held signs calling for the assassination of President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Donald Trump</a>, images from Iranian state television showed Thursday.</p><p>The footage came from an event held at Tehran University that was organized by the government. Several protesters held photos of Ayatollah <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/ali-khamenei" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Ali Khamenei</a>, while some brought makeshift signs featuring photos of Trump after the failed assassination attempt against him in Pennsylvania.</p><p>Multiple signs featured Arabic text translating to "This time there will be no error."</p><p>Another sign showed the same photo with a caption in English, "The arrow doesn't always miss."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/iran-regime-opened-fire-live-ammunition-protesters-doctor-says-shoot-to-kill" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>IRAN REGIME OPENED FIRE WITH LIVE AMMUNITION ON PROTESTERS, DOCTOR SAYS: ‘SHOOT-TO-KILL’</strong></a></p><p>The protest event comes as the Iranian regime contends with widespread unrest due to the country's economic crisis.</p><p>Humanitarian groups estimate that Iranian forces have killed over 2,000 protesters, though the true number could be far higher.</p><p>Iran appeared to <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/iran-appears-reopen-airspace-after-trump-says-killing-stopping" target="_blank" rel="noopener">reopen its airspace</a> Thursday after a sudden overnight closure disrupted flights across the region.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/iran-shuts-down-airspace-foreign-officials-warn-against-travel-israel" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>IRAN SHUTS DOWN AIRSPACE, FOREIGN OFFICIALS WARN AGAINST TRAVEL TO ISRAEL</strong></a></p><p>The overnight closure lasted around five hours after a Notice to Air Missions, or NOTAM, <a href="https://safeairspace.net/iran/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener"><u>was issued</u></a>, according to the aviation risk-monitoring site Safe Airspace. </p><p>President Donald Trump told reporters in the Oval Office Wednesday he was informed the killing of protesters in Iran was "stopping."</p><p>"We have been notified and really strongly, but we'll find out what that all means. But we've been told that the killing in Iran is stopping," he said.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/iran-allegedly-airs-97-coercive-confessions-amid-record-breaking-north-korea-style-internet-blackout" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>IRAN ALLEGEDLY AIRS 97 'COERCIVE CONFESSIONS' AMID RECORD-BREAKING NORTH KOREA-STYLE INTERNET BLACKOUT</strong></a></p><p>The U.S.-based Human Rights Activists News Agency (HRANA), <a href="https://www.en-hrana.org/day-eighteen-of-the-protests-global-focus-on-casualties-and-mass-arrests-amid-communications-blackout/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener"><u>which tracks</u></a> human rights violations in Iran, said Wednesday that nationwide protests continued into an 18th day as authorities maintained a near-total internet shutdown.</p><p>The group's aggregated figures showed 617 protest gatherings in 187 cities across the country, the arrest of at least 18,470 people and the confirmed deaths of 2,615 individuals.</p><p>HRANA said 2,435 of those killed were protesters, including 13 children under the age of 18.</p><p>Trump has <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-announces-final-25-tariff-countries-doing-business-iran-regime" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><u>threatened action against the regime</u></a>, warning Tehran in multiple Truth Social posts to stop killing its people.</p><p>"We are locked and loaded and ready to go," he wrote on New Year’s Day.</p><p><i>Fox News' Ashley Carnahan contributed to this report.</i></p>
