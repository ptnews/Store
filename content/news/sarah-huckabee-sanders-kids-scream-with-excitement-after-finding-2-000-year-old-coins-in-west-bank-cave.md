<p>The children of Arkansas Gov. Sarah Huckabee Sanders are now <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/science/archaeology" target="_blank" rel="noopener">archaeologists in training</a>.</p><p>Sanders, her husband and her children made a recent discovery during an archaeological tour of a previously unknown cave in the West Bank, Israeli news agency TPS-IL reported Tuesday.</p><p>The finds were first announced by the Yesha Council, an umbrella organization for Jewish communities in the West Bank.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/travel/3000-year-old-egyptian-fortress-uncovered-along-route-biblical-exodus" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>3,000-YEAR-OLD EGYPTIAN FORTRESS UNCOVERED ALONG ROUTE TIED TO BIBLICAL EXODUS</strong></a></p><p>The cave sits near the community of Na’ela in the Binyamin region. The children were accompanied by professional archaeologists and had metal detectors on hand.</p><p>The five coins — which may date to the Bar Kokhba Revolt period, between 132 and 135 A.D. — sent the children screaming.</p><p>"Grandpa, look what we found!" the grandchildren said, per TPS-IL.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/newsletters?cmpid=fnfirstnl" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>CLICK HERE TO SIGN UP FOR OUR LIFESTYLE NEWSLETTER</u></strong></a></p><p>The family also found fragments of a second-century jar in another cave.</p><p>In a statement, Ambassador Huckabee said he has "a special place in my heart for ancient Shiloh."</p><p>"It’s amazing to come to a place that proves that <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/faith-values" target="_blank" rel="noopener">miracles can happen,</a> 3,000 years ago and even today," he said.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/lifestyle" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>CLICK HERE FOR MORE LIFESTYLE STORIES</u></strong></a></p><p>In a statement to Fox News Digital, <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/person/h/sarah-huckabee-sanders" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Gov.
