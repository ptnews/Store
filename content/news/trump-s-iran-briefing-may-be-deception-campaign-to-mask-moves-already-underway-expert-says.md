<p>A national security expert said President <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Donald Trump</a> may already be prepared to act against Iran, suggesting a widely reported upcoming briefing on U.S. options could be intentional "deception" as deadly protests intensify in the country.</p><p>"President Trump definitely already has options in mind, and he has been regularly briefed by the military about the situation [<a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/conflicts/iran" target="_blank" rel="noopener">in Iran</a>]," Rebeccah Heinrichs, a senior fellow at the Hudson Institute, said Monday on "Fox &amp; Friends." </p><p>"This briefing that's supposed to happen tomorrow might, in fact, be part of a deception campaign. It would not surprise me if President Trump initiated some kind of U.S. activity to support the protesters before that briefing."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/irans-distinctive-drone-deployment-sees-death-toll-soar-amid-violent-protests" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>IRAN’S ‘DISTINCTIVE’ DRONE DEPLOYMENT SEES DEATH TOLL SOAR AMID VIOLENT PROTESTS</strong></a></p><p>Trump is set to speak with Secretary of State <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/marco-rubio" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Marco Rubio</a>, Secretary of War Pete Hegseth and Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff Dan Caine as human rights groups report the Iranian regime has already killed more than 500 protesters.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/iran-regime-said-unleash-hezbollah-iraqi-militias-uprising-spreads" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>IRAN REGIME SAID TO UNLEASH HEZBOLLAH AND IRAQI MILITIAS AS UPRISING SPREADS</strong></a></p><p>Widespread civil unrest in Iran began amid economic hardship as the theocracy's currency plunged to historic lows and has been fueled by dissatisfaction with political repression, corruption and clerical leadership. </p><p>The government's response has been severe, including internet blackouts, mass arrests and the use of deadly force.</p><p>Heinrichs laid out some options available to Trump, such as cyberattacks, <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/foreign-policy/middle-east" target="_blank" rel="noopener">military strikes</a> and deploying Starlink satellite internet to allow civilians to communicate.</p><p>Asked if Iran had <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-says-iran-starting-to-cross-us-red-lines-protesters-die-government-crackdown" target="_blank" rel="noopener">crossed Trump's "red line"</a> to trigger a response, the president replied, "They're starting to."</p><p>"We're looking at it very seriously. The military is looking at it, and we're looking at some very strong options," Trump said to reporters aboard Air Force One on Sunday.</p><p>"We’ll make a determination."</p>
