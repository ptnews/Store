<p>Yeison Jiménez, a 34-year-old <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/latino" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Colombian singer-songwriter</a>, died in a plane crash just hours before he was set to perform. </p><p>On Saturday, a plane crashed "in the sector between Paipa and Duitama," in Colombia, the Special Administrative Unit of Civil Aeronautics <a class="pseudoStyle" href="https://x.com/DIACC_COL/status/2010118441408311517?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw%7Ctwcamp%5Etweetembed%7Ctwterm%5E2010118441408311517%7Ctwgr%5E0018f810097cbe954fb04091c082dcdda570a1b4%7Ctwcon%5Es1_&amp;ref_url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.billboard.com%2Fmusic%2Flatin%2Fyeison-jimenez-death-plane-crash-1236152664%2F" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">announced on X</a>. Six people on the flight were killed, one of whom was Jiménez. </p><p>"With heavy hearts and a pain impossible to describe, the organization and team of Yeison Jiménez deeply regret to inform you of his passing," a statement translated from Spanish read from the musician's official <a href="https://www.instagram.com/p/DTWwx8kjHyC/?hl=en" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">Instagram account</a>. "Today we say goodbye not only to an artist; we say goodbye to a son, a brother, a friend, a human being full of dreams and courage, who transformed his story into hope for thousands of people. Yeison embodied perseverance, discipline, and love for his community. His voice and his example were born from hard work, and that is why they will forever mark the lives of those who followed and loved him."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/entertainment/brett-james-jesus-take-wheel-songwriter-dies-north-carolina-plane-crash" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>BRETT JAMES, ‘JESUS, TAKE THE WHEEL’ SONGWRITER, DIES IN NORTH CAROLINA PLANE CRASH</u></strong></a></p><p>According to the statement, Jiménez was traveling with five other members of his team including Jefferson Osorio, Juan Manuel Rodríguez, Óscar Marín and Weisman Mora, as well as Captain Fernando Torres.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/entertainment" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>LIKE WHAT YOU’RE READING? "We ask for your understanding and respect for the families’ grief and for the privacy we need today to say goodbye with the dignity and affection he deserves."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/newsletters" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong><u>CLICK HERE TO SIGN UP FOR THE ENTERTAINMENT NEWSLETTER</u></strong></a></p><p>"Yeison may be gone physically, but his legacy lives on: in his songs, in his words, in his struggles, in his refusal to give up, and in the indelible mark he leaves on Colombian regional music and in the hearts of his people," the statement concluded.
