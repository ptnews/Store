<p>The Trump administration has stepped up pressure on Sir Keir Starmer after <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/finance/sanctions" target="_blank" rel="noopener">imposing sanctions</a> on a Britain-based activist accused by U.S. intelligence of working on behalf of Hamas, according to reports.</p><p>The U.S. Treasury Department’s Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) announced Jan. 21 that it was "taking action against Hamas’s covert links to nonprofit organizations." </p><p>The statement went on to name six Gaza-based groups and the Popular Conference for Palestinians Abroad (PCPA).</p><p>U.S. officials said the PCPA operates as a front for Hamas overseas. OFAC also designated Zaher Birawi, 64, accusing him of being a "senior official" in Hamas through his leadership role in the PCPA.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/uk-prime-minister-slammed-welcoming-home-freed-egyptian-prisoner-after-social-media-posts-resurface" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>UK PRIME MINISTER SLAMMED FOR WELCOMING HOME FREED EGYPTIAN PRISONER AFTER SOCIAL MEDIA POSTS RESURFACE</strong></a></p><p>The Treasury Department said Birawi is a founding member of the organization and part of its general secretariat, alleging the group was used to "clandestinely expand" Hamas’ international influence and fundraising.</p><p>This included backing flotillas that attempted to reach Gaza. Some have received public backing from prominent activists, including Greta Thunberg.</p><p>"Hamas continues to show a callous disregard for the welfare of the Palestinian people," John K. Hurley, the U.S. Undersecretary for Terrorism and Financial Intelligence, said in a <a href="https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/sb0368" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">statement.</a></p><p>Hurley added that "the Trump administration will not look the other way while Hamas leadership and enablers exploit the financial system to fund terrorist operations."</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-admin-weighs-terrorism-sanctions-against-un-palestinian-aid-agency-over-hamas-allegations" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>TRUMP ADMIN WEIGHS TERRORISM SANCTIONS AGAINST UN PALESTINIAN AID AGENCY OVER HAMAS ALLEGATIONS</strong></a></p><p>The sanctions have put pressure on the U.K., which has been reviewing possible <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/world/uk-pm-starmer-praised-egyptian-extremist-faces-counter-terror-probe-over-resurfaced-tweets" target="_blank" rel="noopener">counterterror measures against Birawi</a> for weeks, according to <a href="https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2025/06/09/zaher-birawi-hamas-operative-greta-thunbergs-gaza-flotilla/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">The Times</a>.</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/world/uk-politics" target="_blank" rel="noopener">U.K. Shadow Home Secretary</a> Chris Philp said it was "good to see" the U.S. take action, adding, "Under no circumstances should Britain be a place of sanctuary for anyone involved in or supportive of terrorist (organizations) like Hamas."</p><p>Alex Hearn of <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Labour Against Antisemitism</a> said Birawi continued to broadcast from London and organize flotillas despite the U.S. designation.</p><p>Lord Austin of Dudley has urged the U.K. to examine the intelligence "very carefully."</p><p>Birawi, who lives in Barnet in north London, has been in the U.K. for more than 30 years.</p><p>He chairs the Palestine Forum in Britain and is a prominent organizer of pro-Palestinian demonstrations in the city.</p><p>He has been <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/state-and-local/federal-relations" target="_blank" rel="noopener">named in Parliament</a> as a security concern, and Israel designated him a Hamas member in 2013, allegations he has consistently denied.</p><p>In 2023, Birawi said he is a "<a href="https://www.thejc.com/news/uk/us-sanctions-hamas-linked-british-national-behind-gaza-flotillas-mzzgaikj" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">law-abiding British citizen</a>" and rejected claims of links to Hamas, saying his work focuses on "defending the national and human rights of Palestinians who survive under occupation."</p><p>Fox News Digital has reached out to Birawi and Sir Keir Starmer's office for comment.</p>
